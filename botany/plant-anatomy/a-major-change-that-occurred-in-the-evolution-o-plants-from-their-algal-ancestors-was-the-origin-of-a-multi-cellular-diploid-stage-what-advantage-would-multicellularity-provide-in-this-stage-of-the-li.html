[SOURCES]
A major change that occurred in the evolution o plants from their algal ancestors was the origin of a multi-cellular diploid stage. What advantage would multicellularity provide in this stage of the life cycle?

(A) enhanced potential for independence of the diploid stage from the haploid stage

(B) increased fertilization rate

(C) increased size of the diploid stage

(D) ** increased spore production from each fertilization event

(E) increased gamete production

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants are thought to have evolved from an aquatic green alga protist. Later, they evolved important adaptations for land, including vascular tissues, seeds, and flowers. Each of these major adaptations made plants better suited for life on dry land. The oldest fossils of land plants date back about 470 million years.

Concept note-2: -Origin of vascular plants required three important evolutionary advances: (1) sporophytes became able to grow independently of their parents after the embryonic stage; (2) sporophytes were able to branch; and (3) sporophytes acquired lignin-walled vascular tissues.

Concept note-3: -Today it is widely accepted that land plants (embryophytes) evolved from streptophyte algae, also referred to as charophycean algae. The streptophyte algae are a paraphyletic group of green algae, ranging from unicellular flagellates to morphologically complex forms such as the stoneworts (Charales).

Concept note-4: -The green algae are basically divided into Charophyte and Chlorophyte algae, and it is agreed that the Charophyte algae are the closest algal relatives of land plants. Analyses of both morphological and molecular data have established that land plants evolved within Charophyte algae more than 450 million years ago.
