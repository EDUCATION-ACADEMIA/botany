Which of these are functions of spongy mesophyll. Choose all the correct answers.

(A) ** Allows gases to circulate freely:

(B) ** Allows CO2 to pass to palisade cells for photosynthesis

(C) ** Allows O2 produced to pass out of leaf into air

(D) Covering layer of plant organs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The spongy mesophyllâ€™s function is to allow for the interchange of gases (CO 2) that are needed for photosynthesis. The spongy mesophyll cells are less likely to go through photosynthesis than those in the palisade mesophyll.

Concept note-2: -Spongy layer Although they contain a few chloroplasts, their main function seems to be the temporary storage of sugars and amino acids synthesized in the palisade layer. They also aid in the exchange of gases between the leaf and the environment.

Concept note-3: -Below the palisade parenchyma in dicots is the spongy parenchyma, which contains spaces to facilitate carbon dioxide diffusion and gas exchange via the stomata found on the lower surface of the leaf.

Concept note-4: -The mesophyll is found between the upper and lower epidermis; it aids in gas exchange and photosynthesis via chloroplasts. The xylem transports water and minerals to the leaves; the phloem transports the photosynthetic products to the other parts of the plant.
