Which of the following has a flattened thallus, dominant gametophyte, and motile sperm?

(A) Bryophyta

(B) Pterophyta

(C) Coniferophyta

(D) Antophyta

(E) ** Hepatophyta

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phylum Hepatophyta–liverworts Hepatophyta, along with Bryophyta and Anthocerotophyta are sometimes jointly called “bryophytes” since earlier classification systems placed them in a single phylum. All three of these phyla are said to have the gametophyte generation as the “dominant” generation.

Concept note-2: -THE GAMETOPHYTE OF THALLOID LIVERWORTS Liverworts have unicellular rhizoids that are located on the ventral (bottom) side of the gametophyte. Rhizoids look like roots, but do not absorb water or nutrients. Instead, they attach the plants to their substrate and help with external water retention and conduction.

Concept note-3: -In bryophytes, the gametophyte phase is dominant, whereas, in pteridophytes, gymnosperms, and angiosperms, the sporophyte phase is dominant.
