BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Plants use carbon dioxide, water, mineral salts and sunlight to make food and release oxygen

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create oxygen and energy in the form of sugar.

Concept note-2: -Plants use photosynthesis to capture carbon dioxide and then release half of it into the atmosphere through respiration. Plants also release oxygen into the atmosphere through photosynthesis.

Concept note-3: -Answer: Solution: False; green plants prepare their food by using raw materials, carbon dioxide, chlorophyll, water and light.

Concept note-4: -Their roots take up water and minerals from the ground and their leaves absorb a gas called carbon dioxide (CO2) from the air. They convert these ingredients into food by using energy from sunlight. This process is called photosynthesis, which means ‘making out of light’. The foods are called glucose and starch.

Concept note-5: -Plants take in – or ‘fix’ – carbon dioxide from the atmosphere during photosynthesis. Some of the carbon is used for plant growth, and some of it is used in respiration, where the plant breaks down sugars to get energy.
