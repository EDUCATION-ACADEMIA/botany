What does the seed coat do?

(A) uses sunlight to make food for the plant

(B) makes pollen

(C) ** protects the seed

(D) takes in nutrients from the soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The seed coatâ€™s function is simultaneously to protect the embryo and to transmit information regarding the external environment. An impenetrable seed coat may help to keep the embryo safe, but at the same time it would exclude the sensing of environmental cues.

Concept note-2: -A seed coat protects the internal parts of a seed. The seed coat has two layers. The outer layer is thick and known as the testa.

Concept note-3: -Seed coat is the outermost covering of a seed. It helps in the protection of the seed from the physical stress such as shock, temperature variations or water damage. It also helps the seed to remain dormant within the seed coat and germinates when there are favourable conditions.
