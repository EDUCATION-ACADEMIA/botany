The non-green parts of the plant ____

(A) get their nutrients from raw sap.

(B) ** get their nutrients from elaborated sap.

(C) need water to carry out photosynthesis.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: Non-green plants such as fungi and bacteria obtain their nutrients from decaying organic matter, from the environment they are living in. This organic matter comes from dead plants and animals. So, they are called saprophytes as their mode of nutrition is saprophytic.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: Other than vitamins green plants are rich in protein. This is due to the presence of protein in the cell wall and cell membrane of the non-green plants.

Concept note-3: -Roots are the non-green parts of a plant since it does not have chlorophyll which gives plants its characteristic green colour.

Concept note-4: -Non green leaves do not contain chlorophyll.

Concept note-5: -Complete answer: The non-green plant that we consume is the mushroom. The presence of chlorophyll pigment provides green color to the plant. Thus, the absence of chlorophyll pigments makes mushrooms non-green plants and hence they do not perform photosynthesis, instead gain energy from dead and decaying organic matter.
