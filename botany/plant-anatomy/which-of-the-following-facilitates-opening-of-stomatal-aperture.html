WHICH OF THE FOLLOWING FACILITATES OPENING OF STOMATAL APERTURE?

(A) DECREASE IS TURGIDITY OF GUARD CELL

(B) ** RADIAL ORIENTATION OF CELLULOSE CELL WALL OF GUARD CELL

(C) CONTRACTION OF OUTER WALL OF GUARD CELL

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: The opening of the stomatal aperture takes place by the radial orientation of cellulose microfibrils in the guard cellsâ€™ cell walls.

Concept note-2: -Cellulose microfibrils are oriented radially rather than longitudinally making it easier for the stomata to open.

Concept note-3: -Guard cells use osmotic pressure to open and close stomata, allowing plants to regulate the amount of water and solutes within them. In order for plants to produce energy and maintain cellular function, their cells undergo the highly intricate process of photosynthesis . Critical in this process is the stoma.

Concept note-4: -Two highly specialized cells, the guard cells that surround the stomatal pore, are able to integrate environmental and endogenous signals in order to control the stomatal aperture and thereby the gas exchange.

Concept note-5: -Stomatal pore is regulated by turgor of guard cells. Turgid guard cells pulls the inner walls of two guard cells apart and pore opens.
