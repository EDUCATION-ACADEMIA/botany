A frond is a leaf from a

(A) ** fern

(B) pine tree

(C) moss

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The leaves of ferns are often called fronds. Fronds are usually composed of a leafy blade and petiole (leaf stalk).

Concept note-2: -Fern leaves are often called fronds, the stalk of the leaf is called the stipe or petiole. Distal to this, the laterally expanded portion of the leaf is termed the blade or lamina, whose central midrib is referred to as the rachis.

Concept note-3: -Frond. The leaf of a fern. These are called fronds to distinguish them from the leaves of flowering plants. Leaves in flowering plants are purely concerned with photosynthesis whereas fern fronds have both a photosynthetic function and a reproductive function.

Concept note-4: -ˈfränd. : a large leaf (especially of a palm or fern) usually with many divisions. : a thallus or thalloid shoot (as of a lichen or seaweed) resembling a leaf. fronded.

Concept note-5: -The leaves of ferns are called fronds. Fronds also contain the sex cells called spores, which are held in reproductive centers called sori. Spores from the sori germinate into young fern plants called prothallus. Fronds are most often compound leaves, or simply leaves that divide into smaller segments called leaflets.
