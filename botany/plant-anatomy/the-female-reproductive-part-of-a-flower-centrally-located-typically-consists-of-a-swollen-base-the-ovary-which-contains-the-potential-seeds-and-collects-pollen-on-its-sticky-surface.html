The female reproductive part of a flower, centrally located, typically consists of a swollen base, the ovary, which contains the potential seeds and collects pollen on its sticky surface.

(A) ** pistil

(B) pickle

(C) petal

(D) prance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -pistil, the female reproductive part of a flower. The pistil, centrally located, typically consists of a swollen base, the ovary, which contains the potential seeds, or ovules; a stalk, or style, arising from the ovary; and a pollen-receptive tip, the stigma, variously shaped and often sticky.

Concept note-2: -The pistil is a plant’s female part. It generally is shaped like a bowling pin and is located in the flower’s center. It consists of a stigma, style and ovary. The stigma is located at the top and is connected by the style to the ovary.

Concept note-3: -ovary, in botany, enlarged basal portion of the pistil, the female organ of a flower. The ovary contains ovules, which develop into seeds upon fertilization.

Concept note-4: -The style is the green stalk, while the stigma is the swelling at the end closest to the outside. The stigma is the surface on which pollen must land (or be placed) during pollination.

Concept note-5: -The swollen part of the pistil is known as the ovary. Ovary contains the ovules which contain the female gametes or the egg cells.
