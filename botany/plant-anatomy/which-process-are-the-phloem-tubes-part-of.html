Which process are the phloem tubes part of?

(A) ** Translocation

(B) Transpiration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sugars produced in sources, such as leaves, need to be delivered to growing parts of the plant via the phloem in a process called translocation, or movement of sugar. The points of sugar delivery, such as roots, young shoots, and developing seeds, are called sinks.

Concept note-2: -Translocation is the movement of elements within a plant from leaves to other tissue. Plantsâ€™ phloem, a type of vascular tissue, is where translocation takes place. Sugars that are produced by photosynthesis or that are stored in plant tissues like roots, bulbs, and tubers are examples of sugar sources.

Concept note-3: -phloem, plant vascular tissue that conducts foods made in the leaves during photosynthesis to all other parts of the plant.

Concept note-4: -Answer. Translocation is the process by which assimilates from photosynthesis are moved around the plant, from where they are made to where they are needed. The place where these assimilates are produced is called the source, and the place they are moved to is called the sink.

Concept note-5: -Phloem is a vascular tissue that translocates food material from the leaves to different parts of the plant. The leaves are the site of food synthesis in plants.
