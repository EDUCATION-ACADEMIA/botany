What part of the flower turns into the fruit?

(A) Seeds

(B) ** Ovary

(C) Stamen

(D) Sepals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The fleshy seed coat is made by the ovary and nucellus tissues. The seed receives nutrients from its tough outer flesh layer. Fruit is the name for its fleshy exterior. Thus, the flower’s ovary develops into a fruit.

Concept note-2: -The female reproductive part of a flower that generally develops into fruit is called the ovary. Fruit tissues that surround seeds generated from the ovary are known as pericarps. The flower’s egg-bearing component that matures into a seed is called an ovule. As a result, Fruit Is Produced By The Plant’s Ovary.

Concept note-3: -Pistil: The part of the flower which contains the ovary is called Pistil. It comprises style, stigma, and ovary. The ovary in flowering plants is a part of the female reproductive structure of the gynoecium.

Concept note-4: -Carpel: ↑ Reproductive organ of a flower, which includes the ovary, stigma, and style. After fertilization, the carpel becomes the fruit.

Concept note-5: -A fruit is a mature, ripened ovary, along with the contents of the ovary. The ovary is the ovule-bearing reproductive structure in the plant flower. The ovary serves to enclose and protect the ovules, from the youngest stages of flower development until the ovules become fertilized and turn into seeds.
