The cotyledon is a seed structure that stores nutrients.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Either the nutrients surround the embryo in a tissue known as endosperm, as in a corn seed, or the nutrients are stored in leaf-like structures known as a cotyledons which are part of the embryo, as in bean seeds.

Concept note-2: -The outer covering of a seed is called seed coat. The seed coat protects the baby plant residing inside the seed. The swollen part of the seed called cotyledon stores food and all the other essential nutrients required for the baby plant.

Concept note-3: -The cotyledons contain (or in the case of gymnosperms and monocotyledons, have access to) the stored food reserves of the seed. As these reserves are used up, the cotyledons may turn green and begin photosynthesis, or may wither as the first true leaves take over food production for the seedling.

Concept note-4: -A cotyledon stores food for the seed which it uses during the process of germination. Cotyledons also form the first leaves in a baby plant.
