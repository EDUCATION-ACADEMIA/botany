hard, rigid stem like bark

(A) herbaceous stem

(B) ** woody stem

(C) fibrous root

(D) taproot

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In contrast, woody stems are hard and rigid. Maple trees, pine trees, and roses all have woody stems.

Concept note-2: -Plants which have hard woody stems and branches close to the ground are called shrubs.

Concept note-3: -The correct option is A trunk. The thickened stem of a tree that supports branches and has a woody outer bark is called the trunk of a tree.

Concept note-4: -Stem types and modifications Plants producing woody stems are called trees and shrubs; the latter produce branches from or near the ground, while the former have conspicuous trunks.
