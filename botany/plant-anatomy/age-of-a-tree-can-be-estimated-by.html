AGE OF A TREE CAN BE ESTIMATED BY

(A) ITS HEIGHT AND GIRTH

(B) BIOMASS

(C) ** NUMBER OF ANNUAL RINGS

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Age of a tree can be estimated by counting the number of annual rings in a cut stem. The two kinds of wood (spring and autumn wood) appear as alternate concentric rings of light and dark colour, respectively.

Concept note-2: -EXPLANATION: The most accurate way to determine the age of a tree is to count the annual rings of wood growth. Annual rings can be counted using two different methods. One can extract an increment core from the tree using an increment borer.

Concept note-3: -Every year, trees form new cells arranged in concentric circles. These concentric circles are called annular rings. By counting these rings, the age of a tree can be estimated.
