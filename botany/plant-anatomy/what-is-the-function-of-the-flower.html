What is the function of the flower?

(A) To absorb water and nutrients

(B) To take in sunlight and make food

(C) To hold the plant upright

(D) ** To attract pollinators and help the plant reproduce

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The most important function of flowers is reproduction. They help in the union of male and female gametes. Flowers provide nectar to certain birds and insects, which in turn help in the transfer of pollen from one flower to the other.

Concept note-2: -Answer : The function of a flower is to carry out sexual reproduction in plants through the formation of male and female gametes. Flowers ensure that the gametes fertilise to form seeds that further help in reproducing plants.

Concept note-3: -Flowers are designed to attract pollinators with their vibrant colors and alluring fragrance, and in return the pollinators feed on the flowers’ nectar and pollen. Pollinators are vital to the endurance of many species of plants and animals-and that doesn’t exclude people.

Concept note-4: -Plants produce nectar to attract pollinators. As the pollinator moves from flower to flower collecting nectar, they are also moving pollen from flower to flower. Certain fruits and seeds will not be produced if their flowers are not pollinated.

Concept note-5: -Functions of the flower: Give protection to reproductive organs like stamen and stigma. Attract pollinators with their bright colours. Help the plant to bear fruits and seeds after reproduction.
