What did Louis Pasteur and others do?

(A) They discovered cells

(B) ** They proved that protoplasm is alive when its chemical reaction are running properly not because of “vital force”

(C) They discovered animals do not have cell walls

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Louis Pasteur’s pasteurization experiment illustrates the fact that the spoilage of liquid was caused by particles in the air rather than the air itself. These experiments were important pieces of evidence supporting the idea of germ theory of disease.

Concept note-2: -In 1881, Pasteur performed a famous public experiment in which he injected one group of animals with an anthrax vaccine he had developed, and he did not vaccinate his second, control group. After a few weeks, both groups were injected with live anthrax bacteria, and all the vaccinated animals survived.

Concept note-3: -In 1857 Pasteur’s research showed that the souring of sugar beet in fermenting industrial alcohol was due to germs. He went on to prove that there were germs in the air. He proved sterilised water in a closed flask stayed sterile, whilst sterilised water in an open flask bred germs.

Concept note-4: -Pasteur suspected that this was not the case. He disproved spontaneous generation by boiling beef broth in a special flask that deters contamination. When the broth was not exposed to air, it remained sterile and free of microorganisms.
