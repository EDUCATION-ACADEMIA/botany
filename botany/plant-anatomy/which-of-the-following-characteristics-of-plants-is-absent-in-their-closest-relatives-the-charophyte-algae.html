Which of the following characteristics of plants is absent in their closest relatives, the charophyte algae?

(A) chlorophyll b

(B) cellulose in cell walls

(C) ** alternation of generations

(D) sexual reproduction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term alteration of generation indicates alteration of the life cycle (sexual and asexual phase) of particular organisms such as plants species. It takes place in plants but not in charophytes. Thus, alteration of multicellular generation does not take place in charophytes. Therefore, the given statement is true.

Concept note-2: -In some charophyte groups, such as the Zygnematophyceae or conjugating green algae, flagella are absent and sexual reproduction does not involve free-swimming flagellate sperm.

Concept note-3: -Charophytes have similar sperm structures. Green algae and brown algae have flagellated sperms. Only members of red algae do not have flagellated sperms. Thus, the similar sperm structure does not show evidence that charophytes are the closest algal relatives of plants.

Concept note-4: -Charophytes form sporopollenin and precursors of lignin, phragmoplasts, and have flagellated sperm. They do not exhibit alternation of generations.

Concept note-5: -The land plants are most closely related to the green algae, which together form a clade called Viridiplantae ("green plants").
