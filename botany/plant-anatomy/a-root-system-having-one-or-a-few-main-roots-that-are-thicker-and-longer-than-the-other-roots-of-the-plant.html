PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
A root system having one or a few main roots that are thicker and longer than the other roots of the plant.

(A) ** taproot system

(B) root hair system

(C) fibrous root system

(D) rootbeer system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Taproot systems feature a single, thick primary root, called the taproot, with smaller secondary roots growing out from the sides. The taproot may penetrate as many as 60 meters (almost 200 feet) below the ground surface.

Concept note-2: -Taproots have a thick main root which is also called primary root. It has smaller roots arising from the main root. For example, carrots have tap roots.

Concept note-3: -There are two main kinds of roots: taproots and fibrous roots. A taproot is a long, thick root that points straight down. Smaller roots can grow out of its sides. Plants with taproots include carrots and dandelions.

Concept note-4: -Thick main root is not found in fibrous root system. In this type of root system, roots generally arise from the stem base. Some examples of plants that have fibrous root system are barley and maize.

Concept note-5: -A tap root system has a main root that grows down vertically, and from which many smaller lateral roots arise. Dandelions are a good example; their tap roots usually break off when trying to pull these weeds, and they can regrow another shoot from the remaining root. A tap root system penetrates deep into the soil.
