A young plant inside a seed

(A) cone

(B) conifer

(C) ** embryo

(D) seedling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A seedling is a young plant or sporophyte developing out of a plant embryo from a seed. Seedling development starts with germination of the seed. A typical young seedling consists of three main parts: the radicle (embryonic root), the hypocotyl (embryonic shoot), and the cotyledons (seed leaves).

Concept note-2: -cotyledon, seed leaf within the embryo of a seed. Cotyledons help supply the nutrition a plant embryo needs to germinate and become established as a photosynthetic organism and may themselves be a source of nutritional reserves or may aid the embryo in metabolizing nutrition stored elsewhere in the seed.

Concept note-3: -The germ is the plant embryo which would form the new plant if germinated and is characterized by a high lipid and protein content.

Concept note-4: -Plant growth and buds In both gymnosperms and angiosperms, the young plant contained in the seed, begins as a developing egg-cell formed after fertilization (sometimes without fertilization in a process called apomixis) and becomes a plant embryo.
