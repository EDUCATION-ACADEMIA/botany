The sporophyte is

(A) haploid

(B) ** diploid

(C) triploid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Diploid sporophyte cells undergo meiosis to produce haploid spores. Each spore goes through mitotic divisions to yield a multicellular, haploid gametophyte. Mitotic divisions within the gametophyte are required to produce the gametes. The diploid sporophyte results from the fusion of two gametes.

Concept note-2: -In terms of chromosomes, the gametophyte is haploid (has a single set of chromosomes), and the sporophyte is diploid (has a double set).

Concept note-3: -Diploid Sporophyte Cells A sporophyte is a structure that has been formed from a diploid structure called a zygote. This zygote is resulted due to the fusion of two haploid gametes: male and female gametes. The formed zygote is later developed into a sporophyte, a diploid multicellular structure.

Concept note-4: -Gametes are always haploid, and spores are usually haploid (spores are always haploid in the plant alternations of generations life cycle).

Concept note-5: -spo·ro·phyte ˈspȯr-ə-ˌfīt. : the diploid multicellular individual or generation of a plant with alternation of generations that begins from a diploid zygote and produces haploid spores by meiotic division compare gametophyte.
