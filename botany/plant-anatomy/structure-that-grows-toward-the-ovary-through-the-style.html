BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
structure that grows toward the ovary through the style

(A) stigma

(B) embryo sac

(C) bud

(D) ** pollen tube

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pollen germination and pollen tube growth take place within the carpel. Pollen grains first land on the stigma where they germinate and pollen tube growth takes place along the style, until reaching the ovarian cavity, the locule, at the base of the style.

Concept note-2: -The pollen tube invades the pistil, growing between the walls of the stigmatic cells, then traveling through an extracellular matrix within the transmitting tissue of the style. The pollen tube finally arrives at the ovary, where it is attracted to an ovule that contains an egg cell.

Concept note-3: -A pollen grain on the stigma grows a tiny tube, all the way down the style to the ovary. This pollen tube carries a male gamete to meet a female gamete in an ovule.

Concept note-4: -When a pollen grain comes in contact with the stigma, the ovary produces a positive chemical response for the pollen grain. This helps the pollen tube to grow towards the ovary for the process of fertilization.

Concept note-5: -The obturator is the sporophytic structure which pollen tubes traverse prior to ovule penetration, but it has received little attention (Arbeloa and Herrero, 1987; Herrero and Arbeloa, 1989; Sornsathapornkul and Owens, 1999; Herrero, 2001, 2003; Nores et al., 2015).
