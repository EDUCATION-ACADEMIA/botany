PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
The food that a plant makes is ____

(A) ** sugar or glucose

(B) water

(C) oxygen

(D) carbon dioxide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants use glucose to live and grow. For both plants and humans, glucose is like the fuel for a machine. Plants produce sugar in their leaves, but these leaves do not taste sweet. That is because the sugar does not remain in the leaves.

Concept note-2: -Plants use the energy of the sun to change water and carbon dioxide into a sugar called glucose. Glucose is used by plants for energy and to make other substances like cellulose and starch.

Concept note-3: -Leaves perform photosynthesis using the solar energy to produce food in the form of glucose using carbon dioxide and water. Therefore, the simple food that is prepared by the leaves is called glucose.

Concept note-4: -Green plants manufacture glucose through a process that requires light, known as photosynthesis. This process takes place in the leaf chloroplasts. Carbon dioxide and water molecules enter a sequence of chemical reactions within the chloroplasts.

Concept note-5: -Sucrose is the chemical name for sugar and is a naturally occurring carbohydrate found in fruits and vegetables. Plants produce sucrose through photosynthesis. Photosynthesis is the process where plants make their own food using carbon dioxide, water and sunlight.
