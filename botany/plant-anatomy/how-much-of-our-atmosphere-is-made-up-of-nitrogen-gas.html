How much of our atmosphere is made up of nitrogen gas?

(A) 58%

(B) 28%

(C) ** 78%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The percentage of nitrogen in the air is 78%. Oxygen occupies 21% and argon occupies 0.9%. Gases like carbon dioxide, nitrous oxides, methane, and ozone are trace gases that account for about a tenth of one percent of the atmosphere.

Concept note-2: -Air is mostly gas The air in Earthâ€™s atmosphere is made up of approximately 78 percent nitrogen and 21 percent oxygen. Air also has small amounts of other gases, too, such as carbon dioxide, neon, and hydrogen.

Concept note-3: -Atmosphere is a layer of gasses that surrounds the earth. It is made up of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 0.9% argon, and 0.03% carbon dioxide and a very small percentage of with water vapor.

Concept note-4: -Gases. The most abundant naturally occurring gas is nitrogen (N2), which makes up about 78% of air. Oxygen (O2) is the second most abundant gas at about 21%.

Concept note-5: -However, breathing in air with such a high percentage of nitrogen is not harmful or toxic to our body, as nitrogen is an inert gas, which means it is unreactive or it does not chemically react with other gases to produce any toxic gaseous compound.
