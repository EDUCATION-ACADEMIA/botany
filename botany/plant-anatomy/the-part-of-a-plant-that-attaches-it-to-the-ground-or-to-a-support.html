The part of a plant that attaches it to the ground or to a support?

(A) stem

(B) leaf

(C) ** root

(D) flower

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -root1 / roōt; roŏt/ • n. 1. the part of a plant that attaches it to the ground or to a support, typically underground, conveying water and nourishment to the rest of the plant via numerous branches and fibers: cacti have deep and spreading roots a tree root.

Concept note-2: -The roots absorb water and minerals from the soil and anchor the plant in the ground. The stem supports the plant above ground, and carries the water and minerals to the leaves.

Concept note-3: -The stem is the sturdy stalk that grows from the roots and sprouts leaves and flowers. The stem provides structure to the plant and carries all of the needed nutrients to the plant through stem structures called the xylem and phloem. The leaves are attached to the stem by the leafstalk, called the petiole.

Concept note-4: -The stem is the part of the plant that provides mechanical support to the whole plant body. All the parts of the plants, except roots, depend on the stem for support. These include leaves, fruits, flowers, etc. Additional role of the stem is the translocation of food and conduction of water to all parts of the plant.

Concept note-5: -The stem is the part that stays above the ground. A stem usually bears leaves, fruits plus flowers. It distributes the nutrients and minerals all the way from the plant to the leaves. Furthermore, the stem is the support system of the plant.
