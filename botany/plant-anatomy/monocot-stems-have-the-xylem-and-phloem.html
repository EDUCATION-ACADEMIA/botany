Monocot stems have the xylem and phloem

(A) on each side of a cambium layer.

(B) ** in small bundles scattered through the stem.

(C) side by side.

(D) near the outside of the stem.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vascular tissue is arranged into bundles of xylem and phloem that are scattered throughout the ground tissue. Within each bundle, the phloem is located closer to the outside of the stem, and the xylem is located closer to the inside of the stem.

Concept note-2: -The monocot stem is composed of an epidermis, xylem and phloem (vascular bundles). The vascular bundles in a monocot stem are scattered throughout the origin of the stem underneath the epidermis. The scattered arrangement of the vascular bundles is a key characteristic that separates herbaceous dicots from monocots.

Concept note-3: -Monocot plants contain scattered vascular bundles.

Concept note-4: -The monocot stem sample has scattered vascular bundles, while the dicot stem sample has radially present but fewer vascular bundles. The varieties of stem cuticles, as well as a single layer of epidermis, are present in both.

Concept note-5: -Monocot stems have bundles of vascular tissue scattered throughout, whereas vascular bundles in dicot stems are arranged in a ring.
