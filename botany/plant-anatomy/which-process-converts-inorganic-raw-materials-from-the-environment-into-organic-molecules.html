Which process converts inorganic raw materials from the environment into organic molecules?

(A) Aerobic Respiration

(B) Anaerobic Respiration

(C) ** Photosynthesis

(D) Homeostasis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carbon from carbon dioxide-inorganic carbon-can be incorporated into organic molecules; this process is called carbon fixation, and the carbon in organic molecules is also known as fixed carbon.

Concept note-2: -photosynthesis, the process by which green plants and certain other organisms transform light energy into chemical energy. During photosynthesis in green plants, light energy is captured and used to convert water, carbon dioxide, and minerals into oxygen and energy-rich organic compounds.

Concept note-3: -Photosynthesis can be defined as the synthesis of organic compounds (primarily sugars) from carbon dioxide (CO2) and water using light energy.

Concept note-4: -Photosynthesis in plants makes useful organic compounds out of carbon dioxide through carbon-fixation reactions. The process of photosynthesis in plants involves a series of steps and reactions that use solar energy, water, and carbon dioxide to produce oxygen and organic compounds.
