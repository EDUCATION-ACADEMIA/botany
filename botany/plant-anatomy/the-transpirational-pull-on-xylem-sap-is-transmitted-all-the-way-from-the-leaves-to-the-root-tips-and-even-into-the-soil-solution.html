The Transpirational Pull on Xylem sap is transmitted all the way from the leaves to the root tips and even into the soil solution.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Root pressure is that force that helps to drive fluids upward into the water-conducting vessels (xylem) due to osmotic pressure in the root cells. Transpiration pull is said to be a suction force used to draw the water in an upward direction from the roots towards the leaves.

Concept note-2: -Xylem transports water, minerals, and nutrients from the soil to all the plant parts. There are two types of “transport” tissues in plants-xylem and phloem. Water and solutes are transported by the xylem from the roots to the leaves, and food is transported from the leaves to the rest of the plant by the phloem.

Concept note-3: -A transpiration pull could be simply defined as a biological process in which the force of pulling is produced inside the xylem tissue. This force helps in the upward movement of water into the xylem vessels. In this process, loss of water in the form of vapours through leaves are observed.

Concept note-4: -Xylem is one of the two types of transport tissue in vascular plants, the other being phloem. The basic function of xylem is to transport water from roots to stems and leaves, but it also transports nutrients.
