[SOURCES]
Stems grow ____

(A) ** up towards sunlight

(B) down into the soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the stem grows towards the sunlight, it is called as positively phototropic movement. It is a tropic movement induced in the plant, in response to the stimuli called as light. Whereas, the growth of root is negatively phototropic.

Concept note-2: -As we know from looking at plants on a windowsill, they grow toward the sunlight to be able to generate energy by photosynthesis. Now an international team of scientists has provided definitive insights into the driving force behind this movement-the plant hormone auxin.

Concept note-3: -"Even mature plants bend toward the strongest light. They do this by elongating the cells of the stem on the side that is farthest from the light. This type of light-oriented growth is called phototropism, ‚Äù explains Prof.

Concept note-4: -One of the key parameters that every plant must respond to is the direction of gravity: stems go up (opposite to the pull of gravity) and roots go down (in the same direction as the force of gravity).

Concept note-5: -Stem grows towards the sunlight. They absorb the light from sunlight for preparing their own food through photosynthesis.
