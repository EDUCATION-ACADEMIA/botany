A plant that looses its leaves during the dormant season is

(A) ** Deciduous

(B) Evergreen

(C) Dormant

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deciduous trees are trees that drop their leaves for part of the year. They are found in temperate and tropical climates all over the world.

Concept note-2: -At the end of fall, most deciduous trees lose their leaves for the winter season. In fact, the word deciduous comes from the Latin word decidere, which means to fall down or off. There are, however, a handful of deciduous trees around these parts that have a tendency to keep their leaves past fall.

Concept note-3: -In botany and horticulture, deciduous plants, including trees, shrubs and herbaceous perennials, are those that lose all of their leaves for part of the year. This process is called abscission. In some cases leaf loss coincides with winter-namely in temperate or polar climates.

Concept note-4: -biology : falling off or shed seasonally or at a certain stage of development in the life cycle.

Concept note-5: -Tropical deciduous trees shed their leaves in the dry season.
