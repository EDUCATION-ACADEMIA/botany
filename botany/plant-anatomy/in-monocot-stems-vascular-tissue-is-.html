In Monocot stems, vascular tissue is ____

(A) ** Scattered throughout the stem

(B) Formed into a ring around the stem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vascular tissue is arranged into bundles of xylem and phloem that are scattered throughout the ground tissue. Within each bundle, the phloem is located closer to the outside of the stem, and the xylem is located closer to the inside of the stem.

Concept note-2: -Monocot plants contain scattered vascular bundles.

Concept note-3: -Monocot Stems In monocots, xylem and phloem are organized in vascular bundles scattered throughout the stem. As the plant grows, monocot stems generate new vascular bundles for the new tissue.

Concept note-4: -In monocot roots, the vascular bundles are arranged in a circular pattern. Monocots and dicots contain two main types of vascular tissue: the xylem and phloem. The xylem carries water and dissolved minerals upward from the roots to the stem and leaves.

Concept note-5: -The vascular bundle encloses both xylem and phloem. The xylem is present in the inner surface and phloem in the outer surface and cambium is not present in monocot plants. Hence, the vascular bundles in monocot stem are endarch, closed, and collateral.
