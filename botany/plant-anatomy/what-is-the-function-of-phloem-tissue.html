What is the function of phloem tissue?

(A) Transport water and minerals

(B) Support plants

(C) Photosynthesis process occur

(D) ** Transports glucose and other nutrients

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phloem transport food and nutrients such as sugar and amino acids from leaves to storage organs and growing parts of the plant. Xylem transport water and minerals from roots to aerial parts of the plant.

Concept note-2: -Phloem is one of the two tissues that make up the plant’s long-distance transport system, the other being xylem. Phloem translocates water and products of photosynthesis from source tissues to the sink regions where they are utilized or stored.

Concept note-3: -Phloem tissue transports and distributes the sucrose and nutrients produced by plants during photosynthesis. Phloem is in charge of transporting food and nutrients from the leaves to all the plant’s parts.

Concept note-4: -Phloem, the vascular tissue responsible for transporting organic nutrients around the plant body, carries dissolved sugars from the leaves (their site of production) or storage sites to other parts of the plant that require nutrients.

Concept note-5: -Phloem transports photosynthetically prepared food materials from the leaves to the storage organs and later from storage organs to the growing regions of the plant body.
