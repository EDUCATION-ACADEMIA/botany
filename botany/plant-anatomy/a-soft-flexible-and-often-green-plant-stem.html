A soft, flexible, and often green plant stem.

(A) ** herbaceous stem

(B) woody stem

(C) cork

(D) bark

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Herbaceous plants do not have a woody stem above the ground, instead they have a soft, green flexible stem. They are mostly biennials and annuals, along with a few perennials. In unfavourable seasons, the top growth dies down to the ground.

Concept note-2: -The stems of herbaceous plants are soft and flexible and are found typically on annuals, such as most vegetables, plants that grow for only one season.

Concept note-3: -An example of a herbaceous stem would be a peony, which is a perennial. The tree-like banana plant, also a perennial, is known as the largest herbaceous plant. Biennials, likewise, lack woody stems, therefore they can also be characterized as herbaceous.

Concept note-4: -A small plant with a soft stem is called a herb.

Concept note-5: -Plants with green and tender stems are called as herbs. Its examples are tomatoes, wheat, etc. Trees have their branches in the upper part of the stem, much above the ground.
