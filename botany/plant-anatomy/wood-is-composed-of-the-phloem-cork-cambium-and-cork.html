Wood is composed of the phloem, cork cambium, and cork.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Wood is a product of secondary growth in a plant. This secondary growth arises from the vascular cambium, which creates secondary vascular bundles (i.e. secondary xylem and phloem) after initial cell elongation and growth of a plant has ceased. Wood is mainly composed of the secondary phloem.

Concept note-2: -The vascular cambium is responsible for increasing the diameter of stems and roots and for forming woody tissue. The cork cambium produces some of the bark. In dicot stems, the vascular cambium initially differentiates from procambial cells within the vascular bundles (Fig.

Concept note-3: -Wood is constituted by secondary xylem tissue, comprising parenchyma cells and conduits (vessels or tracheids).

Concept note-4: -Wood (secondary xylem) is derived from vascular cambium, and its formation encompasses a series of developmental processes.
