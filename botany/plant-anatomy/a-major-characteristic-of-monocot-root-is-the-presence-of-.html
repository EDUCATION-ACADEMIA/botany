A MAJOR CHARACTERISTIC OF MONOCOT ROOT IS THE PRESENCE OF ____

(A) SCATTERED VASCULAR BUNDLES

(B) ** VASCULATURE WITHOUT CAMBIUM

(C) OPEN VASCULA BUNDLE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the monocotyledons, the vascular bundles have no cambium present in them. Hence, since they do not form secondary tissues they are referred to as closed.

Concept note-2: -One of the most prominent characteristics of monocot root is the absence of cambium. This limits the rootsâ€™ ability to grow sufficiently. The monocot root consists of a distinct pith around which the vascular bundles arrange themselves alternately and radially forming a ring.

Concept note-3: -Monocots do not have a vascular cambium, even though some of them, such as palms and the Joshua tree, exhibit secondary growth. Instead, they have a thickening meristem that produces secondary ground tissue.

Concept note-4: -Assertion :The vascular cambium is absent in monocots. Reason: The monocots never show secondary growth.

Concept note-5: -In both monocotyledonous and dicotyledonous plants, the stem is mainly composed of basal tissue containing loosely arranged scattered cells. Monocotyledonous plants do not have cambium.
