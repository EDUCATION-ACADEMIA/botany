After photosynthesis, plants store their glucose as ____ in plants .

(A) ** starch

(B) pigment

(C) sugar

(D) Oxygen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glucose produced by photosynthesis is easily soluble in water and it canâ€™t be stored in plant. Plants store glucose as starch in leaves which is insoluble and is utilized as a source of energy for life activities growth.

Concept note-2: -Producing Carbohydrates (Photosynthesis) In plants, glucose is stored in the form of starch, which can be broken down back into glucose via cellular respiration in order to supply ATP.

Concept note-3: -Starch is the storage form of glucose in plants, stored in seeds, roots, and tubers for later use as an energy source for the plant to reproduce.

Concept note-4: -Plants synthesise glucose during the process of photosynthesis. The excess of glucose is stored in the form of starch. It is stored in roots, leaves, tubers, bulbs. Cellulose is present in the cell wall of the plants.

Concept note-5: -Starch is stored in the stroma of the chloroplasts and in the cytoplasm of leaves.
