Which of the following provides food for the embryo?

(A) Fruit

(B) ** Endosperm

(C) Cotyledon

(D) Vascular cambium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nucellus : The nucellus lies at the center of the ovule and contains the embryo sac. The embryo within the embryosac gets nutrition from the nucellus. The nucellus is known to act as a food reserve for the growing embryo.

Concept note-2: -Cotyledons supply food to the developing embryo until the plumule develops green leaves.

Concept note-3: -Endosperm supports embryogenesis not only by providing nutrients and growth regulators but also by regulating the embryo growth and maturation process through mobile protein signals.

Concept note-4: -Endosperm is the most important plant product on Earth for humans. Two thirds of all human calories come from endosperm. In one form or another cereal grains, the one-seeded fruits of grasses, which include rice, wheat, maize, rye, oats, and barley, constitute the majority of human food.

Concept note-5: -Cotyledon is the part of a seed that stores food for the embryo.
