Which tissue provides support and mechanical strength to the plant?

(A) parenchyma tissue

(B) ** sclerenchyma tissue

(C) xylem tissue

(D) collenchyma tisssue

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Collenchyma. Collenchyma cells have thick walls that are especially thick at their corners. These cells provide mechanical support for the plant.

Concept note-2: -Sclerenchyma provides mechanical strength. Q. Which tissue provides mechanical strength to plant?

Concept note-3: -Sclerenchyma is an example of a simple permanent but dead mechanical tissue. It does not have a living protoplast. It has a cell wall that is made up of cellulose and lignin. It provides mechanical support to the plant.

Concept note-4: -Sclerenchyma cells are specialized plant cells that exist to provide strength and support and are found at the stem, roots and even seed coats of some plants.

Concept note-5: -Sclerenchyma is a dead mechanical tissue.
