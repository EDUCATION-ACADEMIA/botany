PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
When the seed gets the proper amount of sunlight, air, and water the seed is going through ____

(A) pollen

(B) watering

(C) ** germination

(D) metamorphosis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This stage is referred to as Imbibition.

Concept note-2: -Small seeds generally germinate better in light than in darkness, while large seeds do not exhibit a difference in germination between these two conditions (Milberg et al. 2000. 2000. Large seeded species are less dependent on light for germination than small-seeded ones.

Concept note-3: -germination, the sprouting of a seed, spore, or other reproductive body, usually after a period of dormancy. The absorption of water, the passage of time, chilling, warming, oxygen availability, and light exposure may all operate in initiating the process.

Concept note-4: -Plants harvest energy from the sun with the help of chlorophyll and carotenoid, two photon-capturing molecules. But if the plants are exposed to too much sun, these molecules absorb more energy than they can handle and generate reactive species of oxygen that can destroy the plant.

Concept note-5: -All seeds need water (moisture), oxygen and proper temperature in order to germinate into new plants. The nutrients required for the germination is already stored inside the cotyledons. So, nutrients need not be present outside the seed. Carbon dioxide is also not required for seed germination.
