BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
plants does not need carbon dioxide and water

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Solution: False; green plants prepare their food by using raw materials, carbon dioxide, chlorophyll, water and light.

Concept note-2: -Plants need three main ingredients to make their food: water, carbon dioxide and sunlight. Plants take up the water that they need from the soil through their roots. Carbon dioxide is a gas found in the air; plants can take in this gas through tiny holes in their leaves.

Concept note-3: -A new study involving ANU and international collaborators has found plants release more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere through respiration than expected. Plants use photosynthesis to capture carbon dioxide and then release half of it into the atmosphere through respiration.

Concept note-4: -Hint: Plants perform photosynthesis in leaves to make their own food. They require carbon dioxide, water and sunlight to perform this process. It occurs in the chloroplast.

Concept note-5: -The plants in the bottle exposed to light are able to photosynthesize and in this process they take up CO2 from the water, resulting to an increased pH (green colour). In the absence of light, the plants in the covered bottle cannot photosynthesize, so they do not take up CO2, resulting in the water remaining acidic.
