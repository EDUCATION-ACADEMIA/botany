Which type of meristem is correctly paired with the type of growth it does?

(A) apical-makes plant wider/thicker

(B) lateral-makes leaves longer

(C) ** axillary-makes new leaves and branches

(D) cambium/intercalary-makes plant taller

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An axillary bud, the precursor of a branch or lateral shoot, is formed at the junction between a leaf and the stem. The shoot apex tends to inhibit outgrowth of axillary buds, a phenomenon called apical dominance. Removal of the shoot tip leads to growth of these buds to produce lateral shoots.

Concept note-2: -Accessory meristems develop between the axillary bud and the cotyledon (Fig.

Concept note-3: -During the formation of leaves and elongation of stems, some cells are left behind from the shoot apical meristem. These cells constitute axillary bud and are present in the axils of leaves and are capable of forming a branch or a flower. Axillary buds can modify into stem tendrils and thorns.

Concept note-4: -The Shoot Apical Meristem (SAM) gives rise to organs like the leaves and flowers, while the Root Apical Meristem (RAM) provides the meristematic cells for the future root growth.
