PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
The type of arrangement in which protoxylem lies towards the outside and metaxylem lies towards the inside is called:

(A) ** Exarch

(B) Endarch

(C) Mesarch

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: –In exarch condition, the protoxylem (the first-formed xylem) lies towards the periphery and the metaxylem (the later-formed xylem) lies towards the center (pith). Hence, the youngest lies closest to the center in this type of arrangement. So, the answer is, “Exarch.”

Concept note-2: -There are three types of arrangement of primary xylem, namely endarch, exarch and mesarch. In endarch the protoxylem lies towards the centre and metaxylem towards the periphery, in exarch protoxylem lies towards the periphery and metaxylem towards the centre and in mesarch protoxylem is surrounded by metaxylem.

Concept note-3: -The first formed primary xylem is called protoxylem and the later formed primary xylem is called metaxylem. In stems, the protoxylem lies towards the centre (pith) and the metaxylem lies towards the peripheryof the organ. This type of primary xylem is called endarch.

Concept note-4: -Endarch: In vascular bundle, endarch is the arrangement where protoxylem (older xylem) is inside and metaxylem (newly formed) is outside. Exarch: When protoxylem is outside and metaxylem is inside, the arrangement is known as exarch.

Concept note-5: -So, the correct answer is ‘Mesarch condition. ‘
