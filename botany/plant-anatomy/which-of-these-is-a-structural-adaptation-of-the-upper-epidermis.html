Which of these is a structural adaptation of the upper epidermis?

(A) Long, vertically arranged cells

(B) ** Transparent, no chloroplasts

(C) Tightly packed

(D) Rounded shape

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cuticle is a waxy, waterproof layer which cuts down the water lost by evaporation and protects against parasitic fungi. A single layer of cells that are transparent and contain no chloroplast allowing light to pass straight through.

Concept note-2: -Upper Epidermis: Upper epidermis contains less number of chloroplasts since it contains less number of guard cells. Lower Epidermis: Lower epidermis contains more chloroplasts than the upper epidermis.

Concept note-3: -Upper Epidermis: A protective layer of cells that produces the cuticle. The epidermis is also transparent and very thin to allow maximum light penetration (let the light come through).

Concept note-4: -Upper epidermis This is a single layer of cells containing few or no chloroplasts. The cells are quite transparent and permit most of the light that strikes them to pass through to the underlying cells. The upper surface is covered with a waxy, waterproof cuticle, which serves to reduce water loss from the leaf.

Concept note-5: -The clear epidermal cells exist in a single layer and do not contain chloroplasts, because the onion fruiting body (bulb) is used for storing energy, not photosynthesis. Each plant cell has a cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus, and a large vacuole. The nucleus is present at the periphery of the cytoplasm.
