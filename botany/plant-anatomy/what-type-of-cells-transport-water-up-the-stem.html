What type of cells transport water UP the stem?

(A) ** Xylem cells

(B) Phloem Cells

(C) Epidermal Cells

(D) Root Cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem tissue consists of a variety of specialized, water-conducting cells known as tracheary elements.

Concept note-2: -Continuous transpiration occurring in all of the leaves of a plant creates a negative pressure in the water column (xylem). This exerts an upward pull on the water column, called transpiration pull. And thus, the water present in the xylem column transports up to the tip of the stem, leaves, etc.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The xylem tissue that helps in the transport of water and nutrients contain parenchyma cells. These cells are responsible for the soft parts of the plants. The tissue also contains tracheids that are long cells.

Concept note-4: -Xylem is one of the two types of transport tissue in vascular plants, the other being phloem. The basic function of xylem is to transport water from roots to stems and leaves, but it also transports nutrients.
