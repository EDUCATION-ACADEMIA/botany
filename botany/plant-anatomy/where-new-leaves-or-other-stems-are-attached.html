BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Where new leaves or other stems are attached.

(A) ** node

(B) stolon

(C) stem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The growing portion at the apex of the shoot is the terminal bud of the plant, and by the continued development of this bud and its adjacent tissues, the stem increases in height. Lateral buds and leaves grow out of the stem at intervals called nodes; the intervals on the stem between the nodes are called internodes.

Concept note-2: -Leaves are attached to the plant stem at areas called nodes. An internode is the stem region between two nodes. The petiole is the stalk connecting the leaf to the stem.

Concept note-3: -A node is an area on a stem where buds are located (figure 6). It is a site of great cellular activity and growth. It is here that small buds develop into leaves, stems or flowers.

Concept note-4: -The stem of the plant connects the roots to the leaves, helping to transport absorbed water and minerals to different parts of the plant.

Concept note-5: -A node on a plant is simply the location on a stem to which a leaf or branch is attached. If the leaf has fallen off then there will be a leaf scar. More: A node is an area on a stem where buds are located.
