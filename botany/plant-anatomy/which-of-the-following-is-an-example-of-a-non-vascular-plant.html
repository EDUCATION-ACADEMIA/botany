Which of the following is an example of a non-vascular plant?

(A) grass

(B) corn

(C) trees

(D) ** moss

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is Mosses. Mosses are the non-vascular plants. All the bryophytes and the algae are non-vascular plants.

Concept note-2: -Mosses and liverworts are small, primitive, non-vascular plants. They lack the conductive tissue most plants use to transport water and nutrients. Instead, moisture is absorbed directly into cells by osmosis.

Concept note-3: -Nonvascular plants include the mosses, the hornworts, and the liverworts.

Concept note-4: -Mosses and ferns fall into two types: vascular and non-vascular. Vascular means they have tubes running throughout that can carry water and nutrients. Non-vascular plants, like liverworts, do not have tubes, so they are much smaller.
