Bryophytes have all of the following characteristics EXCEPT:

(A) multicellularity

(B) a reduced, dependent sporophyte

(C) specialized cells and tissue

(D) ** well-developed vascular tissue

(E) a protected, stationary egg cell

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bryophytes do not have vascular tissues.

Concept note-2: -They are non-vascular plants, which means they have no roots or vascular tissue, but instead absorb water and nutrients from the air through their surface (e.g., their leaves).

Concept note-3: -General Characteristics of Bryophytes: Plants occur in damp and shaded areas. The plant body is thallus-like, i.e. prostrate or erect. It is attached to the substratum by rhizoids, which are unicellular or multicellular. They have a root-like, stem-like, and leaf-like structure and lack true vegetative structure.

Concept note-4: -In bryophytes the long-lived and conspicuous generation is the gametophyte, while in vascular plants it is the sporophyte. Structures resembling stems, roots, and leaves are found on the gametophore of bryophytes, while these structures are found on the sporophytes in the vascular plants.
