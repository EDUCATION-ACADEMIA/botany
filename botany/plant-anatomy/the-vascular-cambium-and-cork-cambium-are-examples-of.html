The vascular cambium and cork cambium are examples of

(A) Intercalery meristem

(B) ** Lateral meristem

(C) Apical meristem

(D) Primary meristem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Assertion :Fascicular vascular cambium, interfascicular cambium and cork-cambium are examples of lateral meristems Reason: These are responsible for producing the secondary tissues.

Concept note-2: -So, the correct answer is ‘Lateral meristem’.

Concept note-3: -Cambium are present along the lateral sides of stem and roots, therefore these are called lateral meristems. Interstelar cambium ring formed by intrafasicular and interfasicular cambium are examples of lateral meristems.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is Lateral Meristem. Cork cambium is the type of cambium that is related to the formation of the wood in the plants. Lateral meristem is the meristematic tissue that is situated at the peripheral zone of the plant both at the stem and the roots. Hence, Option 2 is correct.

Concept note-5: -The vascular cambium and cork cambium are secondary meristems that are formed in stems and roots after the tissues of the primary plant body have differentiated.
