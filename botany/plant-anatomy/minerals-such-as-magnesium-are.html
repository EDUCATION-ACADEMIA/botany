BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Minerals, such as magnesium, are

(A) absorbed by the leaves.

(B) absorbed by the phloem and transported up through the plant

(C) ** absorbed by the root in water and transported to the vascular tissue and through the shoot system.

(D) harmful to plants and cause them to wilt.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem transports water, minerals, and nutrients from the soil to all the plant parts. There are two types of “transport” tissues in plants-xylem and phloem. Water and solutes are transported by the xylem from the roots to the leaves, and food is transported from the leaves to the rest of the plant by the phloem.

Concept note-2: -xylem, plant vascular tissue that conveys water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the rest of the plant and also provides physical support. Xylem tissue consists of a variety of specialized, water-conducting cells known as tracheary elements.

Concept note-3: -Plants absorb water from the soil by osmosis. They absorb mineral ions by active transport, against the concentration gradient. Root hair cells are adapted for taking up water and mineral ions by having a large surface area to increase the rate of absorption.

Concept note-4: -Plants absorb minerals in ionic form: nitrate (NO3−), phosphate (HPO4−) and potassium ions (K+); all have difficulty crossing a charged plasma membrane. It has long been known plants expend energy to actively take up and concentrate mineral ions.
