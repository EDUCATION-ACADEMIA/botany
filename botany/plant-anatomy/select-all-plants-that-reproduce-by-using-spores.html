[SOURCES]
Select ALL plants that reproduce by using spores.

(A) Gymnosperms

(B) Angiosperms

(C) ** Seedless vascular plants

(D) ** Nonvascular plants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A few algae, liverworts, hornworts and mosses are nonvascular plants. Reproduction in such plants is through spore production in contrast to seeds, as a result they do not bear wood, flowers or fruits.

Concept note-2: -Nonvascular plants include liverworts, hornworts, and mosses. They lack roots, stems, and leaves. Nonvascular plants are low-growing, reproduce with spores, and need a moist habitat.

Concept note-3: -Most nonvascular plants reproduce sexually by creating single-celled spores or asexually by vegetative propagation. Vegetative propagation is when part of the plant breaks off and develops into a new plant with the exact same genetic information as the original plant.

Concept note-4: -Examples are mosses, liverworts and hornworts. They reproduce by spores.
