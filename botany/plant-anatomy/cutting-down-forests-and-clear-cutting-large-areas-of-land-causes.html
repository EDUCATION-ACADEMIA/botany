BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Cutting down forests and clear-cutting large areas of land causes:

(A) Contamination of ground water

(B) Depletion of aquifers

(C) ** Erosion of soil

(D) Death due to poisoning

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -More immediately, the loss of trees from a forest can leave soil more prone to erosion. This causes the remaining plants to become more vulnerable to fire as the forest shifts from being a closed, moist environment to an open, dry one. While deforestation can be permanent, this is not always the case.

Concept note-2: -Harvesting activities reduce surface cover, and compact the soil, leading to increased runoff and erosion. Erosion generally decreases productivity of forests by decreasing the available soil water for forest growth and through loss of nutrients in eroded sediment.

Concept note-3: -Soil compaction, low organic matter, loss of soil structure, poor internal drainage, salinisation and soil acidity problems are other serious soil degradation conditions that can accelerate the soil erosion process. This fact sheet looks at the causes and effects of water, wind and tillage erosion on agricultural land.

Concept note-4: -It is a natural process. The major causes of soil erosion are namely, deforestation, overgrazing, forest fires and poor farming techniques. Hence the statement is correct.

Concept note-5: -Deforestation and forest degradation are responsible for around 15% of all greenhouse gas emissions. These greenhouse gas emissions contribute to rising temperatures, changes in patterns of weather and water, and an increased frequency of extreme weather events.
