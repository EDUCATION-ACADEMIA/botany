In woody plants the dermal tissue is called the

(A) dermal

(B) ** periderm

(C) epidermis

(D) phloem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The periderm is a protective tissue that is formed during secondary growth in the stems, branches and roots of most dycotyledons and gymnosperms.

Concept note-2: -The dermal tissue system-the epidermis-is the outer protective layer of the primary plant body (the roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds). The epidermis is usually one cell layer thick, and its cells lack chloroplasts.

Concept note-3: -Periderm is known as the secondary dermal tissue. In older stems it replaces the roots of the plant. It helps in protecting from mechanical injury, diseases, heat, freezing, and desiccation.

Concept note-4: -In woody plants, the epidermis of newly grown stems is replaced by the periderm later in the year. As the stems grow a layer of cells form under the epidermis, called the cork cambium, these cells produce cork cells that turn into cork.

Concept note-5: -The periderm acts as armor protecting the plantâ€™s inner tissues from biotic and abiotic stress. It forms during the radial thickening of plant organs such as stems and roots and replaces the function of primary protective tissues such as the epidermis and the endodermis.
