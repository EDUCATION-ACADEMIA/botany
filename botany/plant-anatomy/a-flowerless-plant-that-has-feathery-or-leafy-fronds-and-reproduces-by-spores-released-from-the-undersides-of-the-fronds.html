BOTANY

PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
A flowerless plant that has feathery or leafy fronds and reproduces by spores released from the undersides of the fronds.

(A) weed

(B) cactus

(C) moss

(D) ** fern

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -More information: Ferns is a flowerless plant which has feathery or leafy fronds and reproduces by spores released from the undersides of the fronds.

Concept note-2: -Sporangium. The reproductive structures on the underside of the frond. Each sporangium is a capsule that contains spores. They are usually aggregated into clusters called sori.

Concept note-3: -The leaves of ferns are often called fronds. Fronds are usually composed of a leafy blade and petiole (leaf stalk).

Concept note-4: -Frond. The leaf of a fern. These are called fronds to distinguish them from the leaves of flowering plants. Leaves in flowering plants are purely concerned with photosynthesis whereas fern fronds have both a photosynthetic function and a reproductive function.

Concept note-5: -These neat clusters of dots on the backs of fern foliage are spores, and it is the method by which ferns reproduce. So these dots are a good thing – they tell you that your fern is happy, and virile!
