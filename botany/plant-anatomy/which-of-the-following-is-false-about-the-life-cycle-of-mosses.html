Which of the following is FALSE about the life cycle of mosses?

(A) External water is required for fertilization

(B) Gametophytes arise from the protonema.

(C) Antheridia and archegonia are produced by gametophytes.

(D) ** Gametes are directly produced by meiosis.

(E) Flagellated sperm are produced.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Moss gametes are produced by undergoing meiosis. Moss gametes are haploids which mean that they contain half the chromosome number. They produce an egg and a sperm. Meiosis is cell division that creates four daughter cells with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cells that produced them.

Concept note-2: -The life cycle of mosses alternates between the haploid gametophyte and the diploid sporophyte, called alternation of generation. The female and male gametophyte produce haploid gametes, which fuse to form a zygote and give rise to the diploid sporophyte.

Concept note-3: -Sporophyte means “spore-making plant.” The sporophyte produces haploid spores via meiosis. Haploid spores germinate and grow into the haploid gametophyte generation. Gametophyte refers to the “gamete-making plant.” This generation produces the gametes (egg and sperm) by mitosis.

Concept note-4: -The sexual reproduction of the moss (bryophyte) life cycle alternates between diploid sporophyte and haploid gametophyte phases. In a nutshell, haploid gametophytes produce haploid gametes, which can be sperm or eggs.
