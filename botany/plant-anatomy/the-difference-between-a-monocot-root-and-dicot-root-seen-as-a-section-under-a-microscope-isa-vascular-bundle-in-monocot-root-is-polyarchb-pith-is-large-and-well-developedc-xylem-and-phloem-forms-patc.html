The difference between a monocot root and dicot root seen as a section under a microscope isa) Vascular bundle in monocot root is polyarchb) Pith is large and well developedc) Xylem and Phloem forms patches numbered 2-4d) Pith is small

(A) ** a, b

(B) a, c

(C) a, d

(D) s, d

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The difference between dicot and monocot roots is that monocot roots are fibrous roots, they have a wide network of thin roots that emerges from the stem. Dicots, on the other hand, have taproots, which means that they form a single thick root from which small, lateral branches emerge.

Concept note-2: -In monocot roots, the number of xylem and phloem bundles are polyarch (more than 6 vascular bundles) while in dicots, the number of vascular bundles usually varies from diarch to hexarch.

Concept note-3: -One could visually distinguish a cross-section fo a monocot root from a dicot root by the arrangement of the xylem and phloem. In a dicot, the xylem and phloem form an X or Y shape compared to the monocot are arranged in a ring formation.
