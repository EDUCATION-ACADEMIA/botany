The outermost layer of CELLS of the leaf is the

(A) cuticle

(B) ** epidermis

(C) spongy mesophyll

(D) bundle sheath

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The outermost layer of tissue on the leaf is known as the epidermis. This outer layer is a thin layer of cells that helps protect the plant against water loss. Leaves also have a layer of cuticle on the epidermis.

Concept note-2: -The outermost layer of the leaf is the epidermis. It consists of the upper and lower epidermis, which are present on either side of the leaf. Botanists call the upper side the adaxial surface (or adaxis) and the lower side the abaxial surface (or abaxis). The epidermis aids in the regulation of gas exchange.

Concept note-3: -The stratum corneum is the outermost layer of the epidermis and is composed of dead scale-like cells that become progressively flattened and joined together.

Concept note-4: -The epidermis (from the Greek , meaning “over-skin") is a single layer of cells that covers the leaves, flowers, roots and stems of plants. It forms a boundary between the plant and the external environment.

Concept note-5: -Epidermis: A single layer of cells that covers all parts of a plant. Plants leaves contain an upper epidermis which is located on the upper side of the leaf and a lower epidermis located on the underside of the leaf.
