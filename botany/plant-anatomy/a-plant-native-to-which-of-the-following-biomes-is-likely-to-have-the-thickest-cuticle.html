A plant native to which of the following biomes is likely to have the thickest cuticle?

(A) Tropical Rainforest

(B) ** Desert

(C) Wetlands

(D) Temperate Grasslands

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: The leaves of the banyan plants have thick cuticles.

Concept note-2: -The cuticle is the waxy layer secreted by the epidermis on the two surfaces of the leaf. The thickness of the cuticle varies from plant to plant. Desert plants have thicker cuticles to cut down transpiration.

Concept note-3: -Desert plants tend to have thicker cuticles to cut down transpiration. The thicker cuticle will help to reduce water loss through leaves.

Concept note-4: -Therefore, desert plants have thicker epidermis in order to prevent loss of water in dry conditions.
