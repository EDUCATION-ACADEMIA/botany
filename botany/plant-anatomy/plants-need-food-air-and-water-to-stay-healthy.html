PLANT ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Plants need food, air and water to stay healthy

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants, like all living things, need food to survive. Plants make their food using a process called photosynthesis, which means “putting together through light.” During photosynthesis, a plant traps energy from sunlight with its leaves. It also takes up water from its roots and carbon dioxide gas from the air.

Concept note-2: -Humans and animals get food by eating it. Plants don’t get food the same way. Instead of eating food, plants take materials with no energy from the environment and use the energy from sunlight to make an energy-rich food, sugar. Plants use two types of materials to make food: water and air.

Concept note-3: -The vital needs of a plant are very much like our own-light, water, air, nutrients, and a proper temperature. The relative importance of each of these needs differs widely among plants.

Concept note-4: -Water, air, and sunlight are the most important things that a plant needs for growth. Plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and convert it into glucose through the process of photosynthesis, which is powered by sunlight.

Concept note-5: -Plants have specific needs-light, air, water, nutrients, and space-to survive and reproduce. Almost all plants need these five things to survive: Light. Air.
