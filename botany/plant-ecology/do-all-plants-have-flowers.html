Do ALL plants have flowers?

(A) Yes

(B) ** No

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Flowers are found on all sorts of plants, but not all plants have them. Moss, ferns, and pine trees, for example, don’t have flowers, but a lot of plants do. Plants have flowers because they need to make seeds.

Concept note-2: -Shade: Lack of adequate light is another very common reason that many types of plants do not flower. Plants may grow but not flower in the shade. Cold or Frost Injury: Cold weather may kill flower buds or partially opened flowers.

Concept note-3: -Liverwort, Ferms, and Mosses are all examples of non-flowering plants because they produce spores, instead of seeds. Spores are small and unique structures present in the plants for reproduction. They are very light in weight and look like dust. Spores are dispersed by wind to different places where they grow.

Concept note-4: -No. Although most of the world’s plants are flowering plants called angiosperms (from the Greek words for “vessel” and “seed”), there are hundreds of plants that do not make flowers.

Concept note-5: -Some plants have no flowers at all. Flowering plants are a group of plants called angiosperms. There are also non-flowering plants. These include mosses, ferns and conifers.
