Plants are essential to animals because they produce ____ gas.

(A) carbon dioxide

(B) nitrogen

(C) hydrogen

(D) ** oxygen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oxygen is a very essential gas on the earth atmosphere and is necessary for every living organism to survive. Oxygen is released by plants during the process of photosynthesis. And oxygen is used by animals or humans for survival so we can say that Oxygen gas is produced by plants and used by animals.

Concept note-2: -Oxygen is given off by green plants in the process of photosynthesis. Green plants are the lungs of the earth! All animals inhale oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide. Plants take in carbon dioxide and give off oxygen as they make food.

Concept note-3: -Answer: Solution: Animals, during respiration, take in oxygen and release carbon dioxide gas. Plants, on the other hand, utilize this carbon dioxide gas in the process of photosynthesis to produce food and release oxygen in the atmosphere.

Concept note-4: -Through a process called photosynthesis, leaves pull in carbon dioxide and water and use the energy of the sun to convert this into chemical compounds such as sugars that feed the tree. But as a by-product of that chemical reaction oxygen is produced and released by the tree.

Concept note-5: -Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create oxygen and energy in the form of sugar.
