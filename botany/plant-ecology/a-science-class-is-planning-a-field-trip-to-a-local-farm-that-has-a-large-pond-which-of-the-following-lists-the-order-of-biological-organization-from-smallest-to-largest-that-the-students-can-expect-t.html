A science class is planning a field trip to a local farm that has a large pond. Which of the following lists the order of biological organization from smallest to largest that the students can expect to find at the pond?

(A) Organism, community, population, ecosystem

(B) ** Organism, population, community, ecosystem

(C) Population, organism, community, ecosystem

(D) Population, organism, ecosystem, community

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The students can expect to find at the pond from smallest to largest: organism » population » community » ecosystem.

Concept note-2: -The biological levels of organization of living things arranged from the simplest to most complex are: organelle, cells, tissues, organs, organ systems, organisms, populations, communities, ecosystem, and biosphere.

Concept note-3: -The correct order from least inclusive to most inclusive is: Cell (basic unit of life), tissue (made up of cells), an organ (made up of tissues), an organ system (made up of organs), and an organism (made up of organ systems).

Concept note-4: -The smallest level of organization for a living thing is the cell. Cells are the smallest living units and all of the biochemical work that occurs in an organism will occur inside of cells.

Concept note-5: -The levels, from smallest to largest, are: molecule, cell, tissue, organ, organ system, organism, population, community, ecosystem, biosphere.
