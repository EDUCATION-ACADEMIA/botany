Tia looked at a leaf under a microscope and noticed a number of pores on its surface. These pores are called ____

(A) cells

(B) veins

(C) ** stomata

(D) chlorophyll

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This evolutionary innovation is so central to plant identity that nearly all land plants use the same pores-called stomata-to take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen. Stomata are tiny, microscopic and critical for photosynthesis.

Concept note-2: -Stomata (noun, “STO-mah-tah”, singular “stoma”) These are the small pores in plant stems or leaves that allow carbon dioxide in and oxygen and water vapor out.

Concept note-3: -Specialized cells known as guard cells surround stomata and function to open and close stomatal pores. Stomata allow a plant to take in carbon dioxide, which is needed for photosynthesis. They also help to reduce water loss by closing when conditions are hot or dry.

Concept note-4: -The tiny pores present on the surface of leaves are known as stomata.
