PLANT ECOLOGY

[SOURCES]
A large, distinctive complex of plant communities created and maintained by climate.

(A) Ecosystem

(B) ** Biome

(C) Biosphere

(D) Biology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A biome is a large, distinctive complex of plant communities created and maintained by climate.

Concept note-2: -Tropical forests have the highest biodiversity and primary productivity of any of the terrestrial biomes.

Concept note-3: -Biomes are regions showing similar temperature, elevation, humidity, precipitation, wind, and other rainfall patterns. It is more short term, and can change on an almost hourly basis! Weather-related climatic factors divide the climate into polar, temperate, arid, tropical, Mediterranean, and mountainous zones.

Concept note-4: -A biome is an area classified according to the species that live in that location. Temperature range, soil type, and the amount of light and water are unique to a particular place and form the niches for specific species allowing scientists to define the biome.

Concept note-5: -Biome can be defined as a group of organisms that share common characteristics, and these characteristics can be correlated to the environment in which they exist. A biome consists of both plants and animals. Biomes can be found on almost all continents.
