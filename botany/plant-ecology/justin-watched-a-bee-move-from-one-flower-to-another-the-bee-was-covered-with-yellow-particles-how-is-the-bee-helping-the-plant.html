Justin watched a bee move from one flower to another. The bee was covered with yellow particles. How is the bee helping the plant?

(A) It is fertilizing the plant.

(B) ** It is pollinating the plant.

(C) It is dispersing the plantâ€™s seeds.

(D) It is helping the plant germinate.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The bee has effectively taken the pollen from that flower. The bee now moves to another flower, on another plant. As the bee collects nectar some of the pollen from the other plant is rubbed onto the stigma of another plant. Now, this new plant will reproduce.

Concept note-2: -Pollen, which contains a lot of protein and is primarily used to rear the brood, is picked up by the bees almost as they fly past. It sticks to the hairs on the bees, and when they visit the next plant of the same species it is transferred in adequate quantities to their stigma.

Concept note-3: -Pollination is the transfer of pollen from an anther of a plant to the stigma of a plant, later enabling fertilisation and the production of seeds, most often by an animal or by wind.

Concept note-4: -Mutualism is when two organisms are involved. For example, A bumble bee and a flower. The bee lands on the flower and starts to take the pollen from the flower. This helps the bee live.

Concept note-5: -Mutualism, on the other hand, is a relationship between two species where both benefit from the association. In mutualistic relationships between flowers and their pollinators, flowers benefit by having their pollen efficiently distributed to other flowers of the same species, allowing them to reproduce.
