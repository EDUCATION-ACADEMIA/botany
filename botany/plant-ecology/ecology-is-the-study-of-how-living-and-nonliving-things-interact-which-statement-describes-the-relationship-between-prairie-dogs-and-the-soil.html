Ecology is the study of how living and nonliving things interact. Which statement describes the relationship between prairie dogs and the soil?

(A) The prairie dogs provide oxygen for the soil.

(B) The prairie dogs cause the soil to erode.

(C) ** The soil provides a shelter for the prairie dogs.

(D) The soil provides nutrients for the prairie dogs.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: The study of the Interaction between living organisms and their environment is called ecology.

Concept note-2: -Ecology is the study of the relationships between living organisms, including humans, and their physical environment; it seeks to understand the vital connections between plants and animals and the world around them.

Concept note-3: -Ecology is the study of ecological systems and the mutual interactions between organisms and the environment. The primary objective of forest ecology is to understand what controls the patterns of distribution and abundance of different organisms in forests of the world.

Concept note-4: -Ecology is the study of organisms, the environment and how the organisms interact with each other and their environment. It is studied at various levels, such as organism, population, community, biosphere and ecosystem.
