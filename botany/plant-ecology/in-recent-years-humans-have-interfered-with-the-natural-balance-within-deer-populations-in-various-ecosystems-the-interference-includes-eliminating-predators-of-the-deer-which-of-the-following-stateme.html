In recent years humans have interfered with the natural balance within deer populations in various ecosystems. The interference includes eliminating predators of the deer. Which of the following statements correctly describes the long-term outcome of this interference?

(A) The deer that are the fastest and most agile will survive and reproduce.

(B) ** The deer population will be too large to be supported by producers

(C) Other browsing species will thrive and outcompete the deer.

(D) The producers will evolve into species that are less palatable to the deer.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The interference includes eliminating predators of the deer. Which of the following statements correctly describes the long-term outcome of this interference? The deer that are the fastest and most agile will survive and reproduce.

Concept note-2: -Catastrophic events, such as deadly hurricanes, tornadoes, forest fires, or floods, can decrease biodiversity by killing a species or harming an ecosystem. Human activity can also cause a loss of biodiversity by displacing species from their natural habitat or killing organisms.

Concept note-3: -Species diversity is much greater in the ecosystem undergoing primary succession because that ecosystem is experiencing a longer period with a lack of competition for space.

Concept note-4: -Within all biological communities, energy at each trophic level is lost in the form of heat (as much as 80 to 90 percent), as organisms expend energy for metabolic processes such as staying warm and digesting food (see biosphere: The organism and the environment: Resources of the biosphere: The flow of energy).

Concept note-5: -Which of the following best describes the role that these microorganisms play in natural habitats? The microorganisms help keep nutrients cycling through the ecosystem. Some species of fungi, protists, and bacteria all play a vital role in every biogeochemical cycle.
