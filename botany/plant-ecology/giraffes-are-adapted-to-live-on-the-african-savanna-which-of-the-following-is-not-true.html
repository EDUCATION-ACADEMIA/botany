Giraffes are adapted to live on the African savanna. Which of the following is NOT true?

(A) They are tall so that it is easy to spot predators

(B) They can drink up to 12 gallons of water when available

(C) They have a long neck to eat leaves from tree tops

(D) ** They have a tiny heart to pump blood up their necks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A giraffe’s long neck is an adaptation that helps the giraffe reach foods that other animals can’t eat, such as leaves that are very high up in trees.

Concept note-2: -There are three types of adaptations: structural, physiological, and behavioral. Structural adaptations are how the animal’s body functions or looks on the outside. Body parts (like feet and ears) and body coverings (like fur and scales) are structural adaptations.

Concept note-3: -Answer: We close our eyes when a beam of light suddenly falls on it is not an adaptation.

Concept note-4: -An adaptation is a characteristic that helps an animal survive in its habitat. All animals must be able to obtain food and water, protect themselves from harm, withstand the climate, and reproduce young so the species doesn’t become extinct.
