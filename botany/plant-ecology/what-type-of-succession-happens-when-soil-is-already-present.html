What type of succession happens when soil is already present?

(A) primary

(B) ** secondary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Secondary succession is more common and usually occurs faster than primary succession because the substrate is already present. Secondary succession occurs on a surface where an ecosystem has previously existed. This happens after some sort of disturbance.

Concept note-2: -Secondary succession is the ecological succession that occurs after the initial succession has been disrupted and some plants and animals still exist. It is usually faster than primary succession as soil is already present, and seeds, roots, and the underground vegetative organs of plants may still survive in the soil.

Concept note-3: -Secondary succession is usually faster than primary succession because soil and nutrients are already present due to ‘normalization’ by previous pioneer species, and because roots, seeds and other biotic organisms may still be present within the substrate.

Concept note-4: -Secondary succession. Secondary succession occurs when the starting point is bare soil that already exists. It is similar to primary succession, however, the pioneer species are often fast-growing plants and often come from pre-existing groups of organisms in the area.
