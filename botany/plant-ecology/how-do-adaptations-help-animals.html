How do adaptations help animals?

(A) to hide from predators

(B) to hunt for food

(C) to survive in their habitats

(D) ** All of these are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adaptation can protect animals from predators or from harsh weather. Many birds can hide in the tall grass and weeds and insects can change their colour to blend into the surroundings. This makes it difficult for predators to seek them out for food.

Concept note-2: -An adaptation is defined as a physical or behavioral feature of an animal that helps them better survive in their environment. In other words, an adaptation is something on their body or something they do with their bodies that help them find food, water, mates, and shelter.

Concept note-3: -There are three types of adaptations: structural, physiological, and behavioral. Structural adaptations are how the animalâ€™s body functions or looks on the outside. Body parts (like feet and ears) and body coverings (like fur and scales) are structural adaptations.

Concept note-4: -Adaption refers to the behavioral or physical attributes of an animal that helps them survive better in its ecosystem. Animal adaptation can be in the way they look (camouflage) to escape from their predators. Different animals have developed different adaptations to survive in their habitat.
