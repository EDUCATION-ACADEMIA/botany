Describe reproduction in flowering plants (angiosperms)

(A) ** pollen is released by the anthersticks to the stigmaa pollen tube forms that grows down the stylepollen tube reaches an ovule within the ovary

(B) pollen is released by the anthersticks to the stigmaa pollen tube grows down from the anther through the filamentpollen fuses with an ovule

(C) pollen moves from the ovule up the styleit is released from the stigmapollen is then transferred to the filament where the egg is fertilized

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Flowering plants or angiosperms reproduce sexually by the process known as pollination. In this process, the pollen from the male anther is transferred to the female stigma. These pollens travel through the pollen tube and produce gametes which fuse with the egg in the embryo sac. This gives rise to fruit.

Concept note-2: -Flowering plants reproduce sexually through a process called pollination. Flowers contain male sex organs called stamens and female sex organs called pistils. The anther is the part of the stamen that contains pollen. Pollen contains the male gametes.

Concept note-3: -In flowering plants, male and female reproductive structures can be found in the same individual plant. The organ of sexual reproduction is the flower. Male gametes are found in pollen grains and produced in the anthers of the flower. Female gametes are found in ovules and produced in the ovary of the flower.

Concept note-4: -Pollen is carried by insects or blown by the wind from one flower to another. This process is called pollination. Pollen reaches the new flower and travels to the ovary where it fertilises egg cells (ovules) to make seeds. This is fertilisation.

Concept note-5: -What are the reproductive parts of a flower? Stamen is the male reproductive part of a flower, while pistil is the female reproductive part of a flower. The stamen is surrounded by anther and filament. The anthers produce pollen grains.
