What is one observable interaction of how organisms live and survive in their environment?

(A) ** Grass supplying food and oxygen to a rabbit

(B) A deer providing oxygen and food to a shrub

(C) Rocks and water eroding a hillside environment

(D) A tree providing carbon dioxide for a squirrel

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Summary. Species interactions within ecological webs include four main types of two-way interactions: mutualism, commensalism, competition, and predation (which includes herbivory and parasitism).

Concept note-2: -A habitat is a place where an organism makes its home. A habitat meets all the environmental conditions an organism needs to survive. For an animal, that means everything it needs to find and gather food, select a mate, and successfully reproduce.

Concept note-3: -Living organisms depend on each other and on their environments, or habitats, to meet their needs for survival. We call this interdependence. A food chain is one way to show interdependence. A food chain shows the energy transfer from one organism to another.

Concept note-4: -Living organisms require five basic needs: sunlight, water, air, habitat, and food.
