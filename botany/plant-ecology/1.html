Which of the following pairs of plants produce spores?

(A) ** Mosses and Ferns

(B) Ferns and Cone-bearing plants

(C) Cone-bearing plants and Flowering plants

(D) Flowering plants and Mosses

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -so, the correct answer is ‘Cryptogams ’

Concept note-2: -Gymnosperms and angiosperms form two kinds of spores: microspores, which give rise to male gametophytes, and megaspores, which produce female gametophytes.

Concept note-3: -Some plants don’t produce flowers and seeds. Plants such as ferns and mosses are called nonflowering plants and produce spores instead of seeds.

Concept note-4: -Fungi, moss and fern all three reproduce by a common method of asexual reproduction which is called spore formation. Spore formation occurs in non-flowering plants. Spore are minute structures that are produced by the parent plant and are released into the air.

Concept note-5: -Ferns, lycophytes, mosses, liverworts, and hornworts (the latter three are collectively termed bryophytes) reproduce via spores.
