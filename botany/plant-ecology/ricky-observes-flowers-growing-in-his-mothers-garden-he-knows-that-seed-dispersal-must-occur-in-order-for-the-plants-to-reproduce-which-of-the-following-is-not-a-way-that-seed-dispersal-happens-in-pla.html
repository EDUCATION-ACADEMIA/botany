EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dispersion or seed dispersal is the movement or transport of seeds away from the parent plant.

Concept note-2: -The wind is the natural and fundamental means of seed dispersal in the plant kingdom. This process of dispersal is mainly seen in those plants which bear very light seeds.

Concept note-3: -There are five main modes of seed dispersal: gravity, wind, ballistic, water, and by animals. Some plants are serotinous and only disperse their seeds in response to an environmental stimulus. These modes are typically inferred based on adaptations, such as wings or fleshy fruit.

Concept note-4: -The movement of seeds away from their parent plant is called seed dispersal.
