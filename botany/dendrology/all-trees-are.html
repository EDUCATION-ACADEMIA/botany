All trees are:

(A) Annuals

(B) Biennials

(C) ** Perrenials

(D) None of the choices

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -perennial, any plant that persists for several years, usually with new herbaceous growth from a part that survives from growing season to growing season. Trees and shrubs, including all gymnosperms (cone-bearing plants), are perennials, as are some herbaceous (nonwoody) flowering plants and vegetative ground covers.

Concept note-2: -Perennial Plants Perennials are plants that can live for three or more growing seasons (oftentimes, especially in St. Louis, bulbs must be planted in autumn to produce spring-blooming plants). Garden Heights Nursery carries a wide variety of container perennials grown for sun and shade.

Concept note-3: -A perennial plant is a type of plant that survives for a long period of time. By definition, perennial plants survive more than two years in their natural habitats unlike an annual (survives for one year) or biennial (survives for two years) plant.

Concept note-4: -perennial : present at all seasons of the year. 2 : persisting for several years usually with new herbaceous growth from a perennating part. 3 a : persistent, enduring. b : continuing without interruption : constant, perpetual.

Concept note-5: -Perennial typically describes things that are permanent, constant, or repeated.
