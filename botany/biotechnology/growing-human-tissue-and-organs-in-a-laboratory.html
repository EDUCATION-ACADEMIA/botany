growing human tissue and organs in a laboratory

(A) Gene Therapy

(B) Bioremediation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tissue culture – Tissue culture (or cell culture) is a way to grow cells in a laboratory. Cells are taken, and put into in a flask or Petri dish and cultured on a growth medium. The cells divide, and can be treated in various ways. This is done for a number of purposes, especially for the use of scientific research.

Concept note-2: -Now-after nearly a decade of figuring out how to make cells grow, organize, and specialize into 3D structures similar to human tissues, scientists have created a veritable zoo of “organoids, ” including livers, pancreases, stomachs, hearts, kidneys, and even mammary and salivary glands.

Concept note-3: -While more complex organ tissues like heart, lung, and liver tissue have been successfully recreated in the lab, they are a long way from being fully reproducible and ready to implant into a patient. These tissues, however, can be quite useful in research, especially in drug development.

Concept note-4: -These organoids resemble brains in the early embryonic stage. Liver buds have been grown from human stem cells by scientists from Yokohama City University. These immature clumps of liver cells formed their own blood supply and matured into adult liver cells when transplanted into mice.

Concept note-5: -Tissue Engineering is the science of growing replacement organs and tissue in the lab to replace damaged or diseased tissue. The process usually starts with a three-dimensional structure called a scaffold that is used to support cells as they grow and develop.
