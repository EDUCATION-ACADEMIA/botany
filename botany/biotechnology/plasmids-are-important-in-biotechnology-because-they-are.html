Plasmids are important in biotechnology because they are

(A) ** A vehicle for the insertion of recombinant DNA into bacteria

(B) Recognition sites on recombinant DNA strands

(C) Proviruses incorporated into the host DNA

(D) Surfaces for protein synthesis in eukaryotic recombinants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -a vehicle for the insertion of recombinant DNA into bacteria. The plasmid usually contains a stretch of DNA with sequences that can be cut by multiple restriction enzymes, facilitating the insertion of the gene of interest (recombinant DNA) into the vector.

Concept note-2: -Plasmids have been key to the development of molecular biotechnology. They act as delivery vehicles, or vectors, to introduce foreign DNA into bacteria. Using plasmids for DNA delivery began in the 1970s when DNA from other organisms was first ‘cut and pasted’ into specific sites within the plasmid DNA.

Concept note-3: -Plasmids play an important role in gene therapy. Recombinant DNA technology makes use of plasmids to deliver drugs such as insulin and different hormones into the body. The modified plasmids cause antibiotic resistance and are used to kill harmful bacteria in the body.

Concept note-4: -Plasmids are used to transfer the information from one cell to another, i.e., transfer of important genes (e.g., they may confer resistance of particular, antibiotics to their bacterial cells), enabl to metabolise a nutrient, which normally a bacteria is unable to. It also helps in conjugation of bacteria.
