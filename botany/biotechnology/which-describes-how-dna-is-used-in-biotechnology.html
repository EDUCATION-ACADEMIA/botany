Which describes how DNA is used in biotechnology?

(A) ** The genetic information contained in all living cells can be changed, removed, or added to make products and living organisms function better.

(B) DNA is not used in biotechnology, only RNA is used.

(C) Only the DNA in animal cells can be changed to improve products and living organisms.

(D) Only the DNA in plant cells can be changed to improve products and living organisms.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition. Genetic engineering (also called genetic modification) is a process that uses laboratory-based technologies to alter the DNA makeup of an organism. This may involve changing a single base pair (A-T or C-G), deleting a region of DNA or adding a new segment of DNA.

Concept note-2: -Many forms of modern biotechnology rely on DNA technology. DNA technology is the sequencing, analysis, and cutting-and-pasting of DNA. Common forms of DNA technology include DNA sequencing, polymerase chain reaction, DNA cloning, and gel electrophoresis.

Concept note-3: -Recombinant DNA: DNA which has been altered from its original form by joining genetic material from two different sources. It usually involves putting a gene or part of a gene obtained from one organism into the genome of a different organism.

Concept note-4: -Genetic information is carried in the linear sequence of nucleotides in DNA. Each molecule of DNA is a double helix formed from two complementary strands of nucleotides held together by hydrogen bonds between G-C and A-T base pairs.

Concept note-5: -Biotechnology is the branch of applied science that uses living organisms and their derivatives to produce products and processes. These products and processes feature in healthcare, medicine, biofuels, and environmental safety.
