Scientists consider genetically altering cedar trees so they are more resistant to root rot. Which is the most accurate statement regarding the alteration?

(A) genetic alteration should never be considered as an option

(B) genetically altering the trees would endanger the species

(C) ** the population of cedar trees would increase and grow healthier

(D) the demand for cedar trees would be reduced because of possible negative consequences of genetic alterations.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -With genetic engineering, however, any gene from any organism can be transferred to a different organism. Thus, that allows a snippet of DNA that codes for an insecticidal protein from a bacterium, such as Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), to be transferred into a corn or cotton plant.

Concept note-2: -The foundation of Mendelian genetics enabled plant breeders to cross plants with new precision, carefully manipulating the plant genome to produce new, improved varieties. These breeding techniques have been used to develop higher-yielding varieties, including plants resistant to pests or disease.
