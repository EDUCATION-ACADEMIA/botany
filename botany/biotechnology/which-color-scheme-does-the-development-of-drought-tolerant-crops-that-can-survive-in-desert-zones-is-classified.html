Which color scheme does the development of drought-tolerant crops that can survive in desert zones is classified

(A) ** brown biotechnology

(B) blue biotechnology

(C) red biotechnology

(D) green biotechnology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Brown biotechnology is related to the management of arid lands and deserts. One application is the creation of enhanced seeds that resist extreme environmental conditions of arid regions, which is related to the innovation, creation of agriculture techniques and management of resources.

Concept note-2: -Kafarski (2012) developed a color code to differentiate the main areas of biotechnology: white (industrial), green (agricultural), blue (marine and fresh-water), red (pharmaceutical), brown (desert biotechnology), purple (patents and inventions), among others.

Concept note-3: -Brown Biotechnology It involves GM technology to create better-quality seeds that can grow crops in arid areas where there are low amounts of rainfall. There are already crops that are resistant to desert conditions because of how they have been genetically engineered. An example is DroughtGard.

Concept note-4: -Brown biotechnology is also called Arid Zone and Desert Biotechnology. The technology aims at making a beneficial impact by using improved disease-free high-quality seeds and makes the rational use of water in low-rainfall areas. Deserts and arid lands make a large part of the earth.
