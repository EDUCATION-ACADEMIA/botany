What is antibiotic resistance?

(A) ** Bacteria are resistant and not being killed by antibiotics.

(B) Bacteria are resistant and being completely wiped out by antibiotics.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Antimicrobial resistance happens when germs like bacteria and fungi develop the ability to defeat the drugs designed to kill them. That means the germs are not killed and continue to grow. Resistant infections can be difficult, and sometimes impossible, to treat.

Concept note-2: -Antibiotics and antifungals kill some germs that cause infections, but they also kill helpful germs that protect our body from infection. The antimicrobial-resistant germs survive and multiply. These surviving germs have resistance traits in their DNA that can spread to other germs.
