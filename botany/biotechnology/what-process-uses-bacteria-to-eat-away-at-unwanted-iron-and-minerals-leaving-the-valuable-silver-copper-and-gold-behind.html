What process uses bacteria to eat away at unwanted iron and minerals, leaving the valuable silver, copper and gold behind?

(A) Fermentation

(B) Bioremediation

(C) ** Biomining

(D) Composting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Biomining is the process of using microorganisms (microbes) to extract metals of economic interest from rock ores or mine waste. Biomining techniques may also be used to clean up sites that have been polluted with metals.

Concept note-2: -Instead of separating the metal from the pyrite with high temperatures or pressures, biomining uses microbes from the Acidithiobacillus and Leptospirillum genera to do the job.

Concept note-3: -Bioleaching involves the use of microorganisms to catalyze the oxidation of iron sulfides to create ferric sulfate and sulfuric acid. Ferric sulfate, which is a powerful oxidizing agent, then oxidizes the copper sulfide minerals and the copper contained is then leached by the sulfuric acid formed.

Concept note-4: -Ores are placed into acid, and then researchers introduce bacteria that change the solution so that it dismantles the rock and frees copper, in liquid form. And after a special electrochemical process, it is then turned into solid metal that can be used in the industrial applications we so much depend on.

Concept note-5: -Bioleaching (or biomining) is a process in mining and biohydrometallurgy (natural processes of interactions between microbes and minerals) that extracts valuable metals from a low-grade ore with the help of microorganisms such as bacteria or archaea.
