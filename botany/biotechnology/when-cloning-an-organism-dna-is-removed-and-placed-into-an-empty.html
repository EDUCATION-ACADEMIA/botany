When cloning an organism, DNA is removed and placed into an empty

(A) virus

(B) sperm cell

(C) bacteria cell

(D) ** egg cell

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cloning using somatic cell nuclear transfer (SCNT) [ 1]. This procedure starts with the removal of the chromosomes from an egg to create an enucleated egg. The chromosomes are replaced with a nucleus taken from a somatic (body) cell of the individual or embryo to be cloned.

Concept note-2: -The first step in the process of generating embryonic stem cells is to remove the nucleus from an unfertilized egg cell (A).

Concept note-3: -4. What is the purpose of the Egg Cell Donor? Provides the egg, which contains the machinery (organelles, proteins, etc. in the cytoplasm) to create the embryo.

Concept note-4: -The method for adult cell cloning is: the nucleus is removed from an unfertilised egg cell. the nucleus from an adult body cell, such as a skin cell, is inserted into the egg cell. an electric shock stimulates the egg cell to divide to form an embryo.

Concept note-5: -DNA cloning is the process of making multiple, identical copies of a particular piece of DNA. In a typical DNA cloning procedure, the gene or other DNA fragment of interest (perhaps a gene for a medically important human protein) is first inserted into a circular piece of DNA called a plasmid.
