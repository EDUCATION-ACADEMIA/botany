Permeability is the rate of movement of what through the soil?

(A) air and fertilizer

(B) ** water and air

(C) water and fertilizer

(D) water and microorganisms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Permeability is commonly measured in terms of the rate of water flow through the soil in a given period of time.

Concept note-2: -The permeability rate is a measure of how fast applied irrigation water moves through the soil. If an irrigator applies water at a rate exceeding the ability of the soil to accept it, the water will pool on the surface and eventually will run off, causing a non-uniform irrigation application and soil erosion.

Concept note-3: -The permeability of soil describes how water (or other liquid) and air are able to move through the soil. In the case of rainfall or irrigation, water moves very easily through highly permeable soils and very slowly through soils with low permeability.

Concept note-4: -Percolation is the movement of water through the soil itself. Finally, as the water percolates into the deeper layers of the soil, it reaches ground water, which is water below the surface. The upper surface of this underground water is called the â€œwater table".

Concept note-5: -Gravel and sand are both porous and permeable, making them good aquifer materials. Gravel has the highest permeability.
