In preparation for an electrophoresis procedure, enzymes are added to DNA in order to

(A) convert the DNA into gel

(B) ** cut the DNA into fragments

(C) change the color of the DNA

(D) produce longer sections of DNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -QUESTION 7 preparation for an electrophoresis procedure, enzymes are added to DNA in order 0 A convert the DNA into gel change the color of the DNA ccut the DNA into fragments Dproduce longer sections of DNA.

Concept note-2: -In the laboratory, restriction enzymes (or restriction endonucleases) are used to cut DNA into smaller fragments. The cuts are always made at specific nucleotide sequences.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is D. restriction enzymes. Restriction enzymes, which are produced naturally by bacteria as a defense against viruses, recognize and cut DNA into pieces at specific sequences.

Concept note-4: -Each restriction enzyme moves along a DNA molecule until it finds a specific recognition sequence in the DNA. The enzyme cuts the double-stranded DNA, resulting in DNA fragments.

Concept note-5: -Restriction enzymes are DNA-cutting enzymes. Each enzyme recognizes one or a few target sequences and cuts DNA at or near those sequences. Many restriction enzymes make staggered cuts, producing ends with single-stranded DNA overhangs.
