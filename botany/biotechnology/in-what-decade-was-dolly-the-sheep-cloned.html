In what decade was Dolly the sheep cloned?

(A) 1930s

(B) ** 1950s

(C) 1970s

(D) 1990s

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dolly was cloned from a cell taken from the mammary gland of a six-year-old Finn Dorset sheep and an egg cell taken from a Scottish Blackface sheep. She was born to her Scottish Blackface surrogate mother on 5th July 1996.

Concept note-2: -Everyone knows Dolly, but less known is that the first cloned animal success was a tadpole in the 1950s.

Concept note-3: -1952-First successful nuclear transfer The resulting cell developed into a tadpole. The scientists created many normal tadpole clones using nuclei from early embryos.

Concept note-4: -The lamb cloned from the udder cell was the first example of an animal cloned from an adult cell and although only referred to as “6LL3” in the Nature paper, she was later named Dolly, after the singer Dolly Parton.
