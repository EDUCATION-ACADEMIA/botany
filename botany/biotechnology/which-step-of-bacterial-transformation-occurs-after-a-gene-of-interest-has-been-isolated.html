Which step of bacterial transformation occurs after a gene of interest has been isolated?

(A) removing the plasmid from the bacterium

(B) inserting the plasmid into a bacterium

(C) inserting the gene of the parent generation into the plasmid

(D) ** cutting the plasmid with restriction enzymes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The piece of DNA or gene of interest is cut from its original DNA source using a restriction enzyme and then pasted into the plasmid by ligation. The plasmid containing the foreign DNA is now ready to be inserted into bacteria. This process is called transformation.

Concept note-2: -Key steps in the process of bacterial transformation: (1) competent cell preparation, (2) transformation of cells, (3) cell recovery, and (4) cell plating.

Concept note-3: -A small section is then cut out of the circular plasmid by restriction enzymes, ‘molecular scissors’. The gene for human insulin is inserted into the gap in the plasmid. This plasmid is now genetically modified. The genetically modified plasmid is introduced into a new bacteria or yeast cell.

Concept note-4: -To isolate a specific gene, one often begins by constructing a DNA library-a comprehensive collection of cloned DNA fragments from a cell, tissue, or organism.

Concept note-5: -In simplistic terms genetic engineering is accomplished in three basic steps. 1) The isolation of DNA fragments (sometimes specific genes) from a donor organism. 2) The insertion of an isolated donor DNA fragment into a vector genome. 3) The growth of a recombinant vector in an appropriate recipient host organism.
