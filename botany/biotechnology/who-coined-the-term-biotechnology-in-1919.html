Who coined the term “biotechnology” in 1919?

(A) Charles Darwin

(B) ** Karol Ereky

(C) James Watson

(D) Rosalind Franklin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1919, Hungarian agricultural engineer Karl Ereky foresaw a time when biology could be used for turning raw materials into useful products. He coined the term biotechnology to describe that merging of biology and technology.

Concept note-2: -The actual term ‘biotechnology’ was introduced by Karl Ereky, a Hungarian agricultural engineer, in his book Biotechnologie (abbr.), published in 1919 (and it stuck). By his definition, biotechnology was simply the process of using technology to convert raw, biological material into a useful product.

Concept note-3: -Károly Ereky (German: Karl Ereky; 20 October 1878 – 17 June 1952) was a Hungarian agricultural engineer. The term ‘biotechnology’ was coined by him in 1919. He is regarded by some as the “father” of biotechnology.

Concept note-4: -Father of indian biotechnology? Kiran Mazumdar Shaw (born in 1953) is considered as father of biotechnology in India.

Concept note-5: -The term ‘Biotechnology’ was coined in the year 1919 by an agricultural engineer Karoly Ereky; hence he is called the father of Biotechnology.
