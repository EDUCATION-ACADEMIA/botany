[SOURCES]
Antibiotics are prescribed to

(A) ** kill bacteria.

(B) breed bacteria.

(C) work as vaccines.

(D) assist in PCR.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Antibiotics are used to treat or prevent some types of bacterial infection. They kill bacteria or prevent them from reproducing and spreading. Antibiotics aren’t effective against viral infections. This includes the common cold, flu, most coughs and sore throats.

Concept note-2: -What is an antibiotic? Antibiotics are medicines that fight infections caused by bacteria in humans and animals by either killing the bacteria or making it difficult for the bacteria to grow and multiply. Bacteria are germs.

Concept note-3: -Antibiotics work by blocking vital processes in bacteria. They kill the bacteria or stop it from spreading. This helps the body’s natural immune system to fight the infection.

Concept note-4: -Antibiotics kill bacteria, not archaea, fungi, or protists.
