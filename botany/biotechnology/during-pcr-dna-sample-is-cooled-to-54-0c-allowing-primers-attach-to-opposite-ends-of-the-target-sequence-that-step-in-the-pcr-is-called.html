BIOTECHNOLOGY

During PCR, DNA sample is cooled to 54 0C, allowing primers attach to opposite ends of the target sequence. That step in the PCR is called:

(A) Denaturation

(B) ** Annealing

(C) Elongation

(D) Condensation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During this stage the reaction is cooled to 50-65⁰C. This enables the primers to attach to a specific location on the single-stranded template DNA by way of hydrogen bonding (the exact temperature depends on the melting temperature of the primers you are using).

Concept note-2: -Step 2-Annealing The sample mixture is then cooled to between 50 to 60°C (122 to 140°F) allowing the DNA primers and the DNA polymerase enzyme to bind to the individual strands of DNA that were separated by the heat (this is termed annealing of the primers).

Concept note-3: -Following the denaturation step is the annealing step. The temperature is decreased so that the primers can anneal to the complementary sequences on the DNA templates. The temperature of the annealing step depends on the melting temperatures of the primer. Selection of the annealing temperature is critical.

Concept note-4: -PCR is based on three simple steps required for any DNA synthesis reaction: (1) denaturation of the template into single strands; (2) annealing of primers to each original strand for new strand synthesis; and (3) extension of the new DNA strands from the primers.

Concept note-5: -target sequence. Defined region or sequence within the original DNA sample that is amplified in a PCR reaction. thermocycler. Machine used to rapidly and accurately shift between several temperatures in a pre-set order (for PCR).
