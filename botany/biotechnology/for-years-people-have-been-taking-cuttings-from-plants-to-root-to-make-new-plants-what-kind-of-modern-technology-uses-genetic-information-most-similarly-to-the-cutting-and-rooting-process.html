For years, people have been taking cuttings from plants to root to make new plants. What kind of modern technology uses genetic information most similarly to the cutting and rooting process?

(A) genetic engineering

(B) ** cloning of organisms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -7.7 Meristem Culture. The most important modern method of plant cloning is meristem culture, using the actively dividing tissue (meristems) of plants (shoots, roots, or axillary buds) (Fig. 7.21).

Concept note-2: -For example, when you take a cutting from a plant and grow it into a new plant (vegetative propagation), you are cloning the original plant because the new plant has the same genetic makeup as the donor plant.

Concept note-3: -Asexual propagation, sometimes referred to as vegetative propagation, involves taking vegetative parts of a plant (stems, roots, and/or leaves) and causing them to regenerate into a new plant or, in some cases, several plants. With few exceptions, the resulting plant is genetically identical to the parent plant.

Concept note-4: -Some common methods of cloning plants are cutting, grafting, layering, and tissue culture. Cuttings are small fragments of a plant that are removed and placed in substrate under the right growth conditions until they develop into independent offspring.

Concept note-5: -Somatic cell nuclear transfer (SCNT), also called nuclear transfer, uses a different approach than artificial embryo twinning, but it produces the same result: an exact genetic copy, or clone, of an individual. This was the method used to create Dolly the Sheep.
