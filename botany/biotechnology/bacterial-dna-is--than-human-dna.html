Bacterial DNA is ____ than human DNA

(A) ** More simple

(B) More complex

(C) uglier

(D) deadlier

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thus, bacterial genomes are only about 0.1% as big as the human genome, and have about 10% as many genes as we do. A comparison of those two percentages shows immediately that in bacteria the “gene density” (how many genes there are per unit length along the genome) is much higher than in humans.

Concept note-2: -The human genome is thought to contain approximately 100, 000 genes-only about 25 times more than E. coli has. Much of the complexity of eukaryotic genomes thus results from the abundance of several different types of noncoding sequences, which constitute most of the DNA of higher eukaryotic cells.

Concept note-3: -Bacteria and some related microorganisms called the Archeans have exceptions to the standard genetic code, but the bulk of their genetic code is the same one as humans.

Concept note-4: -Mammal genes often haven introns while bacterial genes never have introns. The mammal’s genome is much larger than the bacteria’s for MANY reasons. One reason is that mammal’s have many more genes and another reason is that a lot of mammals’ genomes don’t code for protein.

Concept note-5: -While humans possess 46 chromosomes consisting of linear DNA, bacteria possess a single circular chromosome comprised of double stranded DNA. This circular DNA provides the same function to bacteria as genetic material found in all living organisms-to support growth, cellular respiration, and reproduction.
