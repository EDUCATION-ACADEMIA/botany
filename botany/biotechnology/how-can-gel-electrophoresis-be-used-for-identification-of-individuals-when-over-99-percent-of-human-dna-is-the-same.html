EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How can gel electrophoresis be used for identification of individuals when over 99 percent of human DNA is the same? It focuses only on the small regions of the DNA which are known to vary among individuals. Experts can determine the different genes by reading the genetic alphabet displayed in the gel.

Concept note-2: -A transgenic, or genetically modified, organism is one that has been altered through recombinant DNA technology, which involves either the combining of DNA from different genomes or the insertion of foreign DNA into a genome.

Concept note-3: -With genetic engineering, however, any gene from any organism can be transferred to a different organism. Thus, that allows a snippet of DNA that codes for an insecticidal protein from a bacterium, such as Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), to be transferred into a corn or cotton plant.

Concept note-4: -The production of transgenic livestock has the opportunity to significantly improve human health, enhance nutrition, protect the environment, increase animal welfare, and decrease livestock disease.
