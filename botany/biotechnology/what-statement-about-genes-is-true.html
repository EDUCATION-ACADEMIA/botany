What statement about genes is TRUE?

(A) ** Nearly identical genes are found in a wide variety of organisms.

(B) Genes are only found in humans.

(C) Genes are found in all living organisms, but humans do not share genes with other organisms

(D) Genes in all mammals are identical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer to this question is B. They always cause a change to an organism’s genotype. Regardless of the cause of the change, a DNA sequence is always altered when it mutates.

Concept note-2: -In humans, alleles of particular genes come in pairs, one on each chromosome? (we have 23 pairs of chromosomes). If the alleles of a particular gene are the same, the organism is described as homozygous? for that gene.

Concept note-3: -Each variation of a gene is called an allele (pronounced ‘AL-eel’). These two copies of the gene contained in your chromosomes influence the way your cells work. The two alleles in a gene pair are inherited, one from each parent. Alleles interact with each other in different ways.

Concept note-4: -(b) Genes are fragments of DNA.
