Which of these is the correct definition of genetic engineering?

(A) Choosing individuals to mate based on desired characteristics

(B) Observing genetic changes in populations over time based on adaptive traits

(C) Extracting stem cells to differentiate into specialized tissues

(D) ** Altering the DNA of an organism at the molecular level

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition. Genetic engineering (also called genetic modification) is a process that uses laboratory-based technologies to alter the DNA makeup of an organism. This may involve changing a single base pair (A-T or C-G), deleting a region of DNA or adding a new segment of DNA.

Concept note-2: -A transgenic, or genetically modified, organism is one that has been altered through recombinant DNA technology, which involves either the combining of DNA from different genomes or the insertion of foreign DNA into a genome.

Concept note-3: -The correct answer is (c) an organism carrying a gene that was acquired by artificial means. “Genetically-modified organism” is a general term which refers to organisms that contain genetic material acquired through artificial means.

Concept note-4: -Genetic engineering, also called recombinant DNA technology, involves the group of techniques used to cut up and join together genetic material, especially DNA from different biological species, and to introduce the resulting hybrid DNA into an organism in order to form new combinations of heritable genetic material.

Concept note-5: -Gene editing, based on a technology known as CRISPR-Cas9, allows researchers to customize a living organism’s genetic sequence by making very specific changes to its DNA.
