This is the process of creating a unique image of an individual’s alleles based on their size and using that image to identify an individual’s DNA

(A) Cloning

(B) Gel Electrophoresis

(C) Genetic Engineering

(D) ** DNA Fingerprinting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DNA fingerprinting (also called DNA profiling or forensic genetics) is a technique employed by forensic scientists to assist in the identification of individuals or samples by their respective DNA profiles.

Concept note-2: -DNA fingerprinting is a method used to identify an individual from a sample of DNA by looking at unique patterns in their DNA.

Concept note-3: -Genetic engineering refers to the direct manipulation of DNA to alter an organism’s characteristics (phenotype) in a particular way.

Concept note-4: -Recombinant DNA: DNA which has been altered from its original form by joining genetic material from two different sources. It usually involves putting a gene or part of a gene obtained from one organism into the genome of a different organism.

Concept note-5: -Genetic Engineering This may involve changing a single base pair (A-T or C-G), deleting a region of DNA or adding a new segment of DNA. For example, genetic engineering may involve adding a gene from one species to an organism from a different species to produce a desired trait.
