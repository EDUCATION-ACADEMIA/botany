Which choice describes a chromosome in a prokaryotic cell?

(A) linear in the nucleus

(B) circular in the nucleus

(C) linear in the cytoplasm

(D) ** circular in the cytoplasm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Instead, the chromosome of a prokaryote is found in a part of the cytoplasm called a nucleoid. Prokaryotes generally have a single circular chromosome that occupies a region of the cytoplasm called a nucleoid.

Concept note-2: -Prokaryotic chromosomes are found in the nucleoid of prokaryotic cells, and they are circular in shape. Unlike eukaryotic cells, prokaryotic cells donâ€™t have a membrane-bound nucleus. Instead, their genetic material can be found in a region of the cytoplasm called the nucleoid.

Concept note-3: -Prokaryotic cells typically have a single, circular chromosome located in the nucleoid. Since prokaryotic cells typically have only a single, circular chromosome, they can replicate faster than eukaryotic cells. In fact, a prokaryotic cell can undergo two rounds of DNA replication before the cell, itself, has divided.

Concept note-4: -In some organisms, like humans, chromosomes are linear, but in other organisms, like bacteria, chromosomes are typically circular. In prokaryotes, the circular chromosome is contained in the cytoplasm in an area called the nucleoid.

Concept note-5: -Many prokaryotes also carry small, circular DNA molecules called plasmids, which are distinct from the chromosomal DNA and can provide genetic advantages in specific environments.
