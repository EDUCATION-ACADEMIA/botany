Biobased energy companies want to replace fossil fuels with biofuels like ethanol made from

(A) Seaweed.

(B) ** Corn.

(C) Viruses.

(D) Rain water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Replacing fossil fuels with biofuels has the potential to generate a number of benefits. In contrast to fossil fuels, which are exhaustible resources, biofuels are produced from renewable feedstocks. Thus, their production and use could, in theory, be sustained indefinitely.

Concept note-2: -Biofuels help reduce the carbon footprint of transportation and other industries, by making the most of our planetâ€™s carbon cycle. Every gallon of biofuel that replaces a gallon of fossil fuel helps reduce greenhouse-gas emissions.

Concept note-3: -Corn grain makes a good biofuel feedstock due to its starch content and relative easy conversion to ethanol. Infrastructure to plant, harvest and store corn in mass quantities benefits the corn ethanol industry.

Concept note-4: -Corn grain makes a good biofuel feedstock due to its starch content and its comparatively easy conversion to ethanol. Infrastructure to plant, harvest, and store corn in mass quantities benefits the corn ethanol industry.
