What is the benefit of geneticially modified organisms?

(A) ** They may be better looking or more productive/effective

(B) They are easily reproduced

(C) They are higher in population

(D) They live longer/stronger

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Increased supply of food with reduced cost and longer shelf life. Faster growing plants and animals. Food with more desirable traits, such as potatoes that produce less of a cancer-causing substance when fried. Medicinal foods that could be used as vaccines or other medicines.

Concept note-2: -Some benefits of genetic engineering in agriculture are increased crop yields, reduced costs for food or drug production, reduced need for pesticides, enhanced nutrient composition and food quality, resistance to pests and disease, greater food security, and medical benefits to the worldâ€™s growing population.

Concept note-3: -Genetically modified organisms (GMOs) provide certain advantages to producers and consumers. Modified plants, for example, can at least initially help protect crops by providing resistance to a specific disease or insect, ensuring greater food production. GMOs are also important sources of medicine.

Concept note-4: -Advantages of GMOs: i tolerant against abiotic stresses cold drought salt heat. ii reduces reliance on chemical pesticides. iii reduces post-harvest losses. iv increase efficiency of mineral usage by plants.
