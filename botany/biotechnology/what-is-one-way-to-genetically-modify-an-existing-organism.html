What is one way to genetically modify an existing organism?

(A) ** by adding genes to its DNA from another organism

(B) by modifying the genes of a similar organism

(C) by removing all of its genes

(D) by making it mutate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A transgenic, or genetically modified, organism is one that has been altered through recombinant DNA technology, which involves either the combining of DNA from different genomes or the insertion of foreign DNA into a genome.

Concept note-2: -GM is a technology that involves inserting DNA into the genome of an organism. To produce a GM plant, new DNA is transferred into plant cells. Usually, the cells are then grown in tissue culture where they develop into plants. The seeds produced by these plants will inherit the new DNA.

Concept note-3: -Recombinant DNA: DNA which has been altered from its original form by joining genetic material from two different sources. It usually involves putting a gene or part of a gene obtained from one organism into the genome of a different organism.

Concept note-4: -Genetic engineering is the modification of an organism’s phenotype by manipulating its genetic material. Some genetic engineering uses the principle of recombination. Recombination is the process through which a new gene is inserted into a bacterial DNA “The plasmid".
