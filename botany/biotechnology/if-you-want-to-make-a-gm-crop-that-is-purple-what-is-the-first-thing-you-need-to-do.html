If you want to make a GM crop that is purple, what is the first thing you need to do?

(A) ** Find a plant that is naturally purple

(B) Paint a plant purple

(C) Use an enzyme to cut a gene out of a plant

(D) Use a virus to insert a gene into a plant

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To engineer the purple tomatoes, the scientists used transcription factors from snapdragons to trigger the tomatoes to produce more anthocyanin, creating a vibrant purple color. Martin and her colleagues published the first results of their research in 2008 in an article in Nature Biotechnology.

Concept note-2: -The first stage in making a GM plant requires transfer of DNA into a plant cell. One of the methods used to transfer DNA is to coat the surface of small metal particles with the relevant DNA fragment, and bombard the particles into the plant cells. Another method is to use a bacterium or virus.

Concept note-3: -Two testing methods are considered to be the most effective for detecting GMOs: DNA-based and protein-based methods. DNA-based GMO testing analyzes the genome of a plant to identify the presence of a genetic modification. EnviroLogix uses a patented technology called DNAble to perform DNA-based detection of GMOs.

Concept note-4: -GM crops were first introduced in the USA in 1994 with the Flavr Savr tomato, which had been genetically modified to slow its ripening process, delaying softening and rotting.
