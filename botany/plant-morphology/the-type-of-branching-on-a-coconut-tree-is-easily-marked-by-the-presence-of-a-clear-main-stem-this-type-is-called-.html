The type of branching on a coconut tree is easily marked by the presence of a clear main stem. This type is called ____

(A) dicots

(B) ** monopodial

(C) simpodial

(D) bipedal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Monopodial branching occurs when the terminal bud continues to grow as a central leader shoot and the lateral branches remain subordinate-e.g., beech trees (Fagus; Fagaceae). Concept note-2: -Monopodial branching is when the buds do not degrade and all the shoots continue to grow. Sympodial branching is when the terminal buds do degrade (make FU and/or die out) and the lateral shoot closest to the terminal bud now becomes the terminal shoot and continues the vertical growth.

Concept note-3: -There are two major types of shoot branching: lateral (axillary), which involves the formation of a primordial bud in the organogenic zone of the apex, and terminal (dichotomous), which is an outcome of the meristem bifurcation.

Concept note-4: -D) Deliquescent means branching so that the stem is lost in branches, as in most of the deciduous trees.
