If you find a plant that has a large diameter stem that grows tall, as a biologist you can do the basic hypothesis that the plant is experiencing growth____ From the surface characteristics of the stem, it is most likely in the form of____

(A) seconds; pilosus

(B) ** secondary; sulcatus

(C) primer; pilosus

(D) primer furrowed

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1: Primary and secondary growth: In woody plants, primary growth is followed by secondary growth, which allows the plant stem to increase in thickness or girth.

Concept note-2: -Grafting and budding This method involves joining a stem piece (as in grafting) or a single bud (as in budding) onto the stem of a plant that has roots. The stem piece or bud is called the scion, and the plant with roots is called the rootstock.

Concept note-3: -The key to plant growth is meristem, a type of plant tissue consisting of undifferentiated cells that can continue to divide and differentiate. Meristem allows plant stems and roots to grow longer (primary growth) and wider (secondary growth).

Concept note-4: -The vascular cambium and cork cambium are secondary meristems that are formed in stems and roots after the tissues of the primary plant body have differentiated. The vascular cambium is responsible for increasing the diameter of stems and roots and for forming woody tissue.
