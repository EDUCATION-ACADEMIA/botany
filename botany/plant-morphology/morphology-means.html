EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -morphology, in biology, the study of the size, shape, and structure of animals, plants, and microorganisms and of the relationships of their constituent parts. The term refers to the general aspects of biological form and arrangement of the parts of a plant or an animal.

Concept note-2: -Morphology, that is, the study of form comprising shape, size, and structure, is important for materials research in general. For nanostructured materials, popularly known as nanomaterials, morphology has special significance since form, in this case, dictates physical and chemical properties [1, 2].

Concept note-3: -Morphology – the internal structure of words Morphology is the study of the internal structure of words and forms a core part of linguistic study today. The term morphology is Greek and is a makeup of morph-meaning ‘shape, form’, and-ology which means ‘the study of something’.

Concept note-4: -Morphology is the study of tissue and organs, physics affects the body and animals. It is the study of how the organism adjusts to their environment and regulates the critical function of tissue, system and cellular molecule. Focusing on the external appearance of an organism.
