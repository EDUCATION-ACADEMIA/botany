The type of flower where the gynoecium occupies the highest position while the other parts are situated below it.

(A) ** Hypogynous

(B) perigynous

(C) epigynous

(D) monogynous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the gynoecium occupies the highest position in comparison to the other parts of the flower, then such kind of flower is described as hypogynous. The ovary is superior (having flower parts attached below the ovary).

Concept note-2: -In hypogynous flowers, the gynoecium is situated at the top whereas all the other parts are below it. Examples are Mustard, China Rose, Brinjal etc. In perigynous flowers, a hypanthium is attached to the receptacle below the gynoecium and it also surrounds the ovary.

Concept note-3: -In hypogynous flowers, the perianth and stamens are attached to the receptacle below the gynoecium; the ovary is superior to these organs, and the remaining floral organs arise from below the point of origin of the carpel.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct answer is ‘Hypogynous flower’.

Concept note-5: -In hypogynous flowers, ovary will be superior, gynoecium occupies the highest position and other floral parts are situated below it.
