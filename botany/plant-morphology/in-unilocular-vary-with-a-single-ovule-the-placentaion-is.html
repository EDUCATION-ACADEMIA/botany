In unilocular vary with a single ovule, the placentaion is

(A) marginal

(B) ** basal

(C) free central

(D) axile

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In Basal placentation, the ovary is unilocular. It bears a single placenta positioned at the base of the ovary and a single ovule is attached to it. The pistil or carpel may be monocarpellary or syncarpous.

Concept note-2: -Unilocular ovaries with single ovule show basal or apical placentation.

Concept note-3: -Hence, the correct answer is ‘Unilocular’

Concept note-4: -In basal placentation, the placenta develops at the base of the ovary and a single ovule is formed. This kind of placentation is seen in sunflowers and marigolds. Thus the correct answer is option D.

Concept note-5: -In the Basal Placentation, the placenta gets developed on the base of the ovaries in which the single ovule has been attached. This can be seen in the marigold flowers in their unilocular ovaries. The ovules which are present inside the ovaries of the flowers are joined to the ovary with the funiculi.
