____ refers to the pattern of arrangement of leaves on the stem or the branch.Venation Venation Inflorescence Inflorescence Phyllotaxy Phyllotaxy Taxonomy Taxonomy

(A) Venation

(B) Inflorescence

(C) ** Phyllotaxy

(D) Taxonomy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Phyllotaxy. Key Points. The arrangement of leaves on stems or their branches is known as Phyllotaxy. Phyllotaxy can be classified as either alternate, spiral, opposite, or whorled.

Concept note-2: -Phyllotaxy is the pattern of arrangement of leaves on the stem. It is of three types. Alternate: A single leaf arise at each node. E.g. China rose, sunflower, etc. Opposite: A pair of leaves arise at each node, opposite to each other.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct option is ‘Phyllotaxy’.

Concept note-4: -The way in which the leaves are arranged on a stem is called phyllotaxy. When only one leaf arises from a node, and leaves are borne along the stem alternately, it is called alternate phyllotaxy. When two leaves arise from one node, opposite to each other it is called opposite phyllotaxy.

Concept note-5: -Phyllotaxy is the term used to describe the arrangement of leaves around the stem. The proper arrangement of leaves is important for providing sunlight and photosynthesis. Leaves arise from the node of the stem.
