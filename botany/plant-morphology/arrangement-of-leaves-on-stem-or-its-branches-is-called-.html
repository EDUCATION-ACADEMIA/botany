Arrangement of leaves on stem or its branches is called-

(A) ** Phyllotaxy

(B) Venation

(C) Vernation

(D) Heterophylly

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct option is B Phyllotaxy. The arrangement of leaves on a stem is known as phyllotaxy. Based on the phyllotaxy, leaves can be classified as either alternate, opposite, or whorled.

Concept note-2: -Phyllotaxy is the arrangement of leaves around the stem. One leaf attached per node; leaves may be spirally arranged around the stem (left) or distichous–arranged in two opposite ranks (right). Two leaves are borne at each node on opposite sides of the stem. More than two leaves are at each node.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct option is ‘Phyllotaxy’.

Concept note-4: -Phyllotaxy refers to the pattern or arrangement of leaves on the stem or branch of a plant. It is of three types, alternate, opposite, and whorled phyllotaxy. In alternate phyllotaxy, a single leaf arises from the node of a branch. This type of phyllotaxy is observed in the sunflower, mustard, and peepal.

Concept note-5: -Alternate, whorled, and opposite are the three kinds of phyllotaxy.
