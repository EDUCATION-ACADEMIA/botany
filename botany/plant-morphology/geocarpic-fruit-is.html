Geocarpic fruit is

(A) potato

(B) ** groundnut

(C) onion

(D) garlic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Geocarpy is a very rare type of plant reproduction. These plants produce diaspores within the soil. Groundnut is the best example of geocarpic fruit.

Concept note-2: -Geocarpic fruit is a peanut. Geocarpic means producing or ripening the fruit beneath the surface of the ground like the peanuts. So the correct answer is ‘Pea nut’.

Concept note-3: -The best-known example of Geocarpy is the peanut, that is, Arachis hypogaea. So, the correct answer is ‘Groundnut’. Note: For reproduction, geocarpic plants bend their stems so that the fruit can be embedded in the soil but the fruit remains attached to the plant.

Concept note-4: -Hint: The groundnut or peanut is a false fruit that grows below the surface of the ground. This means that when you look at the plant you will not be able to see the fruits as it is present underground.

Concept note-5: -Geocarpy is most frequent in tropical or semi-desert areas, and geocarpic species may be found in the families Araceae, Begoniaceae, Brassicaceae (Cruciferae), Callitrichaceae, Convolvulaceae, Cucurbitaceae, Fabaceae (Leguminosae), Loganiaceae, Moraceae and Rubiaceae.
