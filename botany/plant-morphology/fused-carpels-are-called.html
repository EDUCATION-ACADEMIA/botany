fused carpels are called

(A) ** syncarpous

(B) polycarpous

(C) apocarpous

(D) acarpous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -∙ Gynoecium with fused carpels is called syncarpous. ∙ Syncarpous term is used for flower in reference to the fused carpels or pistils present in the gynoecium of that flower. The flowers of mango, tomato, mustard, coconut, etc possess syncarpous ovaries.

Concept note-2: -Final answer: When the carpels are fused, the gynoecium is called syncarpous.

Concept note-3: -The ovary of the flower which contains more than one carpel, where carpels are free is known as the apocarpous ovary, for example-in the rose flower. Syncarpous ovary: The ovary of the flower which contains more than one carpel and carpels are fused is known as the syncarpous ovary, for example-in the mustard flower.

Concept note-4: -The carpels may be “fused” entirely, except for retaining separate stigmas. Sometimes (e.g., Apocynaceae) carpels are fused by their styles or stigmas but possess distinct ovaries. In a syncarpous gynoecium, the “fused” ovaries of the constituent carpels may be referred to collectively as a single compound ovary.

Concept note-5: -Syncarpous ovaries are a type of compound ovaries in which the carpels of the gynoecium are united with each other. There are several carpels in flowers with syncarpous ovaries. These carpels, however, are united. Blooms having syncarpous ovaries include tomato and mustard flowers.
