‘Fairchild’ is the first artificial hybrid of

(A) ** Carnations and Sweet William

(B) Daisy and Rose

(C) Sunflower and Rose

(D) Rose and Chrysanthemum

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1716-17 he was the first person to scientifically produce an artificial hybrid, Dianthus Caryophyllus barbatus, known as “Fairchild’s Mule", a cross between a Sweet William (Dianthus barbatus) and a Carnation pink.

Concept note-2: -Thomas Fairchild was the first person to scientifically produce an artificial hybrid in 1717. He successfully crossbred a carnation pink (Dianthus caryophyllus) with a Sweet William (Dianthus barbatus), and the resulting plant Dianthus caryophyllus barbatus or Fairchild’s Mule was obtained.

Concept note-3: -Let me introduce you to the Fairchild Mule (also known as Dianthus caryophyllus barbatus). It was first introduced to the world in 1717 by Thomas Fairchild, a cross between a sweet william and a carnation. It’s also the first artificial hybrid plant to have been created.

Concept note-4: -Thomas Fairchild is generally credited with having produced the first artificial plant hybrid, about 1716.

Concept note-5: -So, the correct answer is ‘Thomas Fairchild’.
