Polyploidy is induced through

(A) Irradiation

(B) Mutagenic chemicals

(C) Ethylene

(D) ** Colchicine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As microtubules function in chromosome segregation, colchicine induces polyploidy by preventing the segregation of chromosomes during meiosis that results into half of the gametes (sex cells) containing double the chromosome number than usual.

Concept note-2: -Polyploidy can be artificially induced through interspecific hybridization, in vitro endosperm culture or somatic cell doubling through colchicine [21]. There are two different ways to induce polyploidy artificially: (i) meiotic (sexual) or (ii) mitotic (somatic).

Concept note-3: -Irradiation, mutagenic substances and ethylene do not induce polyploidy. Thus, the correct answer is option A.

Concept note-4: -Colchicine is a toxic chemical that is often used to induce polyploidy in plants. Basically, the colchicine prevents the microtubule formation during cell division, thus the chromosomes do not pull apart like they normally do.

Concept note-5: -Our results revealed that colchicine restrained the microtubules and inhibited gene expression of cytokinesis, which might slow down the cell activity, delay the cell into anaerobic respiration, resulting in apoptosis at late stage, and relieving of waterlogging.
