Sonalika and kalyan sona are high yielding varieties of

(A) Rice

(B) ** Wheat

(C) Maize

(D) Sugar cane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sonalika and Kalyan Sona are the high-yielding and disease-resistant varieties of wheat which were introduced all over the wheat-growing belt of India in 1963. Sonalika and Kalyan Sona are the semi-dwarf varieties of wheat, popularly known as Mexican wheat, produced by Dr. Borlaug in Mexico in 1963.

Concept note-2: -Though there are various types of high yielding seeds found in India, Sonalika, and Kalyan Sona are the two examples of wheat varieties introduced in India which are high yielding and disease resistant.

Concept note-3: -Sonalika and kalyan sona are considered as varieties of wheat crop because sonalika is single dwarf whereas kalyan sona is double dwarf.

Concept note-4: -Sonalika and Kalyan Sona are varieties of wheat. Sonalika and Kalyan Sona both are resistant to rusts and has a wide range of adaptation. It is very suitable for late sowings and has good grains. As Kalyan Sona is susceptible to rust hence it should be planted early.

Concept note-5: -Sonalika :-This variety of wheat is suitable for delayed sowing. The land can be sown after preparing the paddy. It yields 30 to 35 quintals per hectare.
