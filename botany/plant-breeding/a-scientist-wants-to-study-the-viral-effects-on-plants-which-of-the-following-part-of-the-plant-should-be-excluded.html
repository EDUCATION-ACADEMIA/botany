A scientist wants to study the viral effects on plants. Which of the following part of the plant should be excluded?

(A) pith

(B) ** shoot apex

(C) phloem

(D) cortex

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: A scientist who wishes to study the viral effect on plants should exclude the Shoot apex.

Concept note-2: -Thus shoot tips or axillary buds can be used as explants to raise virus free plants via tissue culture. Such a culture using explants containing meristems is known as meristem culture.

Concept note-3: -Meristem culture technique is widely used for obtaining virus-free plants.

Concept note-4: -What are plant viruses? Pathogenic plant viruses can have a significant negative impact on plants by causing a wide range of symptoms including discolouration, distortion and loss of vigour and yield. With very few control options, virus infection in garden plants can be a nuisance for gardeners.

Concept note-5: -Symptoms. Because viruses are microscopic, the presence of a virus is only noticed if it produces recognizable symptoms in the organism it is infecting. In plants these symptoms include mosaic patterns, flower-break, deformed growth, chlorosis or yellowing, stunting and leaf distortion, ringspots, and vein clearing.
