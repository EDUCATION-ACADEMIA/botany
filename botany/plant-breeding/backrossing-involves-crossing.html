Backrossing involves crossing

(A) Parent and parent

(B) Hybrid and hybrid

(C) ** Parent and hybrid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Backcrossing is a crossing of a hybrid with one of its parents or an individual genetically similar to its parent, to achieve offspring with a genetic identity closer to that of the parent. It is used in horticulture, animal breeding, and production of gene knockout organisms.

Concept note-2: -Backcross breeding enables breeders to transfer a desired trait such as a transgene from one variety (donor parent, DP) into the favored genetic background of another (recurrent parent, RP). If the trait of interest is produced by a dominant gene, this process involves four rounds of backcrossing within seven seasons.

Concept note-3: -backcross, the mating of a hybrid organism (offspring of genetically unlike parents) with one of its parents or with an organism genetically similar to the parent. The backcross is useful in genetics studies for isolating (separating out) certain characteristics in a related group of animals or plants.

Concept note-4: -Donor parent is used only once and hence known as ‘non-recurrent parent’.

Concept note-5: -This is called selective backcrossing. This technique allows breeders to introduce new traits into their crops, thereby improving crop quality and providing a variety of options for farmers.
