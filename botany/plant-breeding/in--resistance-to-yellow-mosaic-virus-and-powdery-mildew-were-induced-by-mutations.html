In ____, resistance to yellow mosaic virus and powdery mildew were induced by mutations

(A) ** Mung bean

(B) Brassica

(C) Cauliflower

(D) Wheat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The mung bean varieties, resistant to yellow mosaic virus and powdery mildew were developed by induced mutations. Powdery mildew is a fungal disease that affects many plants and the fungus grows well in humid environments. Mungbean yellow mosaic virus is a plant pathogenic virus.

Concept note-2: -Option (2) is correct. This is because – In mung bean, resistance to yellow mosaic virus and powdery mildew were induced by mutations.

Concept note-3: -How has mutation breeding helped in improving the production of mung bean crop ? Mutational breeding was used to introduce resistance to yellow mosaic virus and powdery mildew in the mung bean crop thus improving the production of the crop.

Concept note-4: -Complete answer: Parbhani Kranti is the name of a lady’s finger variety scientifically known as Abelmoschus esculentus. It is resistant to yellow mosaic virus. The resistance to the yellow mosaic virus was transferred from another wild species known as Abelmoschus manihot land.
