Who discovered the unit factors of inheritance by doing series of plant hybridisation experiments in 1866?

(A) John Hopkins

(B) Muller

(C) ** Mendel

(D) Hardy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"Experiments in Plant Hybridization” (1866), by Johann Gregor Mendel. During the mid-nineteenth century, Johann Gregor Mendel experimented with pea plants to develop a theory of inheritance.

Concept note-2: -In 1866, Mendel published the paper Experiments in plant hybridisation (Versuche über plflanzenhybriden). In it, he proposed that heredity is the result of each parent passing along 1 factor for every trait. If the factor is dominant, it will be expressed in the progeny.

Concept note-3: -Johann Gregor Mendel studied plants and their patterns of inheritance in Austria during the nineteenth century. Mendel experimented with the pea plant, Pisum, and his publication, “Versuche über Pflanzenhybriden” (“Experiments on Plant Hybridization”), published in 1866, revolutionized theories of trait inheritance.

Concept note-4: -Gregor Mendel, through his work on pea plants, discovered the fundamental laws of inheritance.

Concept note-5: -Mendel conducted hybridization experiments on garden pea (Pisum sativum) for eight years (1856-1863). One reason behind choosing pea as a model plant for the experiment was that many varieties are available with distinct and visible characteristics.
