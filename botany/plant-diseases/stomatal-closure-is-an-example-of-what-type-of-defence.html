Stomatal closure is an example of what type of defence?

(A) ** Physical

(B) Passive Chemical

(C) Active Chemical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The opening and closing of stomata is regulated by the integration of environmental signals and endogenous hormonal stimuli. The various different factors to which the guard cells respond translates into the complexity of the network of signaling pathways that control stomatal movements.

Concept note-2: -Abscisic acid (ABA) is a stress hormone that accumulates under different abiotic and biotic stresses. A typical effect of ABA on leaves is to reduce transpirational water loss by closing stomata and parallelly defend against microbes by restricting their entry through stomatal pores.

Concept note-3: -Stomatal closure is a common adaptation response of plants to the onset of drought condition. Stomata are microscopic pores on the leaf epidermis, which regulate the transpiration/CO2 uptake by leaves.

Concept note-4: -ABA is a key hormone that regulates water status and stomatal movement. Under drought conditions, plants produce and accumulate increased amounts of ABA in the guard cells, and this induces stomatal closure to conserve water.
