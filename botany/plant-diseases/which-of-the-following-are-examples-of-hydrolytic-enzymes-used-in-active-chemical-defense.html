Which of the following are examples of hydrolytic enzymes, used in active chemical defense?

(A) Cellulase

(B) ** Chitinase

(C) Glucogenase

(D) ** Glucanase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Examples of Hydrolytic Enzymes E.g. pancreatic lipase, lysosomal lipase. Pancreatic lipase converts triglycerides to monoglycerides and two fatty acids. Lysosomal lipase digests lipids and cholesterol to free fatty acids. Amylase – Amylase is a type of glycoside hydrolases.

Concept note-2: -The activity of six hydrolytic enzymes-carboxyl esterase, acid phosphatase, alkaline phosphatase, -galactosidase, -glucosidase and -hexosaminidase, were studied in different regions of the normal human brain tissue obtained at autopsy.

Concept note-3: -The main function of glucanase is to catalyze the hydrolysis of glycosidic bonds in polysaccharides. This function is not highly specific, and the enzymes distinguish among substrates mostly by the types of bonds present and -or -configuration.

Concept note-4: -Beta Glucanase is an enzyme that hydrolyzes -glucans. The most important -glucanases for brewing are those that break down the -glucans located in the cell walls of the barley endosperm.
