How do insects damage a plant?

(A) They reduce the area for photosynthesis.

(B) They destroy cells.

(C) ** They act as a vector, transferring viruses, bacteria and fungi between the plants.

(D) They prevent plants from acquiring the water and minerals it needs.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Insects that vector viruses are mainly hemipteran sap-feeders for plants and mainly dipteran blood-feeders (primarily mosquitoes) for vertebrates. In both cases the virus is acquired during feeding on the non-insect host and then is transferred to a naïve non-insect host again via feeding.

Concept note-2: -Generally, insect pests attack the plants in three ways: (i) they cut the root, stem, and leaf, (ii) they suck the cell sap from various parts of the plant, and (iii) they bore into stem and fruits. They thus affect the health of the crop and reduce yields.

Concept note-3: -Most insect vectors of plant pathogens are hemipteran insects, e.g., aphids, whiteflies, and psyllids, that have piercing sucking mouthparts in common.

Concept note-4: -Another important group of insect vectors found worldwide is the order Thysanoptera, the thrips. Other insects also spread plant diseases; however, aphids alone are responsible for spreading the majority of known plant viral diseases, followed closely by the leafhoppers, whiteflies, and thrips.
