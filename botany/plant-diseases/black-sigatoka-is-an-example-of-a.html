EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Black Sigatoka is caused by the plant-pathogenic fungus Mycosphaerella fijiensis, whereas yellow Sigatoka is caused by M. musicola (Bennett and Arneson 2003). Black Sigatoka or black leaf streak (BLS) is the most important fungal disease of banana (Musa species) worldwide.

Concept note-2: -Black Sigatoka is a foliar disease of banana caused by the fungus Pseudocercospora fijiensis.

Concept note-3: -Mycosphaerella fijiensis (black Sigatoka)

Concept note-4: -Black Sigatoka, also known as black leaf streak, is caused by Mycosphaerella fijiensis Morelet (anamorph Pseudocercospora fijiensis (Morelet) Deighton). It is the most significant disease of bananas and plantains (Musa spp.)

Concept note-5: -Black Sigatoka is caused by the Ascomycete fungus Pseudocercospora fijiensis (formerly Mycosphaerella fijiensis). P. fijiensis spreads via aerial spores, infecting banana leaves via the stomata and causing characteristic streaked lesions and cell death when fungal toxins are exposed to light [15].
