Some glucose is used by plants to produce Cellulose, this is used to

(A) build cell membranes

(B) ** strengthen cell walls

(C) make the nucleus

(D) make ribosomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cellulose molecules provide tensile strength to the primary cell wall. Each molecule consists of a linear chain of at least 500 glucose residues that are covalently linked to one another to form a ribbonlike structure, which is stabilized by hydrogen bonds within the chain (Figure 19-70).

Concept note-2: -Molecular Breeding of Woody Plants Cellulose is synthesized by the enzyme cellulose synthase, a membrane protein that catalyzes the direct polymerization of glucose from the substrate UDP-glucose into a cellulose product.

Concept note-3: -Cellulose: Beta glucose is the monomer unit in cellulose. As a result of the bond angles in the beta acetal linkage, cellulose is mostly a linear chain. Starch: Alpha glucose is the monomer unit in starch.

Concept note-4: -The cell wall is the tough layer that surrounds plant cells. Glucose is converted into cellulose for making cell walls, especially in a rapidly growing plant.

Concept note-5: -During photosynthesis, plants trap light energy with their leaves. Plants use the energy of the sun to change water and carbon dioxide into a sugar called glucose. Glucose is used by plants for energy and to make other substances like cellulose and starch. Cellulose is used in building cell walls.
