PLANT DISEASES

[SOURCES]
Viral diseases can be transferred from plant to plant by:

(A) vegetative propagation

(B) mites, nematodes, and thrips

(C) hands and tools

(D) ** all of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All viruses that spread within their host tissues (systemically) can be transmitted by grafting branches or buds from diseased plants on healthy plants. Natural grafting and transmission are possible by root grafts and with parasitic dodder (Cuscuta species). Vegetative propagation often spreads plant viruses.

Concept note-2: -While the spread of animal viruses is most often associated with direct contact or proximity to infected individuals, plant viruses are transmitted through wounds on the plant or via a vector, most often insects, fungi and nematodes that feed or infect the plant [3].

Concept note-3: -The answer is simply, no. Since plants are not a susceptible host for the virus that gives us the flu. VIruses are incredibly host specific, so we can’t get sick with a plant virus and plants can’t get sick with an animal virus.

Concept note-4: -Plant viruses can be transmitted by very diverse ways. Transmission can occur through abiotic means such as soil or water, or mechanically via grafting or contacts during the agricultural practices, or through seeds, pollen.
