EPIGENETICS

[SOURCES]
Does a skin cell looks different from a brain cell or a muscle cell?

(A) Yes, because of the function

(B) ** Yes, due to Epigenetics

(C) No, due to Epigenetics

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Epigenetic mechanisms directly regulate skin homeostasis and regeneration, but they also mark cell senescence and the natural and pathological aging processes.

Concept note-2: -These cells are different because they use the same set of genes differently. So even though each of our cells has the same 20, 000 or so genes, each cell can select which ones it wants to “turn on” and which ones it wants to keep “turned off”.

Concept note-3: -We learned in biology class that every cell in the body has the same DNA. Whether a heart cell, skin cell or muscle cell-they all read from the same genetic blueprint.
