External factors include

(A) Gender, temperature, metabolism

(B) Metabolism, gender, hormones

(C) ** Temperature, light, drugs

(D) Light, metabolism, hormones

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Air, water, climate, soil, natural vegetation and landforms are all environmental factors. By definition, the environmental factors affect everyday living, and play a key role in bringing health differences across the geographic areas.

Concept note-2: -Solution. The environment is affected by biotic and abiotic factors such as temperature, pressure, humidity, and organisms like human activity.

Concept note-3: -Environmental factors include temperature, food, pollutants, population density, sound, light, and parasites.

Concept note-4: -Internal factors that affect gene expression include hormones, gender and metabolic activities. External factors include temperature, light and other chemicals which might be taken with food.
