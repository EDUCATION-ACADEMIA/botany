EPIGENETICS

[SOURCES]
Which of these are true of epigenetics?

(A) ** Results in changes to how tightly packaged the DNA is

(B) ** Results in changes to how much of a particular protein is made

(C) Results in changes to the base sequence

(D) Results in the destruction of mRNA

(E) ** Changes can be passed down to offspring

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Germline transmission of epigenetic information requires that the epigenetic marks be first established in the germ cells of the parent generation, maintained through meiosis, then passed to the offspring.

Concept note-2: -Epigenetic changes affect what genes are turned on and off. Epigenetic changes don’t change the DNA code, just what genes are turned on and off. These changes can be passed down in families.

Concept note-3: -Which of the following is true about the effects of epigenetics on gene expression? Explanation: Methylation and acetylation of histones occurs on lysine residues, thereby decreasing or increasing gene expression, respectively.

Concept note-4: -Environmental influences, such as a person’s diet and exposure to pollutants, can impact the epigenome. Epigenetic modifications can be maintained from cell to cell as cells divide and, in some cases, can be inherited through the generations.
