What was the name of the ship Charles Darwin sailed on?

(A) Boaty McBoaty Face

(B) ** HMS Beagle

(C) HMS Bagle

(D) HMS Bulldog

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Charles Darwin set sail on the ship HMS Beagle on December 27, 1831, from Plymouth, England.

Concept note-2: -Admiral John Lort Stokes was a naval officer who travelled on HMS Beagle for almost 18 years. On the second voyage of the Beagle, he was the assistant surveyor and he shared a cabin with Charles Darwin.

Concept note-3: -Eight vessels of the Royal Navy have been named HMS Beagle, after a dog breed. The most notable of these ships is the second HMS Beagle, 1820–1870, which transported Charles Darwin around the world in the voyage of Beagle.

Concept note-4: -Beagle was a Royal Navy ship, famed for taking English naturalist Charles Darwin on his first expedition around the world in 1831–36. Beagle was launched at Woolwich Dockyard, London, in 1820.

Concept note-5: -The purpose of the Beagle’s voyage was to survey the coast of South America. Charles Darwin was invited on board as the Captain’s Companion and naturalist. In his time aboard the Beagle, Darwin would describe and collect many new types of animals and plants.
