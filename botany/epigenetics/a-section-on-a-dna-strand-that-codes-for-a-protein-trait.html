A section on a DNA strand that codes for a protein (trait)

(A) ** gene

(B) protein

(C) nucleus

(D) chromosome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Genes are small sections of DNA within the genome that code for proteins. They contain the instructions for our individual characteristics – like eye and hair colour.

Concept note-2: -The sections of DNA (or RNA) that code for proteins are called exons.

Concept note-3: -The type of RNA that contains the information for making a protein is called messenger RNA (mRNA) because it carries the information, or message, from the DNA out of the nucleus into the cytoplasm.

Concept note-4: -The segments of DNA that code for specific traits are called genes.
