EPIGENETICS

[SOURCES]
Two traits caused by two separate genes on the same or different autosomal chromosome.

(A) ** dihybrid cross

(B) monohybrid cross

(C) recessive allele

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a dihybrid cross, the parents carry different pair of alleles for each trait. One parent carries homozygous dominant allele, while the other one carries homozygous recessive allele.

Concept note-2: -When the two genes in a dihybrid cross were situated on the same chromosome, the proportain of parental gene combinations were much higher than the non-parental type.

Concept note-3: -The F1 offspring of this cross were dihybrid plants of the genotype AaBb. Mendel performed a dihybrid cross and examined the phenotypes and genotypes of F2 plants. Mendel observed that each pair of traits was inherited independently.

Concept note-4: -A dihybrid cross describes a mating experiment between two organisms that are identically hybrid for two traits.
