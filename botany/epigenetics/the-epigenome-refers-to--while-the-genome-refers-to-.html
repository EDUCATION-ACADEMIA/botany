[SOURCES]
The epigenome refers to ____ while the genome refers to ____

(A) DNA; methyl and acetyl tags

(B) DNA; DNA

(C) Methyl tags; Acetyl tags

(D) ** Methyl and acetyl tags; DNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Epigenetic tags regulate gene expression by acting as gatekeepers, blocking or allowing access to a gene’s ‘on’ switch. These chemical tags (such as methyl or acetyl groups) are added directly to DNA or onto histones, the large spool-like proteins around which DNA is tightly wound.

Concept note-2: -A common type of epigenetic modification is called DNA methylation. DNA methylation involves the attachment of small chemical groups called methyl groups (each consisting of one carbon atom and three hydrogen atoms) to DNA building blocks.

Concept note-3: -Acetylation is the process of the addition of acetyl molecules, whereas methylation is the process of the addition of methyl groups to the DNA molecule.

Concept note-4: -In histone modification, methyl or acetyl tags attach to histones, the proteins wrapped together with the DNA structure. These chemical tags tighten or loosen the DNA coil, allowing different sections to be activated or repressed.
