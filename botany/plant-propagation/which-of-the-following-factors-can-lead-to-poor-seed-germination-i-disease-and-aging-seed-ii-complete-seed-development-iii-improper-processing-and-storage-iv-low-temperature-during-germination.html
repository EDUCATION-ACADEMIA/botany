Which of the following factors can lead to poor seed germination? I. Disease and aging seed. II. Complete seed development. III. Improper processing and storage. IV. Low temperature during germination.

(A) I, II and III

(B) ** I, III and IV

(C) II, III and IV

(D) All of the above.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abiotic factors such as drought, light, salinity, seed burial depth, soil pH, and temperature as well as disturbance events such as a fire, flooding or tillage can play an important role in initiating or inhibiting seed germination [3–4].

Concept note-2: -Environmental factors such as temperature, light, pH, and soil moisture are known to affect seed germination (Chachalis and Reddy 2000; Taylorson 1987).

Concept note-3: -So, the correct answer is ‘Light’.
