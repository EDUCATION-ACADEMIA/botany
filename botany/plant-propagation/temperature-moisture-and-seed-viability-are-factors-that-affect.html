Temperature, moisture and seed viability are factors that affect

(A) ** Germination Rates

(B) Hardwood cuttings

(C) Softwood cuttings

(D) Tree Grafts

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Environmental factors such as temperature, light, pH, and soil moisture are known to affect seed germination (Chachalis and Reddy 2000; Taylorson 1987). Burial depth of seed also affects seed germination and seedling emer-gence.

Concept note-2: -Temperature affects germination in three primary ways: moisture, hormone production, and enzyme activity. For seeds to germinate, they need to imbibe water. For this to occur, sufficient moisture must be present. A warmer climate may increase evaporation and decrease moisture, which would negatively affect germination.

Concept note-3: -seed burial depth and food reserves are the most important factors affecting seed germination. Salinity is one of the main problems of soil degradation. Osmotic and ionic effects occur due to salinity that inhibits plant growth15.

Concept note-4: -Moisture, temperature, and the proportion of oxygen are key environmental factors that affect seed deterioration and loss of viability.

Concept note-5: -Moisture essentially brings the seed back to life. When the seed fills with water in a process called imbibition, it activates enzymes to initiate the germination process. On the other hand, too much water can cause seeds to rot instead of developing into a seedling.
