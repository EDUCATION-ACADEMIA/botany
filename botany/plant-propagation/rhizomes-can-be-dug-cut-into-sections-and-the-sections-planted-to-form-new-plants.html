Rhizomes can be dug, cut into sections, and the sections planted to form new plants.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"They grow via rhizomes-swollen, underground stems which store food and moisture. They provide fuel for new shoots to grow and provide next yearsâ€™ flowers. To divide them, simply chop them up-you can even just break them up pretty easily with your hands. Choose sections with plenty of new shoots."

Concept note-2: -Unlike roots, rhizomes have nodes. The nodes of rhizomes send out roots into the soil to perform asexual reproduction by vegetative propagation. Often roots grow from the bottom part of the rhizome while shoots grow from the upper portion of the nodes.

Concept note-3: -Rhizomes are root-like stems that grow horizontally under the ground. New roots and shoots form at the nodes with shoots growing upwards to form new plantlets.

Concept note-4: -Rhizomes: Multi-layered stem system A multi-layered stem system refers to the formation of both roots and shoots from the nodes in several layers, forming a complex-looking plant. Unlike other plants containing a single layer of roots and shoots, the multi-layered rhizomes have different layers and whorls of leaves.
