What is the passage about?

(A) Using stem to propagate

(B) Growing plant by cutting

(C) The number of plant propagation

(D) ** Propagation by stem cutting and air layering

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A cutting is a small shoot or branch cut from a plant and placed in water, soil, or planting medium to root and form a new plant. Layering is bending and pegging the shoot of a living stem to the soil. The shoot takes root while still attached to the parent plant.

Concept note-2: -It involves taking a section of stem from a parent plant and manipulating it to create a new plant. Since the reproduction is asexual, the new plant is genetically identical to the parent and is often referred to as a clone.

Concept note-3: -Air layering is a method of propagating new trees and shrubs from stems still attached to the parent plant. The stem is wrapped with damp moss to encourage roots to form.

Concept note-4: -The only difference between air layering and taking cuttings is the stem is completely removed when you take a cutting. Propagation by cuttings is a better technique for smaller, younger stems as they often survive via capillary action that delivers water and nutrients to the plant.
