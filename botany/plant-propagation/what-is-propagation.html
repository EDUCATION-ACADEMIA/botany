PLANT PROPAGATION

[SOURCES]
What is propagation?

(A) To drop the number of plants

(B) To shrink the number of plants

(C) ** To increase the number of plants

(D) To decrease the number of plants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant propagation is the process of increasing the number of plants of a particular species or cultivar. There are two primary forms of plant propagation: sexual and asexual. In nature, propagation of plants most often involves sexual reproduction, or the production of viable seeds.

Concept note-2: -Asexual propagation of plants can also be called ‘vegetative propagation’ because it involves the use of vegetative parts of plants like leaves, stems, roots, or modified organs. It’s the best method to use to clone your plants, which means to produce plants identical to their parents.

Concept note-3: -Grafting and budding are standard techniques used to propagate new fruit cultivars. These techniques allow breeders to grow multiple plants of exactly the same genetic material (clones).

Concept note-4: -Asexual propagation, sometimes referred to as vegetative propagation, involves taking vegetative parts of a plant (stems, roots, and/or leaves) and causing them to regenerate into a new plant or, in some cases, several plants. With few exceptions, the resulting plant is genetically identical to the parent plant.
