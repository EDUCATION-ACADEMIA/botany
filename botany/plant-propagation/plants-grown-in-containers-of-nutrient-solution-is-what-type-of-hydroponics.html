Plants grown in containers of nutrient solution is what type of hydroponics?

(A) ** Static Solution Culture

(B) Medium Culture

(C) Irrigation Method Culture

(D) Aquaponocs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In static solution culture, plants are grown in containers of nutrient solution, such as glass Mason jars (typically, in-home applications), pots, buckets, tubs, or tanks.

Concept note-2: -hydroponics, also called aquaculture, nutriculture, soilless culture, or tank farming, the cultivation of plants in nutrient-enriched water, with or without the mechanical support of an inert medium such as sand, gravel, or perlite.

Concept note-3: -Growing plants in a nutrient solution in absence of soil is called hydroponics. This technique was pioneered by Julius Von Sachs. It is also called soilless cultivation. So, the correct answer is ‘Hydroponics’

Concept note-4: -There are six main types of hydroponic systems to consider for your garden: wicking, deep water culture (DWC), nutrient film technique (NFT), ebb and flow, aeroponics, and drip systems.
