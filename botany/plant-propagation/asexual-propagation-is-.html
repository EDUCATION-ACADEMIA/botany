PLANT PROPAGATION

[SOURCES]
Asexual propagation is ____

(A) reproduction of plants by seed

(B) propagating sexually

(C) ** reproduction of plants by using other plant parts

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Asexual propagation involves taking a part of one parent plant and causing it to regenerate itself into a new plant. The resulting new plant is genetically identical its parent. Asexual propagation involves the vegetative parts of a plant: stems, roots, or leaves.

Concept note-2: -“Asexual reproduction is the mode of reproduction that does not involve the fusion of male and female gametes and produces individuals that are genetically identical to the parent.”

Concept note-3: -There are two types of propagation, sexual and asexual. Sexual reproduction is the union of the pollen and egg, drawing from the genes of two parents to create a new, third individual. Sexual propagation involves the floral parts of a plant.
