Nutrient solution constantly flows past roots is what category of hydroponics?

(A) Static Solution Culture

(B) ** Medium Culture

(C) Aeroponics

(D) Aquaculture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A DWC consists of a reservoir filled with water and nutrient solution. The plants are suspended over the reservoir using a net pot and growing media. The roots themselves are submerged in the reservoir, so they have a constant supply of water and nutrients.

Concept note-2: -NFT Systems have a constant flow of nutrient solution, so no timer is required for the submersible pump (Figure 5). Figure 5. The NFT System. The Aeroponic System is probably the most high-tech type of hydroponic gardening.

Concept note-3: -Essentially, a Hydroponic nutrient solution is a liquid filled with all of the necessary nutrients so that plant roots can come into contact for its growth. Compared to soil, Hydroponic makes it easier to measure and fill the exact amounts of nutrients in the water solutions.

Concept note-4: -The Wick system is by far the simplest type of hydroponic system. This is a passive system, which means there are no moving parts. The nutrient solution is drawn into the growing medium from the reservoir with a wick.
