What is perlite used for?

(A) A good soil medium

(B) Absorption of water

(C) Improve drainage

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -While it does also help retain some water, perlite is primarily used to aerate compost. It excellent for creating a free-draining potting compost for plants that need good drainage, such as cacti and succulents. It can also help create an airy compost for seedlings.

Concept note-2: -: volcanic glass that has a concentric structure, appears as if composed of concretions, is usually grayish and sometimes spherulitic, and when heated expands to form a lightweight aggregate used especially in concrete and plaster and as a medium for potting plants. perlitic. ˌpər-ˈli-tik. adjective.

Concept note-3: -Perlite is a highly useful component of gardening for many reasons: It is physically stable and retains its shape, even in heavy or saturated soil. It doesn’t decompose, so it’s ideal for use in potting mixes for plants that are repotted infrequently (such as succulents and other houseplants).
