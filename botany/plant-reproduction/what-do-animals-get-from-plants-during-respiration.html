What do animals get from plants during respiration?

(A) ** Oxygen

(B) Carbon dioxide

(C) Water

(D) Minerals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Animals, during respiration, take in oxygen and release carbon dioxide gas. Plants, on the other hand, utilize this carbon dioxide gas in the process of photosynthesis to produce food and release oxygen in the atmosphere. Thus, we can say that plants and animals help each other in exchange of gases in the atmosphere.

Concept note-2: -Plants, like animals, take in oxygen and give out carbon dioxide during respiration. Only during photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen as a byproduct.

Concept note-3: -Yes, like animals and humans, plants also breathe. Plants do require oxygen to respire, the process in return gives out carbon dioxide.

Concept note-4: -The animals breathe in carbon dioxide and breathe out oxygen in the process of respiration.

Concept note-5: -The leftovers from making the plant food is another gas called oxygen. This oxygen is released from the leaves into the air. Take a look at the picture below to see this system in action. This leftover oxygen is really important because humans and other animals need this oxygen to live which we breathe in from the air.
