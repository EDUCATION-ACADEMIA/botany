A flower that is either male or female but not both.

(A) ** imperfect flower

(B) stamen

(C) perfect flower

(D) gymnosperm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Such a flower is called unisexual flowers. Unisexual flowers are the flowers that possese either the stamen or carpel, that is, it may be a male flower or female flower. They are also known as incomplete flowers. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-2: -A flower that possesses both male and female reproductive parts are called bisexual flowers. The male part is called androecium and the female part is called gynoecium. Hibiscus and lily are two common examples of bisexual flowers.

Concept note-3: -An imperfect flower is one that only has male or female parts. A complete flower contains sepals, petals, pistils, and stamens. An incomplete flower is missing one of those parts.

Concept note-4: -Flowers that lack at least one of the whorls of reproductive parts, male or female, are said to be imperfect (unisexual).

Concept note-5: -Lilies are an example of a perfect flower.
