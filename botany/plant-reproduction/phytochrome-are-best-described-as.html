Phytochrome are best described as:

(A) ** A pigment that is sensitive to light

(B) A pigment for inhibiting flowering

(C) A pigment for stimulating flowering

(D) A pigment that increases with light

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The phytochrome system acts as a biological light switch. It monitors the level, intensity, duration, and color of environmental light. The effect of red light is reversible by immediately shining far-red light on the sample, which converts the chromoprotein to the inactive Pr form.

Concept note-2: -Phytochrome is a photoreceptor, a pigment that plants, and some bacteria and fungi, use to detect light. It is sensitive to light in the red and far-red region of the visible spectrum.

Concept note-3: -Phytochrome is a blue-green pigment present in plants which is used to detect light. It act as a photoreceptor. It regulates various plant processes like seed germination, stem growth, pigment synthesis, flowering.

Concept note-4: -Phytochromes are a class of photoreceptors in plants, microorganisms and parasites used to distinguish light. They are delicate to light in the red and far-red locale of the obvious range and can be classed as either Type I, which are actuated by a wide margin red light, or Type II that are enacted by red light.
