The brightly colored parts of flowers that attract insects and birds.

(A) ** petals

(B) pistils

(C) stamen

(D) filaments

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Corolla consists of petals which are the coloured part of the flower and attract the insects for pollination.

Concept note-2: -Petals. The colorful, thin structures that surround the sexual parts of the flower. Not only attract pollinators, but also protect the pistil and stamen.

Concept note-3: -Petal is the brightly coloured part of the flower that attracts insects for pollination.

Concept note-4: -Corolla is the outermost layer of the flower and are brightly coloured which helps attract insects to them which in turn help in the process of pollination.

Concept note-5: -Flowers that are bright in color are meant to attract birds, bees and other insects in order to help the plants reproduce. Bright colors or dull colors are fixed in the genetics of a flower.
