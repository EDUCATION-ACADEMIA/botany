Seedless plants, such as mosses, have a generation that reproduces using

(A) ** spores.

(B) pollen.

(C) flowers

(D) cones

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sporophytes are the plant generation that produces spores. A haploid gametophyte phase follows a diploid sporophyte phase in the life cycle of seedless vascular plants.

Concept note-2: -Seedless vascular plants reproduce through unicellular, haploid spores instead of seeds; the lightweight spores allow for easy dispersion in the wind. Seedless vascular plants require water for sperm motility during reproduction and, thus, are often found in moist environments.

Concept note-3: -Sporangia in Seedless Plants The sporophyte bears the sporangia (singular, sporangium): organs that first appeared in the land plants. The term “sporangia” literally means “spore in a vessel”: it is a reproductive sac that contains spores.

Concept note-4: -Reproduction in Nonvascular Seedless Plants Like all plants, nonvascular plants have an alternation of generations life cycle. That means they alternate between diploid cell stages (having two sets of chromosomes) and haploid cell stages (having one set of chromosomes) during their life cycle.

Concept note-5: -After fertilization, the zygote matures and grows into a sporophyte, which in turn will form sporangia, or “spore vessels, ” in which mother cells undergo meiosis and produce haploid spores. The release of spores in a suitable environment will lead to germination and a new generation of gametophytes.
