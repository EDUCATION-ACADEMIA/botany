The period of time where the seed is under decreased metabolic activity because of its environment is called ____

(A) Germination

(B) Pollination

(C) Fertilization

(D) ** Dormancy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dormancy is defined as the temporary inability of a viable seed to germinate under favorable environmental conditions (Simpson, 1990). Dormancy helps the seed to survive adverse environmental conditions and at the same time helps in seed dispersal (Snape et al., 2001).

Concept note-2: -Seed dormancy has been defined as the incapacity of a viable seed to germinate under favorable conditions (Bewley, 1997; Finch-Savage and Leubner–Metzger, 2006).

Concept note-3: -Dormancy is a period in an organism’s life cycle when growth, development, and (in animals) physical activity are temporarily stopped. This minimizes metabolic activity and therefore helps an organism to conserve energy.

Concept note-4: -Dormancy refers to a period of time when the organism physically stops growing; this is typically done to conserve energy during the colder months of the year or to protect itself from adverse environmental conditions.

Concept note-5: -Dormancy has a significant role in the development of new species and the successful dispersal of existing species [2]. There are two types of seed dormancy in general: seed coat (physical) dormancy and internal dormancy.
