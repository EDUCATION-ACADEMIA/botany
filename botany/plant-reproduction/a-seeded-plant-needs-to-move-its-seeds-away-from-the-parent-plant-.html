A seeded plant needs to move its seeds away from the parent plant ____

(A) ** to avoid competition

(B) to explore new areas

(C) because it doesnâ€™t want its kids living with it forever

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Seed dispersion or seed dispersal is the movement or transport of seeds away from the parent plant. This is to avoid the competition between parent plant and their siblings for light, space, water and nutrients.

Concept note-2: -Seed dispersal is the transport of seeds away from the parent plant.

Concept note-3: -Dispersal of seeds is very important for the survival of plant species. If plants grow too closely together, they have to compete for light, water and nutrients from the soil. Seed dispersal allows plants to spread out from a wide area and avoid competing with one another for the same resources.

Concept note-4: -Because plants cannot walk around and take their seeds to other places, they have developed other methods to disperse (move) their seeds. The most common methods are wind, water, animals, explosion and fire.

Concept note-5: -The seed has to be dispersed far away from the parent plant, so that it has a chance to grow. If there are too many plants in one place, they will have to compete against each other. If a parent plant and a seedling are in the same place they will compete for water, sunlight and carbon dioxide.
