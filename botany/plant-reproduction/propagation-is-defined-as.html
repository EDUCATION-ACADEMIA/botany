PLANT REPRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
Propagation is defined as

(A) union of egg and sperm

(B) ** process of increasing numbers of a species

(C) a cheaper method of propagation than with seeds

(D) the only way to reproduce some species and cultivars

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant propagation is the process of increasing the number of plants of a particular species or cultivar. There are two primary forms of plant propagation: sexual and asexual. In nature, propagation of plants most often involves sexual reproduction, or the production of viable seeds.

Concept note-2: -Propagation is the process of creating new plants. If you have ever planted a seed or stuck a stem in water until it forms roots that you stuck it in a pot or planted it in the garden, you have experienced propagation.

Concept note-3: -: the act or action of propagating: such as. : increase (as of a kind of organism) in numbers. : the spreading of something (such as a belief) abroad or into new regions. : enlargement or extension (as of a crack) in a solid body.

Concept note-4: -propagate. / (ˈprɒpəˌɡeɪt) / verb. biology to reproduce or cause to reproduce; breed. (tr) horticulture to produce (plants) by layering, grafting, cuttings, etc.

Concept note-5: -There are two types of propagation: sexual and asexual. Sexual reproduction is the union of the pollen and egg, drawing from the genes of two parents to create a new, third individual. Sexual propagation involves the floral parts of a plant.
