Has the beginnings of roots, stems, and leaves.

(A) A. Fruit

(B) ** Embryo

(C) Pollination

(D) Fertilization

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The plant embryo, also known as the seed embryo, is the component of a seed or bud that contains the plantâ€™s roots, stem, and leaves in their earliest forms.

Concept note-2: -Plumule is the part of the embryo which emerges from the seed and develops into the shoot of the plant. It further develops organs such as the stem, flowers, fruits and seeds.

Concept note-3: -Abstract. Each plant starts life from the zygote formed by the fusion of an egg and a sperm cell. The zygote gives rise to a multicellular embryo that displays a basic plant body organization and is surrounded by nutritive endosperm and maternal tissue.

Concept note-4: -The radicle (primary embryonic root) emerges from the seed first to enhance water uptake; it is protected by a root cap produced by the root apical meristem.

Concept note-5: -Radicle is defined as the embryonic root of the plant, which develops into the future root of the plant. It is the first part of the embryo to develop into the root system of plants.
