Which of the following consists of only parts of the female parts of the flower?

(A) style, ovary, anther, pollen grain

(B) anther. style, ovary, stigma, egg cell

(C) egg cell, male reproductive cell, ovule

(D) ** ovule, ovary, style, stigma, egg cell

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The female part is the pistil. The pistil usually is located in the center of the flower and is made up of three parts: the stigma, style, and ovary. The stigma is the sticky knob at the top of the pistil. It is attached to the long, tubelike structure called the style.

Concept note-2: -The female part of the flower is called pistil or carpel and contains the stigma, style and the ovary.

Concept note-3: -The female elements are collectively called the pistil. The top of the pistil is called the stigma, which is a sticky surface receptive to pollen. The bottom of the pistil contains the ovary and the narrowed region in between is called the style.

Concept note-4: -The female reproductive part of a flower is called pistil. It is divided into three parts: stigma, style and the ovary. The female sex cells or ovules are present in the ovary.

Concept note-5: -The female part of the flower is called pistil and contains the stigma, style and the ovary. The male part of the flower on the other hand is called stamen and contains anther and filament.
