PLANT REPRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
The best way for a plant to reproduce when sunlight and nutrients are stable is ____

(A) ** asexual

(B) sexual

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Asexual reproduction in plants occurs through budding, fragmentation, vegetative propagation, and spore formation. No flowers are required for this method. The plants produced by asexual reproduction thrive well in stable environments.

Concept note-2: -A single individual can produce offspring asexually and large numbers of offspring can be produced quickly. In a stable or predictable environment, asexual reproduction is an effective means of reproduction because all the offspring will be adapted to that environment.

Concept note-3: -Asexual reproduction results in plants that are genetically identical to the parent plant, since there is no mixing of male and female gametes, resulting in better survival. The cuttings or buds taken from an adult plant produce progeny that mature faster and are sturdier than a seedling grown from a seed.

Concept note-4: -Binary fission is a most common method of asexual reproduction which starts with the replication of genetic material which is followed by cytokinesis. A furrow is formed in the cell membrane which deepens and finally converges to separate the two daughter cell.

Concept note-5: -Plants are living organisms. That means they need to reproduce in order to pass on their genes to future generations. Plants can create offspring through either sexual or asexual reproduction.
