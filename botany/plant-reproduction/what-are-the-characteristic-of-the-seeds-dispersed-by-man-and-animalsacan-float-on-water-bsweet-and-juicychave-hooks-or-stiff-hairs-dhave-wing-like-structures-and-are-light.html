What are the characteristic of the seeds dispersed by man and animals?A:Can float on water B:Sweet and juicyC:Have hooks or stiff hairs D:Have wing-like structures and are light

(A) A and B only

(B) ** B and C only

(C) C and D only

(D) A, B and C only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Animals can disperse seeds by excreting or burying them; other fruits have structures, such as hooks, that attach themselves to animalsâ€™ fur. Humans also play a role as dispersers by moving fruit to new places and discarding the inedible portions containing the seeds.

Concept note-2: -The seeds which are dispersed by air are small, light and possess hair like structures or wings.

Concept note-3: -Many animal-dispersed fruits are dispersed by vertebrates-especially certain mammals and birds, although fish and reptiles can also act as dispersal agents-or ants. Vertebrate-dispersed fruits and seeds may be fleshy, or may have fleshy coverings; ant-dispersed seeds often have nutrient-rich appendages.
