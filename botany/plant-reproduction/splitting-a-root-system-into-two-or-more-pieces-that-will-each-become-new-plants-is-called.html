Splitting a root system into two or more pieces that will each become new plants is called

(A) cuttings

(B) ** division

(C) tissue culture

(D) grafting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Division, in horticulture and gardening, is a method of asexual plant propagation, where the plant (usually an herbaceous perennial) is broken up into two or more parts. Both the root and crown of each part is kept intact.

Concept note-2: -Division is a simple means of vegetative propagation for plants that produce suckers, stolons, bulbs, tubers or rhizomes. To propagate by division, dig up the plant and divide it carefully using a spade or secateurs.

Concept note-3: -The line between ‘division’ and ‘separation’ is delicate. The examples mentioned above are of propagation by division. Whereas in separation, the rooted or unrooted parts of the plant are detached from the main plant on maturity. These start to develop a new plant in the next season.

Concept note-4: -The correct option is A vegetative propagation. Vegetative propagation is a form of asexual reproduction. Stems, leaves and roots are called as the vegetative parts of a plant. These vegetative parts can be used in the production of new plants. E.g. Eyes of potato, rhizomes of ginger, etc.
