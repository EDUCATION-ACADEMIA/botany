PLANT REPRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
____ is when plants are separated and re-planted.

(A) germination

(B) tip cutting

(C) root cutting

(D) ** division

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Division, in horticulture and gardening, is a method of asexual plant propagation, where the plant (usually an herbaceous perennial) is broken up into two or more parts. Both the root and crown of each part is kept intact.

Concept note-2: -The line between ‘division’ and ‘separation’ is delicate. The examples mentioned above are of propagation by division. Whereas in separation, the rooted or unrooted parts of the plant are detached from the main plant on maturity. These start to develop a new plant in the next season.

Concept note-3: -Division is a simple means of vegetative propagation for plants that produce suckers, stolons, bulbs, tubers or rhizomes. To propagate by division, dig up the plant and divide it carefully using a spade or secateurs.

Concept note-4: -Bulbs of daffodils, tulips, and lilies can be separated in this way. Some bulbs also produce smaller bulbs called bulblets that attach to the base of the parent bulb. They can also be separated for propagation. Cormels (baby corms that attach to the parent corm) can also be separated for propagation.

Concept note-5: -There are two primary forms of plant propagation: sexual and asexual. In nature, propagation of plants most often involves sexual reproduction, or the production of viable seeds. When exposed to proper environmental conditions, these seeds germinate and grow into mature, reproductive plants.
