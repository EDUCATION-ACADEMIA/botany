[SOURCES]
A male reproductive structure in a flower is called a(n) ____

(A) ** Stamen

(B) Pistil

(C) Ovary

(D) Stigma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stamen is the male reproductive organ of a plant. They are present inside the ring of petals and are little stalks with swollen tops. Stamen has two parts-anther and filament. The stalk of stamen is called filament and the swollen top is called an anther.

Concept note-2: -stamen, the male reproductive part of a flower. In all but a few extant angiosperms, the stamen consists of a long slender stalk, the filament, with a two-lobed anther at the tip.

Concept note-3: -The male reproductive part of the flower is the stamen which helps in the production of male gametes and is present in pollen grains. The anther consists of four saclike structures (microsporangia) that produce pollen for pollination. The stamens of a single flower are collectively referred to as androecium.

Concept note-4: -The male parts of the flower are called the stamens and are made up of the anther at the top and the stalk or filament that supports the anther. The female elements are collectively called the pistil.

Concept note-5: -The stamens in a flower are collectively called the androecium.
