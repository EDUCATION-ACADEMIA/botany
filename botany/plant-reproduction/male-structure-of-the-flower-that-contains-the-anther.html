Male structure of the flower that contains the anther

(A) ** stamen

(B) pistil

(C) anther

(D) petal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The male parts of the flower are called the stamens and are made up of the anther at the top and the stalk or filament that supports the anther. The female elements are collectively called the pistil. The top of the pistil is called the stigma, which is a sticky surface receptive to pollen.

Concept note-2: -stamen, the male reproductive part of a flower. In all but a few extant angiosperms, the stamen consists of a long slender stalk, the filament, with a two-lobed anther at the tip. The anther consists of four saclike structures (microsporangia) that produce pollen for pollination.

Concept note-3: -A flower with only stamens is called as androecious. A flower with only carpels is called as gynoecious.

Concept note-4: -The male reproductive part of the flower is the stamen which helps in the production of male gametes and is present in pollen grains. The anther consists of four saclike structures (microsporangia) that produce pollen for pollination. The stamens of a single flower are collectively referred to as androecium.

Concept note-5: -Stamen is the male reproductive part of the flower. Stamen is composed of the anther and filament. Anther contains pollen grains which contain the male gametes.
