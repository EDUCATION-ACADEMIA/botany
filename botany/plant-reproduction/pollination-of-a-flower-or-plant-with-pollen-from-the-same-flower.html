Pollination of a flower or plant with pollen from the same flower.

(A) Cross Pollination

(B) ** Self Pollination

(C) Budding

(D) Meiosis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A flower is self-pollinated (a “selfer”) if pollen is transferred to it from any flower of the same plant and cross-pollinated (an “outcrosser” or “outbreeder”) if the pollen comes from a flower on a different plant.

Concept note-2: -If pollen from a flower of a plant is used to pollinate another flower of the same plant, the process is called self pollination. Self pollination also occurs if the pollen of a flower is used to pollinate the same flower (as it is the same plant).

Concept note-3: -Autogamy. It is a type of self-pollination where the transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma takes place within the same flower.

Concept note-4: -But pollen first must be moved from a male part of a flower, called the anther, to a female part, called the stigma. And pollen can come from the same plant or from flowers on other plants of the same species. In fact, pollen from multiple monkeyflower plants could be left during a single bee’s visit.
