What Kind Of Reproduction Do Yeast Perform?

(A) Spore Formation

(B) ** Budding

(C) Grafting

(D) Rhizomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yeast cells reproduce asexually by an asymmetric division process called budding. In yeast, budding usually occurs during the abundant supply of nutrition. In this process of reproduction, a small bud arises as an outgrowth of the parent body.

Concept note-2: -Yeast usually asexually reproduce by a method called budding. A small knob or bud forms on the parent cell, grows, and finally separates to become a new yeast cell. This new yeast cell is genetically identical to the parent cell.

Concept note-3: -The process of reproduction in yeast is budding. During budding, a bud first appears on the cell wall of the yeast cell. The nucleus then divides and one part moves into the bud. The bud then grows and matures and ultimately detaches from the parent cell to form a new yeast cell.
