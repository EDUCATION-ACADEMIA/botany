PLANT REPRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
In which asexual reproduction method does the new plant grow in artificial media under sterile conditions?

(A) Cutting

(B) Division

(C) ** Tissue Culture

(D) Layering

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Using tissue culture, sometimes called micropropagation, tiny fragments of plants are treated with plant hormones in a sterile growing medium. The hormones stimulate the growth of a callus, from which a new seedling can grow. This method is used to produce large numbers of identical seedlings.

Concept note-2: -Artificial methods of asexual reproduction are frequently employed to give rise to new, and sometimes novel, plants. They include grafting, cutting, layering, and micropropagation.

Concept note-3: -Plant cloning, such as the use of plant tissue culture techniques, is an asexual reproduction of plants to produce duplicates from one plant specimen.

Concept note-4: -Vegetative propagation is referred to as an asexual method of reproduction. As plant cells are totipotent, they can develop into the entire plant, this method of asexual reproduction is referred to as Vegetative propagation.

Concept note-5: -Vegetative Propagation It is any form of asexual reproduction occurring in plants, in which new plants are produced from the vegetative parts of the plants, i.e. roots, stems or buds.
