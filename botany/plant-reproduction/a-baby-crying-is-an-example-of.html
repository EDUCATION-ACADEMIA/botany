A baby crying is an example of

(A) learned behaviour

(B) ** instinct

(C) migration

(D) hibernation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Infants cry as a form of basic instinctive communication. Essentially, newborns are transitioning from life in the womb to the external environment. Up to 27% of parents describe problems with infant crying in the first four months. Up to 38% identify a problem with their infant crying within the first year.

Concept note-2: -Rooting reflex. This is a basic survival instinct. Babies know how to find and latch on to a nipple to feed. If you stroke the side of your baby’s cheek with your finger or breast, he or she will turn their head, open their mouth, and begin to make sucking movements.

Concept note-3: -Colic is the term used to describe infants who cry excessively for no apparent reason during the first three months of life. Colic is one of the most distressing problems of infancy.

Concept note-4: -"Crying is a baby’s principal means of communicating its negative emotions and in the majority of cases the only way they have to express them, ” as explained by Mariano Chóliz, researcher at the University of Valencia.
