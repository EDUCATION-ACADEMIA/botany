Where are haploid sex cells produced in a plant?

(A) ovule and filament

(B) ovary and filament

(C) ovule and anther

(D) ** ovary and anther

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Haploid male gametes, pollen, are formed within the anthers of the androecium. Haploid female gametes, eggs, are formed within the ovary of the gynoecium.

Concept note-2: -Germline cells or germ cells undergo meiosis to produce sperm and eggs. Germ cells are diploid and have 46 chromosomes in humans. After meiosis takes place the resulting gametes are haploid and have 23 chromosomes. In females, germline cells are found in the ovaries, and in males they are found in the testes.

Concept note-3: -Haploid cells in humans are produced by meiosis. This is a type of cell division in which a single diploid parent cell divides to produce four, non-identical haploid daughter cells. Meiosis is used to produced gametes and is a necessary precursor to sexual reproduction.

Concept note-4: -Explanation: Since ovary & anther undergoes only mitotic divisions during their development in which chromosome number remains the same, so these structures are diploid. The zygote is formed by the fusion of both male and female gametes (haploid structures) .

Concept note-5: -Male gametes are produced in the anther. It is a haploid (n) gamete.
