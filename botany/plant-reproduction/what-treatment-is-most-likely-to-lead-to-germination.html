What treatment is most likely to lead to germination?

(A) ** Soaking the seeds in a solution of gibberellins

(B) Increasing CO2 concentration

(C) Increasing light intensity

(D) Dehydrating the seeds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Soaking of shallot seeds in 100 ppm gibberellins solution for 30 minutes can increase plant height and the number of leaves [7]. Meanwhile, the immersion treatment of shallot seeds (Tuktuk variety) using gibberellin solution for 4 hours could increase the percentage of germination by 85.33% [8].

Concept note-2: -GA Signaling in Dormancy and Seed Germination GA exerts its influence in two manners, first by increasing the growth potential of embryo and second by inducing hydrolytic enzymes.

Concept note-3: -In short, you fold up a small piece of paper towel, place the seeds in it, along with a pinch of GA3, and add a few drops of water. Let sit for 24 hours in a plastic baggie. Then treat as normal for germination.

Concept note-4: -Germination percentage was significantly different among treatment methods (p < 0.05, ANOVA). Germination percentage of soaking in hot water was the highest (99.2 ± 1.1%).

Concept note-5: -Expose seeds to high temperatures (40−42°C) for 1−2 days prior to sowing. Seed priming-Soak seeds for 4−8 hrs and re-dry prior to sowing. Seeds must be sown within 1−2 days after priming. Pre-germination-Submerge seeds in water for 12−24 hrs or until small shoots appear at the end of the seed.
