The most common type of grafting is

(A) t-budding

(B) simple layering

(C) stem-tip propagation

(D) ** scion cut out of the stock plant

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In grafting, the upper part (scion) of one plant grows on the root system (rootstock) of another plant. In the budding process, a bud is taken from one plant and grown on another.

Concept note-2: -Different types of traditional grafting methods. (a) Cut grafting, (b) cleft grafting, (c) crown grafting, (d), splice grafting, (e) tongue grafting, (f) approach grafting. The success of the graft depends on the compatibility between the rootstock and scion.

Concept note-3: -A stock is a rooted stem into which a scion or bud is grafted, and the portion of the mixture provides the root. Scion is a living plant component that is detached and grafted to the stalk during the grafting process.

Concept note-4: -Grafting roses is the most common example of bud grafting. In this method a bud is removed from the parent plant, and the base of the bud is inserted beneath the bark of the stem of the stock plant from which the rest of the shoot has been cut.

Concept note-5: -Grafting is a horticulture technique where the living detached portion of the plant is used to regenerate the growth. One plant is selected for its roots and this is called the stock or rootstock. The other plant is selected for its stems, leaves, flowers, or fruits and is called the scion.
