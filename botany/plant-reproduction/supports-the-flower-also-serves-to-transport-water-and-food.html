[SOURCES]
Supports the flower, also serves to transport water and food

(A) Filament

(B) Pistil

(C) ** Stem

(D) Style

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Solution: A stem performs the following functions in a plant: (i) It supports branches, leaves, flowers, and fruits. (ii) It transports water and minerals from the roots to the leaves and other parts of plants.

Concept note-2: -The xylem distributes water and dissolved minerals upward through the plant, from the roots to the leaves. The phloem carries food downward from the leaves to the roots.

Concept note-3: -The xylem transports water and minerals from the roots up the plant stem and into the leaves. In a mature flowering plant or tree, most of the cells that make up the xylem are specialised cells called vessels.

Concept note-4: -Stems carry water and nutrients taken up by the roots to the leaves. Then the food produced by the leaves moves to other parts of the plant. The cells that do this work are called the xylem cells. They move water.

Concept note-5: -The xylem transports water and minerals from the roots to the leaves while the phloem moves food substances from leaves to the rest of the plant.
