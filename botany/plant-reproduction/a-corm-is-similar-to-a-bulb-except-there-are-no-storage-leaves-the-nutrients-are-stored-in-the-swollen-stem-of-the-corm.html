[SOURCES]
A corm is similar to a bulb except there are no storage leaves. The nutrients are stored in the swollen stem of the corm.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Corms are actually enlarged underground stems that store nutrients, surrounded by papery outer layers. After stems sprout from the corm, buds form on top of the stem. Corms at their center are solid tissue, whereas Bulbs are immature layers of leaves.

Concept note-2: -Corm and Bulb-Differences. Bulbs are structurally short stems with fleshy leaves or leaf bases. Corms are rounded, underground storage organs consisting of a swollen stem base covered with scale leaves. Bulbs are modified leaves that store nutrients.

Concept note-3: -Corms look like true bulbs but they are solid, so they do not have layers of modified leaves. As the leaves and flowers grow, they absorb the nutrients and the corm shrivels up and disappears.

Concept note-4: -Like bulbs, corms are underground storage structures that have evolved in some plants. Corms are a storage unit made of compressed stems, unlike the leaves that provide the storage function in bulbs. Because they are stems, corms have nodes, internodes, and meristems just like above-ground stems.
