PLANT REPRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
This plant grows under the ground. Examples of plants are onion and garlic. Name this natural way of reproduction.

(A) ** underground stems

(B) stem cuttings

(C) leaves cuttings

(D) suckers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -rhizome, also called creeping rootstalk, horizontal underground plant stem capable of producing the shoot and root systems of a new plant. Rhizomes are used to store starches and proteins and enable plants to perennate (survive an annual unfavourable season) underground.

Concept note-2: -Vegetative propagation is a type of asexual reproduction in which new plants are produced from vegetative parts of plants like roots, stems, leaves, and buds. Underground stems of onion, tulip, and lily are called bulbs. These bulbs have axillary buds protected by fleshy scale leaves.

Concept note-3: -Onion and garlic are also examples of underground stems that are bulbs. The whole parts of underground stems are not edible, only some of the parts are edible.-Onion and garlic are also examples of underground stems that are bulbs.

Concept note-4: -Examples of underground stems include corms, such as taro (left); rhizomes, such as ginger (center); and tubers, such as potatoes (right).

Concept note-5: -An onion isn’t a root or a stem in the traditional sense. It’s a tunicated bulb, or altered stem, with fleshy leaves that keep food and transmit it to other sections of the plant as needed. The onions are a member of the Allium genus, so they are among the most widely farmed plants inside the genus.
