Which of the following plant adaptations allows plants to use animals to transport their seed to new locations?

(A) Long vines

(B) Strong stems

(C) ** Sweet fruit

(D) Large leaves

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -D. Deep Roots. The plant adaptations allow plants to use animals to transport their seeds to new locations is sweet fruit. The correct option is C.

Concept note-2: -Animal dispersal First, some plants, like the burr at left, have barbs or other structures that get tangled in animal fur or feathers, and are then carried to new sites. Other plants produce their seeds inside fleshy fruits that then get eaten be an animal.

Concept note-3: -Plants that depend on animals for dispersal have seeds that are adapted to traveling on the outside or the inside of the animal. Seeds with burrs or hooks can attach to an animalâ€™s fur. For seeds that are transported internally, plants provide an attractive fruit pulp reward in return for the ride.

Concept note-4: -Some have a parachute-like structure to keep them afloat. Some fruits-for example, the dandelion-have hairy, weightless structures that are suited to dispersal by wind. Seeds dispersed by water are contained in light and buoyant fruit, giving them the ability to float.

Concept note-5: -Petals are colored and scented to attract insects and other creatures for the purposes of pollination. The coloration of parts of some plants (fruits for example berries, or flower petals) makes them attractive to some animals (for example birds or bees).
