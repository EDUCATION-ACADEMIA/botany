How many nuclei are contained within an angiosperm embryo sac?

(A) two

(B) four

(C) six

(D) ** eight

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eight celled. Hint: The embryo sac is the gametophyte of the angiosperm. The eight nuclei of the embryo sac are not separated by proper cell walls. During sexual reproduction, only one of the eight nuclei serves as the egg.

Concept note-2: -gametophyte of angiosperms (called the embryo sac) is tiny and contains only a few (typically eight) nuclei; the cytoplasm associated more or less directly with these nuclei is not partitioned by cell walls.

Concept note-3: -A mature embryo sac consists of two synergids, one egg cell and three antipodal cells with one nucleus each. Then there is one large central cell that has two nuclei. Hence, a mature embryo sac consists of 7 cells and 8 nuclei.

Concept note-4: -Thus, a typical angiosperm embryo sac at maturity is 7-celled but 8-nucleated as the central cell has 2-nuclei.

Concept note-5: -Three antipodal cells are located at the chalazal end. Six of the eight nuclei are enclosed by cell walls, whereas, the remaining two nuclei (polar nuclei) are located in the central cell. Hence, a typical angiosperm embryo sac at maturity is 8-nucleate and 7-celled.
