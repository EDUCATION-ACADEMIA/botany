What specialized plant structure increases the probability of successful reproduction?

(A) ** Brightly colored petals

(B) Strong roots

(C) Dark green leaves

(D) The ability to hold in more water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The stamens are the male parts of the flower. The anthers hold pollen, which hold the plant sperm cells. Petals are brightly colored to attract pollinators like bees.

Concept note-2: -To increase the probability of successful reproduction, there are specialized plant structures such as brightly colored petals to attract potential pollinators and extended stamens that require animals to brush against the plants in order to receive the nectar.

Concept note-3: -The flowers are special structures for reproduction. They contain male parts that make pollen and female parts that contain ovules. Some plants have these male and female parts in different flowers.

Concept note-4: -Examples of animal behaviors that affect the probability of plant reproduction could include transferring pollen or seeds, and creating conditions for seed germination and growth.
