A very small and smooth pollen grain with a very low mass would be expected to have which dispersal method?

(A) water

(B) animals

(C) insects

(D) ** wind

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fungal spores and pollens can be dispersed in a number of ways: by animals and insects; by water; by wind or by rain.

Concept note-2: -The pollen grains are very small and light so they are easily carried on the wind. A lot of pollen is produced, which increases the chances of a pollen grain reaching the stigma in another flower. The stigmas are long and feathery, which gives a large surface area for catching pollen.

Concept note-3: -Pollination: The transfer of the pollen grains from the male part of the plant (anther of a stamen) to the female part of the plant (stigma of a pistil) is called pollination.

Concept note-4: -Pollen grains of wind-pollinated flowers are produced in large quantities to make sure that at least some pollen grains reach the stigmas of other flowers and successful pollination takes place as many pollen grains are wasted. These pollen grains are light and hence are easily transferred to other flowers.
