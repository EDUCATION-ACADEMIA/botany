An apple falling from a tree is an example of seed dispersal via ____

(A) Wind

(B) Water

(C) ** Gravity

(D) Animal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Heavy fruits like apples fall on the ground as the ripen and get heavier. Due to their weight, they get pulled towards the Earth i.e. gravity acts on them. So the seeds of heavy fruits like apples get dispersed by gravitational force.

Concept note-2: -Apples, Commelina, canna, coconuts, calabash, passion fruit are a few examples of plants whose seeds are dispersed by Gravity – A force of attraction.

Concept note-3: -Gravity dispersal, also known as “shattering” or “fruit drop” is the dispersal of a seed by falling. As the fruit reaches maturity, a natural process called abscission weakens supporting structures like stems and seed pods. This is what causes a ripe apple to fall from the tree.

Concept note-4: -Different Types of Seed Dispersal Gravity: Where the seeds simply fall to the ground, like apples, black locust tree seed pods, common mullein and teasel seeds.

Concept note-5: -Ans-Several plants, such as apples, Commelina, canna, coconuts, calabash, and passion fruit, are dispersed by Gravity, which is a force of attraction that attracts and pulls things together.
