One of the biggest benefit of tree roots is

(A) providing food for wildlife

(B) ** preventing soil erosion

(C) compacting soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Trees have thick root systems that branch into fine filaments and create a network. This network of root systems help stabilize the soil around the tree and hold it in place. Trees also reduce run-off of nutrients and soil sediment in-stream.

Concept note-2: -Soil erosion is the washing away of soil by wind or water. The roots of a plant hold the soil firmly. Because of this, soil cannot easily be washed or blown away. So, plants prevent soil erosion.

Concept note-3: -The tiny hairs found on plant roots play a pivotal role in helping reduce soil erosion, a new study has found. The research provides compelling evidence that when root hairs interact with the surrounding soil they reduce soil erosion and increase soil cohesion by binding soil particles.
