Which of these describes Clear Cutting, select all that apply

(A) ** All trees in an area are removed

(B) ** Creates open habitat good for ground nesting birds

(C) ** Generate a large profit

(D) Only removes unhealthy or invasive trees

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Clear-cuts are forest-felling when all or most of the trees are removed in the selected area at the same time. The method is the most commercially attractive and thus the most typical one. Commonly, trees are cut uniformly.

Concept note-2: -Many forested regions in North America and Europe have been altered by clear-cutting. Elsewhere, clear-cutting of the rain forests in Borneo, the Amazonian rain forest, and the Great Bear rain forest in British Columbia is decimating these ancient ecosystems.

Concept note-3: -Clearcutting causes erosion, landslides and contamination of our water supply with sediment and the toxic herbicides used in the clearcutting process. Clearcutting also compacts the ground and decreases the soilâ€™s ability to retain water, ultimately reducing our supply of clean water.

Concept note-4: -Clear-cutting is a silvicultural system that removes an entire stand of trees from an area of 1 ha or more, and greater than two tree heights in width, in a single harvesting operation (Figure 5). It can be highly profitable.
