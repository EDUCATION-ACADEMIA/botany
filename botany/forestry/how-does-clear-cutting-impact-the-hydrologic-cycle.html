How does clear cutting impact the hydrologic cycle?

(A) Increases transpiration of water

(B) ** Increase water evaporation from soil

(C) Decreases runoff from watersheds

(D) Decreases siltation of streams

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When significant numbers of trees are cut down, it disturbs the evaporation level and dries up the moisture needed to continue the water cycle. This affects water cycle balance and can lead to drought in critical agricultural areas.

Concept note-2: -Clearcutting causes erosion, landslides and contamination of our water supply with sediment and the toxic herbicides used in the clearcutting process. Clearcutting also compacts the ground and decreases the soilâ€™s ability to retain water, ultimately reducing our supply of clean water.

Concept note-3: -Deforestation may lead to surface warming due to reduced evaporation; reduced evaporation also reduces water vapor in the atmosphere and thereby reduces precipitation.

Concept note-4: -Healthy forests act as a filter to keep pollution out of water. Strong roots anchor soil against erosion and material on the forest floor helps absorb nutrients and sediment. But when forests are disturbed and degraded, sediment flows into streams and pollutes water.

Concept note-5: -Deforestation can disrupt the water cycle by decreasing precipitation which can lead to changes in river flow and water volume.
