What is the role of private timber companies in national forests?

(A) They plan the sale of trees that are harvested.

(B) They build roads for logging.

(C) They manage timber sales.

(D) ** They receive the profits from logging.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Clear-cutting is generally the most cost-efficient method for timber companies, but it also has the greatest impact on forest ecosystems. Clear-cutting results in even-aged stands of regrowth.

Concept note-2: -Timber harvesting “always” results in the destruction of the forest. Timber is harvested from national forests by “government resource managers". “Wildfire suppression” prevents future fires by reducing the amount of forest debris on the forest floor.

Concept note-3: -Trees are important because they provide valuable commodities, including wood, paper, and fruit. However, forests are not distributed equally around Earth, and there are economic and social implications of some regions having more timber resources than others.

Concept note-4: -Timber is a renewable resource because of the regrowth of forests. Unlike other products, such as iron ore for steel, timber requires considerable less energy in the conversion of raw products to usable ones and in the remanufacture of material.
