What is the sequence in a commuity in which animal wildlife species get their food?

(A) ** Food chain

(B) Food web

(C) Biotic

(D) Abiotic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The food chain begins with a producer, which is eaten by a primary consumer. The primary consumer may be eaten by a secondary consumer, which in turn may be consumed by a tertiary consumer. The tertiary consumers may sometimes become prey to the top predators known as the quaternary consumers.

Concept note-2: -A food chain in an ecosystem consists of producers, primary consumers, secondary consumers and decomposers. Here, the producer is grass and the first consumer is the insect. The insect is eaten by a bird, a secondary consumer and the bird is eaten by a snake, which is a tertiary consumer.

Concept note-3: -In ecology, a food chain is a series of organisms that eat one another so that energy and nutrients flow from one to the next.

Concept note-4: -It includes herbivores which are animals that eat plants, carnivores which are animals that eat other animals, parasites that live on other organisms by harming them and lastly the scavengers, which are animals that eat dead animalsâ€™ carcasses.

Concept note-5: -A carnivorous animal that hunts other animals is called a predator; an animal that is hunted is called prey.
