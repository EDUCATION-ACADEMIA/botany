What is board feet?

(A) How tall a tree is

(B) How wide a tree is

(C) The merchantable height of a tree

(D) ** How many boards can be cut from a tree

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Board feet is a measurement of the volume of lumber, often used by wholesalers and sawmills in Canada and the United States. A single board foot is equivalent to 1 square foot of lumber that is 1 inch thick, meaning it has a length of 12 inches, a width of 12 inches, and a thickness of 1 inch.

Concept note-2: -For example, a 20-inch DBH oak tree with a merchantable height of 2½ logs contains 260 board feet Doyle rule or 350 board feet International 1/4-Inch rule.

Concept note-3: -Hardwood lumber is typically sold by the board foot, a unit of volume equivalent to a board that is one inch thick, one foot wide and one foot long, or 144 cubic inches.

Concept note-4: -Find the radius of the tree (r = DBH/2). Divide the DBH by 2. Convert the units from inches into feet by dividing by 12 (r = DBH/2/12).

Concept note-5: -Small trees produce the least firewood. A large tree however with a trunk diameter of 24 inches and a height of 120 feet would produce at least 600 pcs; a half bush cord or 3 face cords of firewood, so tree size before you cut is an important consideration before you fire up the chainsaw.
