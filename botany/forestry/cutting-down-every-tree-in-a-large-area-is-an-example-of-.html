Cutting down every tree in a large area is an example of ____

(A) Selective Cutting

(B) Shelterwood Cutting

(C) ** Clear Cutting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deforestation is removal of forested land. Trees are removed on a large scale for the facilitation of human activities. It is majorly done for commercial purposes. Deforestation harms the environment on a very big scale.

Concept note-2: -Types Of Clear-Cutting In Forests Patch – felling in a certain area (patches). Strip – cutting trees in rows usually at the right angle to prevailing winds. With reserves – leaving some trees for different purposes, e.g., as shelters for wildlife.

Concept note-3: -The cutting down of trees and forests by humans on a large scale is called deforestation.

Concept note-4: -Deforestation is the intentional clearing of forested land.

Concept note-5: -The loss of trees and other vegetation can cause climate change, fewer crops, flooding, soil erosion, increased greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, and creates problems for indigenous people as the effect of deforestation.
