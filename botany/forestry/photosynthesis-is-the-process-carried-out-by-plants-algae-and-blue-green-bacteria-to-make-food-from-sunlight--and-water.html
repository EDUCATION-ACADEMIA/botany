Photosynthesis is the process carried out by plants, algae and blue-green bacteria to make food from sunlight, ____, and water

(A) ** Carbon dioxide

(B) Algae

(C) Carbon monoxide

(D) soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants are autotrophs, which means they produce their own food. They use the process of photosynthesis to transform water, sunlight, and carbon dioxide into oxygen, and simple sugars that the plant uses as fuel. These primary producers form the base of an ecosystem and fuel the next trophic levels.

Concept note-2: -The essential ingredients in making this food are sunlight, the chlorophyll, water and carbon dioxide. Green plants are the only plants that produce oxygen and make food, which is called photosynthesis.

Concept note-3: -This process is called photosynthesis and is performed by all plants, algae, and even some microorganisms. To perform photosynthesis, plants need three things: carbon dioxide, water, and sunlight.

Concept note-4: -Photosynthesis is the biochemical process by which plants, algae and certain bacteria can convert the sunâ€™s energy into chemical energy in the form of carbohydrates.

Concept note-5: -The process in which carbon dioxide reacts with water in presence of chlorophyll and sunlight to produce carbohydrates (glucose) and oxygen is known as photosynthesis. New products (glucose and oxygen) are obtained during photosynthesis, thus it is a chemical change.
