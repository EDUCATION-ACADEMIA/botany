Forest layering that takes place naturally is known as vertical layering or vertical stratification.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abstract. Vertical stratification (VS) is a widespread phenomenon in plant and animal communities in forests and a key factor for structuring their species richness and biodiversity, particularly in tropical forests.

Concept note-2: -Hint: Stratification is the process of vertical distribution of different species occupying different levels. In this process trees occupy the top layer, shrubs arranged in the second layer, herbs and grasses occupy the bottom most layers.

Concept note-3: -Rainforests are characterized by a unique vegetative structure consisting of several vertical layers including the overstory, canopy, understory, shrub layer, and ground level.
