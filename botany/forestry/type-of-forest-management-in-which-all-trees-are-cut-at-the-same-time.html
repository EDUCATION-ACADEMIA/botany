Type of forest management in which all trees are cut at the same time.

(A) selective cutting

(B) shelter wood cutting

(C) Seed cutting

(D) ** clear cutting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Clear-cutting is a silvicultural system that removes an entire stand of trees from an area of 1 ha or more, and greater than two tree heights in width, in a single harvesting operation (Figure 5). It can be highly profitable.

Concept note-2: -A clearcut is an area of forestland where most of the standing trees are logged at the same time and a few trees remain post-harvest. Forested buffers are left around streams and lakes, and the area is replanted within two years of harvest.

Concept note-3: -What Is Clear-Cutting? Clear-cuts are forest-felling when all or most of the trees are removed in the selected area at the same time. The method is the most commercially attractive and thus the most typical one. Commonly, trees are cut uniformly.

Concept note-4: -What is it called when we cut all the trees and leave it? Deforestation is the purposeful clearing of forested land. Throughout history and into modern times, forests have been razed to make space for agriculture and animal grazing, and to obtain wood for fuel, manufacturing, and construction.
