Which of these abiotic factors determine where forests are found and what type they are?

(A) ** Precipitation

(B) ** Soil type

(C) ** Temperature

(D) Wildlife present

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Climatic factors such as sunlight, temperature, rainfall and humidity play a great role in determining the flora and fauna in a forest ecosystem.

Concept note-2: -Abiotic factors include water, sunlight, oxygen, soil, and temperature. Abiotic factors are the non-living parts of the environment that can often have a major influence on living organisms. So Temperature is an abiotic factor and it affects animals, plants, and humans in an ecosystem.

Concept note-3: -Forests consist not only of living (biotic) components like trees, animals, plants, and other living things but also of nonliving (abiotic) components such as soil, water, air, and landforms.

Concept note-4: -Biotic factors also include once-living parts such as dead leaves on the forest floor. Abiotic factors are nonliving aspects of the environment such as sunlight, temperature and water. One important abiotic factor is soil.

Concept note-5: -The term abiotic refers to all the non-living factors present in an ecosystem. Sunlight, water and land, all constitute the abiotic factors.
