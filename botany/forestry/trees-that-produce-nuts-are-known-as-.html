Trees that produce nuts are known as ____

(A) ** Hard Mast-bearing trees

(B) Den trees

(C) Soft Mast-bearing

(D) Mature forest

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tree species such as oak, hickory, and beech produce a hard mast-acorns, hickory nuts, and beechnuts. It has been traditional to turn pigs loose into forests to fatten on this form of mast in a practice known as pannage. Other tree and shrub species produce a soft mast, such as raspberries, blueberries, and greenbriar.

Concept note-2: -Every few years, some species of trees and shrubs produce a bumper crop of their fruits or nuts. The collective term for these fruits and nuts is ‘mast’, so we call this a mast year. Two of our most recognisable trees, oak and beech, fluctuate massively year on year in the amount of acorns and beech nuts they produce.

Concept note-3: -Hard mast consists of hard shelled fruits and seeds (e.g., acorns, hickory nuts, beechnuts, black walnuts, pine seeds) which are high in carbohydrates, fats, and proteins. Hard mast generally matures in the late summer and fall and may persist into winter months.

Concept note-4: -mast seeding, also called masting, the production of many seeds by a plant every two or more years in regional synchrony with other plants of the same species.

Concept note-5: -The term ‘mast’ comes from the ancient English word ‘mæst’ and traditionally means the fruit of beech, oak, chestnut and other forest trees, especially as food for pigs. In mast years, pig herders would fatten their pigs on the glut of acorns and nuts.
