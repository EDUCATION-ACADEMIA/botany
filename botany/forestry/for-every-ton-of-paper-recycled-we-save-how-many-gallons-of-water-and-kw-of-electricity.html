For every ton of paper recycled we save how many gallons of water and kw of electricity

(A) 5000 gallons and 2400 kw

(B) 6000 gallons and 2800 kw

(C) ** 7000 gallons and 4100kw

(D) 9000 gallons and 5100kw

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: â€“Each ton (2000 pounds) of recycled paper can save 380 gallons of oil, three cubic yards of landfill space, 4000 kilowatts of energy, and 7000 gallons of water. This shows a 64 percent energy savings, a 58 percent water savings, and 60 pounds less air pollution.

Concept note-2: -Manufacturing pulp and paper requires a substantial amount of water. The US benchmark for water use within pulp and paper mills is approximately 17, 000 gallons/ton of paper, with one of the most efficient kraft pulp and paper mills only using 4, 500 gallons/ton.

Concept note-3: -U.S. paper manufacturers use about six gallons of water to make a pound of paper, according to the American Forest and Paper Association and other sources, most of which is returned to watersheds. More than a drop in the bucket, but, compared with other water uses, not exactly profligate either.

Concept note-4: -For every ton of recycled paper we can save almost 17 fully grown trees.
