Which part builds the cells?

(A) ** cambium

(B) heartwood

(C) sapwood

(D) crown

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vascular cambium is a layer of meristematic cells (or initials) that arises between primary xylem and phloem.

Concept note-2: -In Arabidopsis and other species which undergo secondary growth, a lateral vascular meristem called cambium develops mainly from the procambium embedded between the differentiated xylem and phloem. In the shoot, the cambium between the vascular bundles arises from parenchyma and endodermis tissues.

Concept note-3: -The cambium layer is made up of a special type of plant tissue known as the cambium tissue. Cambium tissue is made up of stem cells, or plant cells that have not yet been specialized or differentiated. These are also referred to as meristematic cells.

Concept note-4: -In the third stage, a cambial zone develops between the wound periderm and the xylem tissue laid down prior to wounding. This process is initiated by anticlinal and periclinal divisions of a few callus cells only. Later this process extends tangentially to form a continuous belt of wound cambium.
