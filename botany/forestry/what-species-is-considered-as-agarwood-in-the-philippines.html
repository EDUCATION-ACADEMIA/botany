What species is considered as agarwood in the Philippines?

(A) Toog

(B) Philippine Teak

(C) ** Lapnisan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Aquilaria malaccensis, locally known as “lapnisan, ” is a major source of agarwood-a resinous heartwood used for perfume and incense.

Concept note-2: -Lapnisan or agarwood is a non-timber type of wood that is used for its sap or resin, which is used in the perfume and scents industry. The tree is very rare and is found in the deepest parts of the jungles of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines. Agarwood or Lapnisan.

Concept note-3: -Known variously as lapnisan or lanete, the agarwood tree is from the Aquilaria species of the Thymelaeceae Family. There are many Aquilaria species (19) and the one endemic to the Philippines is Aquilaria malaccensis.

Concept note-4: -Product details of LAPNISAN AGARWOOD SEEDS 5 PIECES WORLD’S EXPENSIVE TREE SEEDS. For thousands of years, agarwood has been known as “The Wood of the Gods.” First-grade agarwood can cost as much as $100, 000 per kilogram, making it one of the most expensive raw materials in the world.

Concept note-5: -In the country, lapnisan is found only in the center of the thickest jungles of Mindanao and Visayas. Foreigners sometimes travel to the country and hike to remote villages seeking help to locate the rare tree.
