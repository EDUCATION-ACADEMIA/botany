Deforestation is still occuring rapidly in some developing nations

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to the FAO, agriculture causes around 80% of deforestation. And how does agriculture cause so much deforestation? According to the same report, 33% of agriculture-caused deforestation is a consequence of subsistence agriculture – such as local peasant agriculture in developing countries.

Concept note-2: -According to the Task Force report, the consequences of deforestation are rising prices, floods, siltation, and desertification.

Concept note-3: -Today, most deforestation occurs in the tropics. 71% of this is driven by demand in domestic markets, and the remaining 29% for the production of products that are traded.

Concept note-4: -The loss of trees changes the quality of soil which leads to soil erosion, fewer crop production and flooding. The biological cycles (Bio-geo chemical cycles) are altered because of deforestation. The amount of carbon di oxide increases in the earth which leads to global warming.
