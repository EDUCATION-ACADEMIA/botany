FORESTRY

[SOURCES]
Dense covering formed by the leafy tops of tall rain forest trees

(A) Wildlife habitats

(B) Wilderness areas

(C) Tree farm

(D) ** Canopy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Canopy: A dense covering formed by the leafy tops of tall trees.

Concept note-2: -The tops of trees can form a dense layer of branches and leaves, called the canopy.

Concept note-3: -The canopy, which may be over 100 feet (30 m) above the ground, is made up of the overlapping branches and leaves of rainforest trees. Scientists estimate that 60-90 percent of life in the rainforest is found in the trees, making this the richest habitat for plant and animal life.

Concept note-4: -The tall tress in a tropical rainforest form a leafy roof called the canopy. Under the canopy is a second layer of shorter trees and vines that form an understory.

Concept note-5: -A dense forest specially rain forests have different layers in it. The topmost layer which is formed by tall trees, forms the crown of the forest and is known as canopy.
