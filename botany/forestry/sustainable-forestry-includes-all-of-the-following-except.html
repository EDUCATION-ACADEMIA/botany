FORESTRY

[SOURCES]
Sustainable forestry includes all of the following except

(A) using ecological services to determine economic value

(B) leaving dead trees to enhance wildlife habitat and nutrient recycling

(C) selective cutting of individual trees or small groups of tree species

(D) ** clear-cutting only on steep slopes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Conservation of biological diversity including forests with exceptional conservation value. Management of visual quality and recreational benefits. Protection of special sites. Efficient use of forest resources.

Concept note-2: -Many sustainable forestry practices can protect or encourage forest regeneration. They include putting up a fence to exclude deer, controlling weeds and other plants, and removing some trees to allow more sunlight to reach down into the forest.

Concept note-3: -A clearcut is an area of forestland where most of the standing trees are logged at the same time and a few trees remain post-harvest. Forested buffers are left around streams and lakes, and the area is replanted within two years of harvest.

Concept note-4: -Sustainable forest management offers a holistic approach to ensure forest activities deliver social, environmental and economic benefits, balance competing needs and maintain and enhance forest functions now and in the future.
