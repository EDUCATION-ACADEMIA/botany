FORESTRY

[SOURCES]
any light, easily cut wood; cone bearing or coniferous

(A) Hardwood

(B) Forestry

(C) ** Softwood

(D) Tree farms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Softwoods come from coniferous trees such as pine, fir, spruce and larch. These trees take around 40 years to grow before they are ready to harvest. Hardwoods come from broadleaved trees such as oak, ash and beech. These trees take much longer to grow, up to 150 years before they are ready to harvest.

Concept note-2: -The woods of coniferous forest are useful for making pulp, which is used for manufacturing paper and newsprint. Match boxes and packing boxes are also made from softwood. Chir, pine, cedar are the important variety of trees in these forests.

Concept note-3: -Examples of softwood trees: Pine. Cedar. Redwood.

Concept note-4: -Softwood properties: Flexible, lighter in weight and less dense than most hardwoods, softwoods are frequently used for interior mouldings, the manufacturing of windows, construction framing and generating sheet goods such as plywood and fibreboard.
