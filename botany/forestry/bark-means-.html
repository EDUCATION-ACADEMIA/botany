Bark means ____

(A) trees that maintain their taproot system.

(B) ** the rough outer covering of a tree.

(C) several basic layers.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bark is the outermost layers of stems and roots of woody plants . Plants with bark include trees, woody vines, and shrubs. Bark refers to all the tissues outside the vascular cambium and is a nontechnical term. It overlays the wood and consists of the inner bark and the outer bark.

Concept note-2: -A: The outer bark is the treeâ€™s protection from the outside world. Continually renewed from within, it helps keep out moisture in the rain, and prevents the tree from losing moisture when the air is dry. It insulates against cold and heat and wards off insect enemies.

Concept note-3: -The bark of the tree is probably one of the most familiar parts of a tree. The bark is an outer covering of dead tissue, which protects the tree from weather, disease, insects, fire and mechanical injury.

Concept note-4: -Bark. The protective outer covering of branches and stems that arises from the cork cambium.

Concept note-5: -Bark is the dead skin of trees. It is rough and hard. Protect the tender part of the tree, what is inside. The trunk of a tree grows from the inside out. Every year a new layer of tender wood forms around the trunk, but inside the crust.
