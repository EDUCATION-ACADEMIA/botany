EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What are fuels? Anything that can burn is fuel for a fire. During a wildland fire all kinds of plant material can act as fuel, including grasses, shrubs, trees, dead leaves, and fallen pine needles.

Concept note-2: -Wildfires are classified as burning at different levels of severity: low, medium, and high. Severity is a function of intensity, or how hot the fire was, and its spread rate, or the speed at which it travels. A high-severity fire is going to do some real damage.

Concept note-3: -The stronger the wind, the faster the spread of the fire. Wind brings an additional supply of air to the fire. It flattens the flame which pre-heats the fuel ahead and causes spot fires by blowing sparks and embers ahead of the main fire into new fuel sources.
