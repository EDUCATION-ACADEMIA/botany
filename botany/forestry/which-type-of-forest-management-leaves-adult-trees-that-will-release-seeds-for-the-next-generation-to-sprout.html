Which type of forest management leaves adult trees that will release seeds for the next generation to sprout?

(A) ** shelterwood cut

(B) clear cut

(C) selective cut

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The shelterwood method is a silvicultural approach through which a new generation of trees is established naturally under the shelter of older trees by a series of partial cuttings intended to: stimulate seed production. create favorable seedbed conditions on the forest floor.

Concept note-2: -Shelterwood system definition The uniform system is the system that most often relies on a series of preparatory cuttings (thinnings) to ready the stand for the regeneration/establishment cutting by encouraging crown expansion, and promoting windfirmness and cone production.
