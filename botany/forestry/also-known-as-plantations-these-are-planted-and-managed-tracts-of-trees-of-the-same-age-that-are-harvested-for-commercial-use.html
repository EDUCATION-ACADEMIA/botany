Also known as plantations, these are planted and managed tracts of trees of the same age that are harvested for commercial use.

(A) ** Tree farms

(B) Wildlife habitats

(C) Wilderness areas

(D) Canopy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A tree plantation, forest plantation, plantation forest, timber plantation or tree farm is a forest planted for high volume production of wood, usually by planting one type of tree as a monoculture forest. The term tree farm also is used to refer to tree nurseries and Christmas tree farms.

Concept note-2: -Plantation agriculture is an extensive type of agriculture where the large size of the farm is utilized to grow a single crop which is even known as monoculture.

Concept note-3: -The term plantation crop refers to those crops which are cultivated on an extensive scale in contiguous area, owned and managed by an individual or a company. The crops include tea, coffee, rubber, cocoa, coconut, arecanut, oil palm, palmyrah and cashew.

Concept note-4: -/plænˈteɪ.ʃən/ a large farm, especially in a hot part of the world, on which a particular type of crop is grown: a tea/cotton/rubber plantation. an area where trees are grown for wood: plantations of fast-growing conifers.
