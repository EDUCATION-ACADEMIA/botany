FORESTRY

[SOURCES]
Wildfire suppression prevents future fires by reducing the amount of forest debris on the forest floor.

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Forest conversion is the clearing of natural forests (deforestation) to use the land for another purpose, often agricultural (growing crops like palm oil or creating pasture for cattle), but also for mines, infrastructure or urbanisation.

Concept note-2: -Timber harvesting “always” results in the destruction of the forest. Timber is harvested from national forests by “government resource managers". “Wildfire suppression” prevents future fires by reducing the amount of forest debris on the forest floor.

Concept note-3: -The forest with the greatest biodiversity is the tropical rainforest. Tropical rainforests have warm temperatures and ample precipitation that creates a hot, humid climate. This climate is perfect for the cultivation of life and many species that live in tropical rainforests live nowhere else.

Concept note-4: -Which of the following is true about the preservation of land in the United States? The federal government, state governments, and private groups can all set aside land for preservation.
