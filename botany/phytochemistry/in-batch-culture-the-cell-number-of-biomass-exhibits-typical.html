In batch culture the cell number of biomass exhibits typical

(A) ** Sigmoidal curve

(B) Trigonal curve

(C) Linear curve

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cell number of biomass, of a batch culture exhibits a typical sigmoid curve represented by lag phase (cell number or biomass remain unchanged), log phase (rapid increase in cell number) and finally stationary phase (cell member does not change). The lag-phase lasts about 3-4 cell generation.

Concept note-2: -Batch growth kinetics of a microbe follows a growth curve with lag phase as the initial phase during which cells adapt to a new environment. Multiple lag phases occur if the media is supplemented with more than one sugar and such type of growth is referred to as diauxic growth.

Concept note-3: -Explanation: The batch culture or fermentation can be used to produce primary metabolites such as antibiotics which help in retarding the growth of bacteria. Organic acids, amino acids, SCP can be prepared by Continuous fermentation.

Concept note-4: -2.2. 1 Batch cultures. Batch culture is a closed culture system that contains limited amounts of nutrients. In batch culture cells grow in a finite volume of liquid medium and are usually maintained in conical flasks on orbital shakers at a speed of 80–120 rpm.
