Which of the following is NOT true about osmosis?

(A) There is a difference in concentration of a solute across a membrane

(B) water will move through the membrane

(C) ** solute will move through the membrane

(D) the membrane is selectively permeable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Water moves through a semipermeable membrane in osmosis because there is a concentration gradient across the membrane of solute and solvent. The solute cannot effectively move to balance the concentration on both sides of the membrane, so water moves to achieve this balance.

Concept note-2: -Osmosis and Semipermeable Membranes While diffusion transports materials across membranes and within cells, osmosis transports only water across a membrane. The semipermeable membrane limits the diffusion of solutes in the water.

Concept note-3: -The incorrect statement about osmosis is-i. It occurs only in plants. In osmosis, the diffusion of water molecules takes place from a solution of higher concentration to a solution of lower concentration through a semi-permeable membrane.

Concept note-4: -Molecules that cannot easily pass through the bilayer include ions and small hydrophilic molecules, such as glucose, and macromolecules, including proteins and RNA. Examples of molecules that can easily diffuse across the plasma membrane include carbon dioxide and oxygen gas.
