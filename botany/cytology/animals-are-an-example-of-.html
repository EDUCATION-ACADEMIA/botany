Animals are an example of ____

(A) prokaryotes

(B) ** eukaryotes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eukaryote refers to any of the single-celled or multicellular organisms whose cell contains a distinct, membrane-bound nucleus. Organisms such as animals, plants, fungi, and protists are examples of eukaryotes because their cells are organized into compartmentalized structures called organelles, such as the nucleus.

Concept note-2: -Eukaryotes are organisms whose cells contain a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles. There is a wide range of eukaryotic organisms, including all animals, plants, fungi, and protists, as well as most algae.

Concept note-3: -Animals, plants, fungi, and protists are all eukaryotes-eu means true-and are made up of eukaryotic cells.

Concept note-4: -Animals are eukaryotic. Their cell contains a nucleus that is surrounded by the nuclear membrane and there are membrane-bound organelles viz. mitochondria, vacuole, Golgi bodies, lysosomes, and endoplasmic reticulum.

Concept note-5: -Examples of eukaryotic cells are plants, animals, protists, fungi. Their genetic material is organized in chromosomes. Golgi apparatus, Mitochondria, Ribosomes, Nucleus are parts of Eukaryotic Cells.
