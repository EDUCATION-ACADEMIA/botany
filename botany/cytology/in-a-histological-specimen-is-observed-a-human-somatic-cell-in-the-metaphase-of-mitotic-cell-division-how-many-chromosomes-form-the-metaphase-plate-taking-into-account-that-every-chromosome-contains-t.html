In a histological specimen is observed a human somatic cell in the metaphase of mitotic cell division. How many chromosomes form the metaphase plate, taking into account that every chromosome contains two sisterly chromatids?

(A) 48

(B) 92

(C) 23

(D) ** 46

(E) 24

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a histological specimen is observed a human somatic cell in the metaphase of mitotic cell division. How many chromosomes form the metaphase plate, taking into account that every chromosome contains two sisterly chromatids? A. 48.

Concept note-2: -There are 46 individual chromosomes in each cell. Metaphase: During metaphase, each of the 46 chromosomes line up along the center of the cell at the metaphase plate.

Concept note-3: -During metaphase, there are 46 chromosomes composed of two sister chromatids each that align at the metaphase plate. Then, during anaphase, these chromatids are separated and pulled to opposite poles of the cell. This separation results in 92 separate chromatids in the cell, which are considered 92 chromosomes.

Concept note-4: -Solution : Number of chromatids per chromosome is two both in mitotic and meiotic metaphase. It is different in anaphase-two in meiosis I and one in mitosis.

Concept note-5: -Metaphase I: The chromosome pairs line up next to each other along the centre (equator) of the cell. The centrioles are now at opposites poles of the cell with the meiotic spindles extending from them.
