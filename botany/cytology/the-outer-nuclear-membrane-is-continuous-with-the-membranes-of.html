The outer nuclear membrane is continuous with the membranes of:

(A) ** endoplasmic reticulum

(B) lysosomes

(C) peroxisomes

(D) mitochondria

(E) Golgi complex

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nucleus is surrounded by a system of two concentric membranes, called the inner and outer nuclear membranes. The outer nuclear membrane is continuous with the endoplasmic reticulum, so the space between the inner and outer nuclear membranes is directly connected with the lumen of the endoplasmic reticulum.

Concept note-2: -The endoplasmic reticulum is found in most eukaryotic cells and forms an interconnected network of flattened, membrane-enclosed sacs known as cisternae (in the RER), and tubular structures in the SER. The membranes of the ER are continuous with the outer nuclear membrane.

Concept note-3: -Yes, the ER (endoplasmic reticulum) and the nucleus are attached through their outer membranes. Specifically, it is the rough ER that is attached to the nucleus.

Concept note-4: -The NE consists of two continuous, distinct parallel membranes, the inner and outer nuclear membranes, enclosing a perinuclear space (Figure 1). The outer nuclear membrane and perinuclear space are continuous with the endoplasmic reticulum, and share its functions.
