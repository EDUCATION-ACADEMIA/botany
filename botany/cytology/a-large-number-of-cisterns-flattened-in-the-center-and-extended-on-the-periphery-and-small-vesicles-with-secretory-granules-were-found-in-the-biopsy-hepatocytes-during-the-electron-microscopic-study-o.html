A large number of cisterns flattened in the center and extended on the periphery and small vesicles with secretory granules were found in the biopsy hepatocytes during the electron microscopic study of the biliary pole. Name of this organelle is:

(A) Lysosomes

(B) Endoplasmic reticulum

(C) Pinocytotic vesicles

(D) Microtubules

(E) ** Golgi apparatus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The organelles, which consist of cisterns that flattened in the center and extended on the periphery and small vesicles, were found at the electron microphotography of the nervous cells.

Concept note-2: -Dictyosome or Golgi complex is present in higher number in secretory cells. All glandular cells depend on Golgi complex for concentrating and pouring their secretion to the outside.

Concept note-3: -The Golgi apparatus, also called Golgi complex or Golgi body, is a membrane-bound organelle found in eukaryotic cells (cells with clearly defined nuclei) that is made up of a series of flattened stacked pouches called cisternae. It is located in the cytoplasm next to the endoplasmic reticulum and near the cell nucleus.

Concept note-4: -Cells specialising in the production of proteins will tend to have a larger amount of rough ER whilst cells producing lipids (fats) and steroid hormones will have a greater amount of smooth ER.

Concept note-5: -The endoplasmic reticulum (ER) is a network of membrane-enclosed tubules and sacs (cisternae) that extends from the nuclear membrane throughout the cytoplasm (Figure 9.1). The entire endoplasmic reticulum is enclosed by a continuous membrane and is the largest organelle of most eukaryotic cells.
