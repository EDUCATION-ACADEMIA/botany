The cell theory is not applicable to:

(A) Bacteria

(B) Algae

(C) ** Virus

(D) Fungi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Viruses lack the typical structure of a cell and donot perform any functions of a cell hence cell theory is not applicable to viruses.

Concept note-2: -Viruses are not true cells. They lack the most important part of the cell, the protoplasm. Hence, the cell theory is not applicable to viruses.

Concept note-3: -Viruses lack cellular structure (cell wall, cytoplasm, ribosomes etc.) but do have genetic material in the form of RNA and they can only multiply when inside a host cell. Outside a host, they are unable to survive. Since a virus is not a cell, cell principle is not applicable to them.

Concept note-4: -Note: Bacteria, algae and fungi are the unicellular and multicellular organisms that show the complete definite cellular structure with cell organelles, produce energy by burning food and reproduce. So, all are living organisms and come under cell theory.

Concept note-5: -Character of viruses: Cell theory does not explain the nature of a virus as it behaves like a living organism inside a host but does not possess a normal cellular structure.
