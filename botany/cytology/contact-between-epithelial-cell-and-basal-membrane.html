[SOURCES]
Contact between epithelial cell and basal membrane:

(A) unspecialized

(B) tight junction

(C) desmosome

(D) ** hemidesmosome

(E) gap junction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So named because they resemble half desmosomes by electron microscopy, hemidesmosomes are responsible for strong binding between the basal surface of the epithelial cells and the underlying basement membrane and, intracellularly, for providing a link to the intermediate filament cytoskeleton.

Concept note-2: -Hemidesmosomes (HDs) are highly specialized integrin-mediated epithelial attachment structures that make cells firmly adhere to the extracellular matrix by establishing a link between the underlying basement membrane (BM) and the internal mechanical stress-resilient keratin intermediate filament (IF) network.

Concept note-3: -Hemidesmosomes are found in epithelial cells connecting the basal epithelial cells to the lamina lucida, which is part of the basal lamina. Hemidesmosomes are also involved in signaling pathways, such as keratinocyte migration or carcinoma cell intrusion.

Concept note-4: -Stratum basale, also known as stratum germinativum, is the deepest layer, separated from the dermis by the basement membrane (basal lamina) and attached to the basement membrane by hemidesmosomes.

Concept note-5: -Hemidesmosomes are typically found on the basal surface of certain cells, e.g. basal lamina. Similar to desmosomes, particularly spot desmosomes, hemidesmosomes have an attachment plaque on the inside of the cell membrane with keratin filaments inside the cell attaching to the plaque. See also: cell junction.
