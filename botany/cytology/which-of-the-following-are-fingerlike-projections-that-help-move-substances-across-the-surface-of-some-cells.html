Which of the following are fingerlike projections that help move substances across the surface of some cells?

(A) flagella

(B) microtubules

(C) pseudopodia

(D) ** cilia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The plasma membranes of cells that specialize in absorption fold into fingerlike projections that we call microvilli. Such cells typically line the small intestine, the organ that absorbs nutrients from digested food. This is an excellent example of form following function.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: The projections which are present on the surface of some cells are celled Villi. The villi act like finger-like projection on the surface of the cells which are present in some specific types of the cell.

Concept note-3: -Cilia are mobile, tiny, finger-like projections on the surface of airway cells. Cilia line the airways and help move mucus up and out of the lungs [5].

Concept note-4: -To feel their surroundings, the cells use finger-like structures that are actually tube-like protrusions from the cell membrane. These tubes are called filopodia and they can bring messages back to the cell about both the chemical environment and the physical surroundings.

Concept note-5: -The surface of the small intestine has a finger-like microscale villus structure, which provides a large surface area to realize efficient digestion and absorption.
