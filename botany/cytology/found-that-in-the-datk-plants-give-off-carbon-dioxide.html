[SOURCES]
Found that in the datk, plants give off carbon dioxide

(A) Antoine Lavoisier

(B) ** Jan Ingenouz

(C) Jean Baptiste van Helmont

(D) Nicholas of Cusa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ingenhousz further observed that all living parts of the plant “damage” the air (respire), but the extent of air restoration by a green plant far exceeds its damaging effect. In other words, in darkness he found that plants release carbon dioxide gas, the substance responsible for “damaging” the air.

Concept note-2: -Dutch-born British physician and scientist Jan Ingenhousz is best known for the discovery of the process of photosynthesis, by which green plants in sunlight absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen.

Concept note-3: -Jan Ingenhousz was a Dutch physician and plant physiologist, born on Dec. 8, 1730. Ingenhousz is best known for his discovery of photosynthesis, which he announced in his book, Experiments upon Vegetables, discovering their great power of purifying the common air in the sun-shine, 1779.

Concept note-4: -Jan Ingenhousz (1730-1799), in his experiment with an aquatic plant, showed that in bright sunlight, small bubbles were formed around the green parts while in the dark, no such bubbles formed. He found out these bubbles to be of oxygen.

Concept note-5: -Jan Ingenhousz discovered photosynthesis. He showed that green plants in sunlight convert carbon dioxide into oxygen, which they release from the undersides of their leaves. Moreover, he discovered plant respiration, discovering that in heavy shade or darkness, plants consume oxygen, converting it to carbon dioxide.
