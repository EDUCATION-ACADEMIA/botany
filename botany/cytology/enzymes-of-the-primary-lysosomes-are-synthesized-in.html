CYTOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Enzymes of the primary lysosomes are synthesized in:

(A) agranular endoplasmic reticulum

(B) Golgi complex

(C) ** granular endoplasmic reticulum

(D) mitochondria

(E) free ribosomes and polysomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Enzymes of the lysosomes are synthesised in the rough endoplasmic reticulum. The enzymes are imported from the Golgi apparatus in small vesicles, which fuse with larger acidic vesicles.

Concept note-2: -Lysosomal enzymes are synthesized in the endoplasmic reticulum, transported through the Golgi apparatus, and post-translationally modified by glycosylation, and the addition of mannose-6-phosphate tags.

Concept note-3: -Lysosomes are formed by budding off of the Golgi body, and thus the hydrolytic enzymes contained within them are produced within the endoplasmic reticulum.

Concept note-4: -NADPH cytochrome c (cyt c) reductase and glucose-6-phosphatase, two enzymes thought to be restricted to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) and widely used as ER markers, are present in isolated Golgi fractions assayed immediately after their isolation.

Concept note-5: -Lysosomes are formed by budding off of the Golgi body whereas the hydrolytic enzymes present within these structures are formed within the endoplasmic reticulum. They serve as the digestive bag which destroys any foreign material entering the cell such as bacteria and viruses.
