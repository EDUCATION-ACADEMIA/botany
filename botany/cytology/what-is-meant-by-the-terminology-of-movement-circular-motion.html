What is meant by the terminology of movement? Circular motion.

(A) g. abduction

(B) b.adduction

(C) c.flexion

(D) ** .circumduction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -circumduction (uncountable) (anatomy) The circular (or, more precisely, conical) movement of a body part, such as a ball-and-socket joint or the eye. It consists of a combination of flexion, extension, adduction, and abduction. Windmilling the arms and rotating the hand from the wrist are examples of circumduction.

Concept note-2: -Circumduction and Rotation are two different movements. Circumduction, as weâ€™ve previously stated is the movement of a limb in a circle. Rotation is when a limb moves around its long axis, turning like a screw. For instance, you can perform Circumduction in the arm by moving the arm in a circle.

Concept note-3: -Lying on your back with hip in 70 degrees of flexion (bent), have therapist/caregiver move the knee in a clockwise circular pendulum motion for 3-5 minutes. Lying on your back with hip in 70 degrees of flexion (bent), have therapist/caregiver move the knee in a counterclockwise circular pendulum motion for 3-5 minutes.

Concept note-4: -Abduction is the movement of a limb away from the midline or median plane of the body, along the frontal plane. Adduction is the movement of a limb toward the body midline or, in the case of the digits, toward the midline of the hand or foot. Circumduction is moving a limb so that it describes a cone in space.
