CYTOLOGY

[SOURCES]
These cellular organelle help covert hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen

(A) lysosome

(B) centrosome

(C) ** peroxisome

(D) proteasome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Because hydrogen peroxide is harmful to the cell, peroxisomes also contain the enzyme catalase, which decomposes hydrogen peroxide either by converting it to water or by using it to oxidize another organic compound.

Concept note-2: -Therefore, peroxisomes also contain enzymes such as catalase that convert hydrogen peroxide to water and oxygen, thereby neutralizing the toxicity. In that way peroxisomes provide a safe location for the oxidative metabolism of certain molecules.

Concept note-3: -Peroxisomes are specialized for carrying out oxidative reactions using molecular oxygen. They generate hydrogen peroxide, which they use for oxidative purposes-destroying the excess by means of the catalase they contain.

Concept note-5: -Peroxisomes also maintain interactions with the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). The molecular details of these interactions are not fully under-stood, but a growing number of peroxisomal membrane proteins are known to reach the peroxisome through the ER, thus maintaining their functional connection.
