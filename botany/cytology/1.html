True or false:Living things grow when their cells divide.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final Answer: the given statement is true.

Concept note-2: -All living things, or organisms, are made up of at least one cell. Some organisms, such as amoebas, are made up of only one cell and are therefore called unicellular organisms. On the other hand, multicellular organisms, such as humans, are composed of more than one cell.

Concept note-3: -All eukaryotic organisms-including aquatic plants and algae-grow through the process of mitosis. Mitosis is a process where one cell divides into two cells (Fig. 2.46). Chromosomes in the original cell are duplicated to ensure that the two new cells have full copies of the necessary genetic information.

Concept note-4: -4) Living things can grow, use energy and breathe. 5) Non-living things have senses. 6) Living organisms can reproduce. 7) Non-living things cannot move on their own.

Concept note-5: -All living things grow and reproduce. Multicellular organisms grow by increasing in cell size and number. Single-celled organisms increase in cell size. All organisms can normally reproduce, or produce offspring.
