After the onion has been stained, how long do you wait to observe the specimen?

(A) 0 minutes

(B) 1 minute

(C) ** 3-5 minutes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Onion skin cells have many rectangular cells that are easy to see under a light microscope. Many of the organelles cannot be seem with a light microscope because they are generally clear or too small. However, you can observe the cell wall, nucleus, and cytoplasm. The cell membrane is right inside the cell wall.

Concept note-2: -Peel Your Onion– You want one layer of it and it should be super thin. Put One Drop or Two of Iodine– onto the top of the onion cell. If you are using Methylene blue, you’ll need to apply the stain next to the cover slip after it is down. Go light because too much will mean you can’t see the cell well.

Concept note-3: -A wet mount of an onion membrane is used to represent plant cells and is stained with an iodine stain that reacts with any starch present to produce a blue/black colour.
