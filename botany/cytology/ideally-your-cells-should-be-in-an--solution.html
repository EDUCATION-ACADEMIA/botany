Ideally, your cells should be in a(n) ____ solution

(A) Hypertonic

(B) Hypotonic

(C) ** Isotonic

(D) Cola

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A cell placed into a hypertonic solution will shrivel and die by a process known as plasmolysis. An isotonic solution is any external solution that has the same solute concentration and water concentration compared to body fluids. In an isotonic solution, no net movement of water will take place.

Concept note-2: -The optimal condition for an animal cell is to be in an isotonic solution, with an equal amount of water and solutes both inside and outside. When a plant cell is in an isotonic solution, its cells are no longer turgid and full of water, and the leaves of the plant will droop.

Concept note-3: -Animal cells need to be in isotonic solutions to be at homeostasis. Homeostasis is a balance and is important for all living things to maintain. Animal cells lack a cell wall, so too much water coming into the cell will cause it to burst and too much water leaving will cause it to shrink and die.

Concept note-4: -An isotonic solution is best because the osmotic pressure within and outside the cell is equal, so there is no net movement of water and the cells will retain their shape and function. Equally, when exercising, water should be enough to keep you hydrated unless you are working at high intensity.

Concept note-5: -Animal cells, like red blood cells, function optimally in isotonic solutions.
