Presynthetic in interphase is characterized by ____

(A) synthesis of RNA, ATP, tubulins

(B) reduplication of DNA

(C) ** synthesis of the enzymes that are necessary for the formation of RNA precursors

(D) temporary or definitive loss of the ability for division

(E) division of the initial cell into two

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Interphase is composed of G1 phase (cell growth), followed by S phase (DNA synthesis), followed by G2 phase (cell growth).

Concept note-2: -During the cell cycle, RNA and protein synthesis takes place during the G1 and G2 phases. The cell cycle is an ordered set of events, culminating in cell growth and division into two daughter cells.

Concept note-3: -G1 phase. The G1 phase, gap 1 phase, or growth 1 phase, is the first of four phases of the cell cycle that takes place in eukaryotic cell division. In this part of interphase, the cell synthesizes mRNA and proteins in preparation for subsequent steps leading to mitosis.

Concept note-4: -Conclusions: Cells synthesize RNA during the interphase, but at a variable rate with a peak in S. The synthesis proceeds in a majority of the cells at prophase, but only in a few of them at prometaphase and metaphase, and in none at anaphase and telophase.
