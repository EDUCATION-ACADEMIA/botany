Cell membranes are selectively permeable to the following substances

(A) air

(B) O2

(C) ** amino acids

(D) Ill

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Permeability of phospholipid bilayers. Small uncharged molecules can diffuse freely through a phospholipid bilayer. However, the bilayer is impermeable to larger polar molecules (such as glucose and amino acids) and to ions.

Concept note-2: -The cell membrane is semipermeable to small, uncharged molecules like oxygen and carbon dioxide. It is selectively permeable to molecules the cell needs, such as glucose, amino acids, and more.

Concept note-3: -The cell membrane is made up of proteins and amino acids are building blocks of proteins. The amino acids of the proteins are hydrophobic and situated in the core of the peripheral proteins to form a mosaic pattern in the cell membrane.

Concept note-4: -Amino acids like phenylalanine (phe), isoleucine (ile), leucine (leu) and alanine (ala) are commonly found in transmembrane domains. This poses a problem for ion channels or transporters which need to allow charged molecules to traverse the membrane.
