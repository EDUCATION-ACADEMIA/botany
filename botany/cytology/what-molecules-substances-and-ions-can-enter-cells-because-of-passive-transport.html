What molecules (substances) and ions can enter cells because of passive transport:

(A) ** water

(B) glucose

(C) amino acid

(D) sodium

(E) calcium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Only the smallest molecules like water, carbon dioxide, and oxygen can freely diffuse across cell membranes.

Concept note-2: -4.1 Passive Diffusion For example, small nonpolar gases such as O2, CO2, and N2, and small polar molecules such as ethanol cross lipid membranes rapidly.

Concept note-3: -Osmosis is a form of passive transport when water molecules move from low solute concentration(high water concentration) to high solute or low water concentration across a membrane that is not permeable to the solute. There is a form of passive transport called facilitated diffusion.

Concept note-4: -Osmosis is a passive transport process during which water moves from areas where solutes are less concentrated to areas where they are more concentrated.
