Microfibrils participate in ____

(A) breakdown of the phagocyted particles

(B) lipid synthesis

(C) protein synthesis

(D) transport of material out of the cell

(E) ** formation of endocellular skeleton

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the primary walls of growing plant cells, the glucose polymer cellulose is assembled into long microfibrils a few nanometers in diameter. The rigidity and orientation of these microfibrils control cell expansion; therefore, cellulose synthesis is a key factor in the growth and morphogenesis of plants.

Concept note-2: -Cytoskeletal structures are of three types Microtubules, microfilaments and intermediate fibres. The are made up of proteins like tubulin, actin, desmin, myosin etc.

Concept note-3: -Fibrillin, an extracellular matrix glycoprotein, assembles into microfibrils, a component of many connective tissues, where they form the template for elastic fibre formation. Fibrillin is also found in tissues devoid of elastin such as the ciliary zonules of the eye.

Concept note-4: -Cellulose is the primary structural component responsible for much of the mechanical strength of the cell wall. The distribution and orientation of cellulose microfibrils within the cell wall contribute to the control of cell growth.
