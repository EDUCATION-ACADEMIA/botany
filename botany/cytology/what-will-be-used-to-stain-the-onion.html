What will be used to stain the onion?

(A) monosaccharide blue

(B) methylene red

(C) ** methylene blue

(D) monosaccharide red

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Safranine is used to stain onion peel cells. This makes them easily observable under a microscope. It imparts a characteristic pink colour to the cells. Methylene blue is used to impart a blue colour to cheek cells.

Concept note-2: -Peel Your Onion– You want one layer of it and it should be super thin. Put One Drop or Two of Iodine– onto the top of the onion cell. If you are using Methylene blue, you’ll need to apply the stain next to the cover slip after it is down. Go light because too much will mean you can’t see the cell well.

Concept note-3: -Complete answer: Safranin stain is used to stain and observe the organelle mitochondria in an onion peel.

Concept note-4: -It is a cationic dye that stains cells blue because the positively charged dye is attracted to negatively charged particles such as polyphosphates, DNAs, and RNAs. Specimens collected from patients by swabbing are smeared onto microscope slides and the methylene blue solution is dropped on the slide.

Concept note-5: -What does methylene blue stain? Methylene blue, also known as methylthioninium chloride, can be used to stain or highlight parts of animal, bacteria, and blood tissue specimens in microbiology. Use it to closely observe the shapes, structures, and locations of dead cells and other tissues!
