As a result of mitotic division, the two somatic cells were formed. What chromosome number do they contain:

(A) aneuploid

(B) haploid

(C) ** diploid

(D) tetraploid

(E) octoploid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Somatic cells, which are most cells in the body, are diploid, meaning that the cell doubles its chromosome number to 4N during mitosis before dividing and the resulting daughter cells are 2N.

Concept note-2: -When the cell divides, the copies are pulled apart, and each new cell gets one identical copy of each chromosome. This type of cell division is called mitosis, and it produces cells with a total of 46 chromosomes.

Concept note-3: -At the end of mitosis, the two daughter cells will be exact copies of the original cell. Each daughter cell will have 30 chromosomes.

Concept note-4: -The purpose of mitosis is to make more diploid cells. It works by copying each chromosome, and then separating the copies to different sides of the cell. That way, when the cell divides down the middle, each new cell gets its own copy of each chromosome.

Concept note-5: -Diploid. A diploid cell has two complete sets of chromosomes. Most cells in humans are diploid, comprising 23 chromosome pairs, so 46 chromosomes in total.
