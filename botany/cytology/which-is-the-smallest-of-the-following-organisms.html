Which is the smallest of the following organisms?

(A) bacteria

(B) ** virus

(C) erythrocytes

(D) lymphocytes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adenovirus is the smallest virus and Mycoplasma is the smallest bacteria.

Concept note-2: -Among all the microbes, viruses are the smallest. Viruses are living only inside the host body and are non-living outside. Viruses are pathogens and cause diseases in living organisms. Viruses have the smallest size such as 0.02 microns.

Concept note-3: -Bacteria, the Smallest of Living Organisms.

Concept note-4: -There are things out there even smaller than viruses. The two that scientists have discovered are called prions and viroids. A prion is (as far as we know) just a protein. Prions are proteins that can invade cells and somehow direct their own duplication, making more of the isolated proteins.

Concept note-5: -Size. Bacteria are giants when compared to viruses. The smallest bacteria are about 0.4 micron (one millionth of a meter) in diameter while viruses range in size from 0.02 to 0.25 micron. This makes most viruses submicroscopic, unable to be seen in an ordinary light microscope.
