PLANT PHYSIOLOGY

[SOURCES]
These contain vascular bundles of phloem & xylem sheathed in exceptionally strong, fibrous tissues embedded in a matrix of parenchyma cells and are interspersed within the stem.

(A) Dicots

(B) ** Monocots

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, the correct answer is ‘conjoint’

Concept note-2: -Vascular Bundles (Veins) Like the stem, the leaf contains vascular bundles composed of xylem and phloem (Figure 12.2. 6−7).

Concept note-3: -Monocot stems have vascular bundles, composed of xylem and phloem, that are scattered throughout the ground tissue.
