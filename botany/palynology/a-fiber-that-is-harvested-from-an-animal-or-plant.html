A fiber that is harvested from an animal or plant

(A) ** Natural fiber

(B) Synthetic Fiber

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Natural fibres are made from plants and animals, respectively. Natural fibres include things like cotton, wool, flax, jute, and coir.

Concept note-2: -Animal fibers include keratin-based fibers such as sheep wool and fibers of similar use such as alpaca, angora, cashmere, camel, and mohair.

Concept note-3: -Each year, farmers harvest million tonnes of natural fibres from a wide range of plants and animals – from sheep, rabbits, goats, camels and alpacas, from cotton bolls, abaca and sisal leaves and coconut husks, and from the stalks of jute, hemp, flax and ramie plants.

Concept note-4: -Animal fibers generally comprise proteins such as collagen, keratin and fibroin; examples include silk, sinew, wool, catgut, angora, mohair and alpaca. Animal hair (wool or hairs): Fiber or wool taken from animals or hairy mammals. e.g. sheep’s wool, goat hair (cashmere, mohair), alpaca hair, horse hair, etc.
