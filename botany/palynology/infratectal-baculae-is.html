PALYNOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Infratectal baculae is

(A) ** baculae present below the tectum in subtectate pollen

(B) baculae present below the tectum in pertectate pollen

(C) baculae may be present within the tectum in subtectate pollen

(D) baculae present within the tectum in pertectate pollen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tectum is also used to refer to the roof-like protrusions on the outer surface of a pollen or a spore. When a pollen grain has tecta on its outermost layer, it is described as tectate. When there are no tecta, the pollen is described as intectate.

Concept note-2: -The germ pores are apertures in the exine layer of the pollen grain where the sporopollenin is absent.

Concept note-3: -Pollen sacs are located in the anther. It is the structure where pollen are produced.

Concept note-4: -Stamen: The pollen producing part of a flower, usually with a slender filament supporting the anther. Anther: The part of the stamen where pollen is produced. Pistil: The ovule producing part of a flower.
