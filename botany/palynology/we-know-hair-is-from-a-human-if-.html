We know hair is from a human if ____

(A) The medullary index is 0.5 or greater

(B) ** The medullary index is 0.33 or less

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Usually, human hairs have a medullary width that is less than one-third of the diameter of the hair (approximately 0.3), while animal hairs have larger medulla and medullary indices greater than 0.5, indicating that the medulla is at least half as wide as the total diameter of the hair shaft.

Concept note-2: -Humans have a medullary index of less than 1/3 while the medullary index of animals is greater than 1/3. However, the medulla is often fragmented or interrupted, which may result in differences in the identification of hairs from the same individual (1, 2, 4, 8, 9).

Concept note-3: -If the medullary index is . 5 or greater, the hair came from an animal. If the medullary index is . 33 or less, the hair is from a human.

Concept note-4: -Human hairs are generally consistent in color and pigmentation throughout the length of the hair shaft, whereas animal hairs may exhibit radical color changes in a short distance, called banding. The distribution and density of pigment in animal hairs can also be identifiable features.
