The smallest unit of a textile is called a ____

(A) ** Fiber

(B) Hair

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fiber is the smallest component of a fabric; fibers are typically spun into yarn, and yarns are used to manufacture fabrics. Fiber has a hair-like appearance and a higher length-to-width ratio. The sources of fibers may be natural, synthetic, or both.

Concept note-2: -What is a Fiber? A fiber is the smallest unit of a textile material that has a length many times greater than its diameter. What is a natural fiber? A natural fiber is come from plants and animals are used in the production of fabric.

Concept note-3: -A textile fiber is a unit of matter, either natural or manufactured, that forms the basic element of fabrics and other textile structures.

Concept note-4: -Textiles are made of fibers. A fiber is generally hundreds of times longer than it is wide, giving it a hair-like appearance. Textile fibers can be created from many natural sources, as well as man-made materials (for example, polyester and rayon).
