Synchronous or simultaneous maturation of anthers and stigma of the same flower is called

(A) Cliestogamy

(B) ** homogamy

(C) heterogamy

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Homogamy is when the anthers and the stigma of a flower are being matured at the same time. The action of self-pollination guides the plant to homozygosity, causing a specific gene to be received from each of the parents leading to the possession of two exact formats of that gene.

Concept note-2: -Homogamy: It is the condition in which flowers are bisexual anther and stigma mature at the same time to make self-pollination possible.

Concept note-3: -Anther and stigma of bisexual flowers of some plants mature at the same time. They are brought close to each other to ensure self-pollination. This is called homogamy.

Concept note-4: -Maturation of stigma before anthers of the same flower is called as protogyny.

Concept note-5: -One condition is when anthers and stigma mature at the same time. This condition is called homogamy (example: tomato). Another condition is where pollen grains are transferred to the stigma of the same flower as the flower remains closed.
