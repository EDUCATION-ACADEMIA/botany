Find out the correct choice of events of ‘lag phase’?

(A) start synthesising the necessary proteins and start dividing

(B) cellular metabolism is accelerated and start dividing

(C) adapt to the environment

(D) ** cellular metabolism is accelerated and the cells are increasing in size

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ribosomes and translation during lag phase. During lag phase, the number of ribosomes per cell and the translation rate must increase from the low stationary-phase concentration at inoculation to higher (exponential-phase) concentrations at the end of lag phase.

Concept note-2: -bacterial growth curve This lag phase is the period when the bacteria are adjusting to the environment. Following the lag phase is the log phase, in which population grows in a logarithmic fashion. As the population grows, the bacteria consume available nutrients and produce waste products.

Concept note-3: -There are four distinct phases of the growth curve: lag, exponential (log), stationary, and death. The initial phase is the lag phase where bacteria are metabolically active but not dividing.

Concept note-4: -Lag phase is the initial state in growth. The rate of growth is very slow in this phase. During this phase, bacteria adapt themselves to growth conditions. It is the period, where the individual bacteria are maturing and not yet able to divide.
