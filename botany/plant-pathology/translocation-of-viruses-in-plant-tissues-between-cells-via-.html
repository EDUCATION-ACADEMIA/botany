Translocation of viruses in plant tissues between cells via ____

(A) Stomata

(B) Lignin

(C) Guard cells

(D) ** Plasmoligands

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To move from one cell to the next, viruses exploit the channels that plant cells use to communicate with each other. These channels are called plasmodesmata. They are lined with proteins and can be tightly controlled by the plant. Relative to the diameter of plasmodesmata, virus particles are huge.

Concept note-2: -The movement of sucrose and other substances like amino acids around a plant is called translocation . In general, this happens between where these substances are made (the sources) and where they are used or stored (the sinks): from sources in the root to sinks in the leaves in early spring time.

Concept note-3: -The majority of plant viruses are transmitted from plant to plant by insect vectors, especially those that feed on living green plant parts. Fewer plant viruses are transmitted by other methods, such as seeds, vegetative propagation, saps, fungi, nematodes, and phanerogamic plant parasites.

Concept note-4: -Plant viruses encode movement proteins (MPs) that mediate the intra-and intercellular spread of the viral genome via plasmodesmata, membranous channels that traverse the walls of plant cells and enable intercellular transport and communication.
