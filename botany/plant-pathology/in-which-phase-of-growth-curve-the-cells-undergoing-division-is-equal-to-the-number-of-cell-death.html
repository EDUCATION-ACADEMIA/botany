In which phase of growth curve, the cells undergoing division is equal to the number of cell death?

(A) lag phase

(B) log phase

(C) ** stationary phase

(D) decline phase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -…the growth rate enters a stationary phase in which the number of viable bacteria cells remains the same. During the stationary phase, the rate of bacterial cell growth is equal to the rate of bacterial cell death.

Concept note-2: -In the stationary phase, growth reaches a plateau as the number of dying cells equals the number of dividing cells. The death phase is characterized by an exponential decrease in the number of living cells.

Concept note-3: -In the stationary phase, the rate of growth of the cells becomes equal to its rate of death.

Concept note-4: -The final phase of the growth curve is the death phase, which is characterized by a net loss of culturable cells. Even in the death phase there may be individual cells that are metabolizing and dividing, but more viable cells are lost than are gained so there is a net loss of viable cells.

Concept note-5: -The Death Phase As a culture medium accumulates toxic waste and nutrients are exhausted, cells die in greater and greater numbers. Soon, the number of dying cells exceeds the number of dividing cells, leading to an exponential decrease in the number of cells (Figure 7.5).
