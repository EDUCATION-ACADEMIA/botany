The remnants of nucellus present in the seed constitute

(A) ** perisperm

(B) Endocarp

(C) endosperm

(D) Aril

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nucellus is the mass of parenchymatous cells enclosed within the integuments of an ovule. It is the storehouse of abundant food reserves. The residual, persistent nucellus in the seed is called perisperm.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: Perisperm is the nutritive tissue surrounding the embryo in certain seeds and developing from the nucellus of the ovule. After double fertilization, the remnants of the nucellus of the ovule in the mature seed is called perisperm.

Concept note-3: -A layer of nutritive tissue in the seed of certain flowering plants that are derived from the nucellus and surrounds the embryo. In seeds of some plants remnants of a nucleus is persistent. This residual, persistent nucellus is called perisperm.

Concept note-4: -The remains of nucellus left after the fertilization and absorption by the endosperm and embryo is known as perisperm Seeds which contain perisperm are called perispermic seeds. examples are Piper nigrum and Beta vulgaris.
