Outer seed coat is

(A) Funiculus

(B) Tegmen

(C) Hilum

(D) ** Testa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, the correct answer is ‘Testa’.

Concept note-2: -The outer covering of the seed is known as the seed coat. It develops from the integument of the ovule. The outer layer is called testa and the inner layer is called tegmen.

Concept note-3: -seed development form the seed coat (testa). The product is a structure in which the embryo is protected from temperature extremes by its state of desiccation and is often guarded from further drying and from mechanical or biological degradation by the seed coats.

Concept note-4: -Testa and tegmen are two layers of the seed that function to protect it. Testa is the outer coat, and tegmen is the inner coat.

Concept note-5: -The outer coat of the seed, whose function is to protect the seed and its contents, is known as a testa. Tegmen: Tegmen is the second protective seed coat present within the testa. Just like the testa, it also protects the seed and its contents.
