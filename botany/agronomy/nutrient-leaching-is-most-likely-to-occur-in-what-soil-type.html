Nutrient leaching is most likely to occur in what soil type?

(A) silty clay soil

(B) clay soil

(C) compacted soil

(D) ** sandy soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Soils that have high infiltration rates and a low ability to retain dissolved nutrients, such as sandy soils or well-aggregated soils with low organic matter contents, are much more likely to leach nutrients than soils with higher clay and organic matter contents.

Concept note-2: -Nitrogen and sandy soils Nitrates leach more rapidly from sandy soils than from finer-textured soils because sandy soils have a lower water-holding capacity. This means that the same amount of water from rainfall or irrigation will leach ions deeper in sandy soils than fine-textured soils.

Concept note-3: -Sandy or shallow soils have higher leaching potential than clay, loam or deep soils.

Concept note-4: -The nutrient leaching problem is most severe in areas with sandy soils and heavy rains. Sandy soils usually have a low retention of nutrients and 20–80% of applied nutrients or chemicals leach or runoff to ground and surface waters (Campbell et al., 1985; Manevski et al., 2015; Sims et al., 1998).

Concept note-5: -The correct option is C Laterite soil.
