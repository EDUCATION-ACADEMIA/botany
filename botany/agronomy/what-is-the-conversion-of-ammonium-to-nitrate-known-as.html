What is the conversion of ammonium to nitrate known as?

(A) ammonification

(B) denitrification

(C) mineralization

(D) ** nitrification

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nitrification is the biological conversion of ammonia or ammonium to nitrite followed by the oxidation of the nitrite to nitrate. The process of conversion of atmospheric nitrogen to forms that are usable for the plants is termed as nitrogen fixation .

Concept note-2: -Nitrification: The conversion of ammonia into nitrate is called nitrification. It is carried out by some autotrophic bacteria such as Nitrosomonas, and Nitrococcus.

Concept note-3: -Nitrification. Nitrification is the process that converts ammonia to nitrite and then to nitrate and is another important step in the global nitrogen cycle. Most nitrification occurs aerobically and is carried out exclusively by prokaryotes.

Concept note-4: -The process of conversion of ammonia or reduced nitrogen compounds into the easily absorbable form of nitrogen that is nitrates and nitrites. It is an aerobic process. Chemoautotrophic bacteria play a major role in this process.

Concept note-5: -Nitrification is the process of converting ammonia to nitrates and nitrates to nitrites by oxidation reactions.
