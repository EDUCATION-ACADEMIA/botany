PLANT SYSTEMATICS

[SOURCES]
The location of the shoot and root system is as follows-the shoots are ____ the ground and the roots are ____ the ground

(A) below/above

(B) below/below

(C) above/above

(D) ** above/below

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The shoot system consists stems, leaves, and the reproductive parts of the plant (flowers and fruits). The shoot system generally grows above ground, where it absorbs the light needed for photosynthesis. The root system, which supports the plants and absorbs water and minerals, is usually underground.

Concept note-2: -The shoot system generally refers to the part of the plant growing above ground, such as the leaves and stems. The shoot system is actively involved in photosynthesis. By contrast, the root system is found below ground, where it draws up water and minerals from the soil for distribution throughout the plant.

Concept note-3: -Belowground plant organs are not only those related to roots, but include leaf-and stem-derived organs, such as bulbs and rhizomes.

Concept note-4: -The root system is composed of roots, tubers, and rhizoids of the plant. The shoot system is composed of leaves, buds, flowers, and fruits of the plant. This part of the plant grows below the ground. This part of the plant grows above the ground.
