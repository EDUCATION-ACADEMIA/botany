What do we call the table used to calculate the probability of an offspring’s genotype?

(A) ** Punnett Square

(B) Venn Diagram

(C) Avogadro’s Graph

(D) Circle of Life

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Punnett square is a tabular summary of possible combinations of maternal alleles with paternal alleles. These tables can be used to examine the genotypical outcome probabilities of the offspring of a single trait (allele), or when crossing multiple traits from the parents.

Concept note-2: -A Punnett square is a chart that allows you to determine the expected percentages of different genotypes in the offspring of two parents. A Punnett square allows the prediction of the percentages of phenotypes in the offspring of a cross from known genotypes.

Concept note-3: -Each parent has a 1/2 chance of making an a gamete. Thus, the chance of an aa offspring is: (probability of mother contributing a) x (probability of father contributing a) = (1/2) ⋅ (1/2) = 1/4.

Concept note-4: -Punnett Squares are a diagram which biologists use to determine the probability of an offspring having a particular trait. Creating a Punnett Square and using it to determine traits of offspring is called performing a cross.
