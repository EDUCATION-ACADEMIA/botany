What is a gene?

(A) An entire chromosome that codes for a trait

(B) a piece of clothing

(C) ** a piece of a DNA on chromosome that codes for a trait

(D) Chemical messengers in your brain that code for a trait

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The basic unit of heredity passed from parent to child. Genes are made up of sequences of DNA and are arranged, one after another, at specific locations on chromosomes in the nucleus of cells.

Concept note-2: -Gene. A segment of a DNA molecule (a sequence of bases) that codes for a particular protein and determines the traits (phenotype) of the individual. A gene is the basic unit of heredity in a living organism.

Concept note-3: -A gene is a section of a chromosome that controls a particular trait.

Concept note-4: -Genes carry the information that determines your traits (say: trates), which are features or characteristics that are passed on to you-or inherited-from your parents. Each cell in the human body contains about 25, 000 to 35, 000 genes.

Concept note-5: -Genes are segments of deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) that contain the code for a specific protein that functions in one or more types of cells in the body.
