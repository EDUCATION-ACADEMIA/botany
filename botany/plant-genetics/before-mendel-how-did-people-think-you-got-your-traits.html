Before Mendel how did people think you got your traits

(A) ** equal from each parent

(B) more from the dad

(C) more from the mom

(D) from a stork

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Before Mendel’s experiments, most people believed that traits in offspring resulted from a blending of the traits of each parent.

Concept note-2: -This is because each parent gives the child some of themselves gene. A child is made from the information found in the cells of the parents. These characteristics are called genes. The traits that you can observe in a person are called that person’s phenotype.

Concept note-3: -Gregor Mendel, through his work on pea plants, discovered the fundamental laws of inheritance. He deduced that genes come in pairs and are inherited as distinct units, one from each parent. Mendel tracked the segregation of parental genes and their appearance in the offspring as dominant or recessive traits.

Concept note-4: -While women do inherit 50% of their DNA from each parent, men inherit about 51% from their mother and only 49% from their father.

Concept note-5: -His discoveries concerning genetic inheritance were generally accepted by the scientific community when he published them during the mid 19th century. He believed that genetic traits of parents will usually blend in their children. His ideas about genetics apply equally to plants and animals.
