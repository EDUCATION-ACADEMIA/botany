What substance in flowers attracts bees and butterflies?

(A) Embryo

(B) Periosteum

(C) Carbon dioxide

(D) ** Nectar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nectar is a fluid substance secreted by flowers in plants. It is rich in sugar. It helps in attracting pollinating agents like bees and butterflies.

Concept note-2: -Plants rely on bees and other insects to reproduce and so they have adapted, over time, to become more attractive to them. Bees are drawn to plants with open or flat tubular flowers with lots of pollen and nectar. A flower’s scent can have particular appeal to bees, and its bright colours may lure the bees in.

Concept note-3: -Nectar is produced in glands known as nectaries. The glands are commonly found at the base of flowers, where they produce nectar as a reward for pollinators. However, there are also extrafloral nectaries located elsewhere on the plant, often on the leaves or petiole – the stalk that attaches the leaf blade to the stem.

Concept note-4: -nectar, sweet viscous secretion from the nectaries, or glands, in plant blossoms, stems, and leaves. Nectar is mainly a watery solution of the sugars fructose, glucose, and sucrose but also contains traces of proteins, salts, acids, and essential oils.

Concept note-5: -Bees like flowers because they feed on their nectar and pollen. The nectar is used by bees as food and an energy source to get to and from their home. The pollen they also pick up from flowers are used to feed larva (baby bees) in the hive.
