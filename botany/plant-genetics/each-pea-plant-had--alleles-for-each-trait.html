PLANT GENETICS

[SOURCES]
Each pea plant had ____ alleles for each trait.

(A) 1

(B) ** 2

(C) 3

(D) 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An F1 cross-bred pea plant is a heterozygote – it has 2 different alleles. Mendel followed the inheritance of 7 pea traits. Dominant traits, like round peas, appeared in the first-generation hybrids (F1), whereas recessive traits, like wrinkled peas, were masked.

Concept note-2: -From this, he deduced that pea plants must have two alleles for each gene so that they could carry a trait without actually having it present in their phenotype.

Concept note-3: -Alleles can be recessive or dominant. Every trait has two alleles in each person. Humans are diploid because they have two alleles at each genetic locus.

Concept note-4: -In the case of Mendel’s peas, there are two different versions, or alleles, for seed color: yellow (Y) and green (y). Every plant will have two copies of each gene. We call this combination of alleles the plant’s genotype. The seed color we can observe is called the phenotype.

Concept note-5: -An individual inherits two alleles, one from each parent, for any given genomic location where such variation exists. If the two alleles are the same, the individual is homozygous for that allele. If the alleles are different, the individual is heterozygous.
