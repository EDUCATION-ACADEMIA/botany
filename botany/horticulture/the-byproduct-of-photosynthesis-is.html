The byproduct of photosynthesis is:

(A) Carbon Dioxide

(B) Nitrogen

(C) ** Oxygen

(D) Water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Photosynthesis is the process in which green plants use sunlight, along with water and carbon dioxide to make their own food. Oxygen and glucose are the by-products of photosynthesis.

Concept note-2: -In photosynthesis, solar energy is harvested and converted to chemical energy in the form of glucose using water and carbon dioxide. Oxygen is released as a byproduct.

Concept note-3: -Once they have water and carbon dioxide, they can use energy from sunlight to make their food. The leftovers from making the plant food is another gas called oxygen. This oxygen is released from the leaves into the air.

Concept note-4: -Plants use sunlight to convert carbon dioxide and water into chemical energy that takes the form of organic compounds that they use for growth and reproduction. The process also produces other gases, called “byproducts, ” which include oxygen, and consumes water and carbon dioxide.
