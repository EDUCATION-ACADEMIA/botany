What is a turgid?

(A) a type of soil to use on plants

(B) a type of flower

(C) a plants that doesn’t have enough moisture

(D) ** a plant that is swollen or filled with moisture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant cells become “turgid” when they are put in dilute solutions. Turgid means swollen and hard. The pressure inside the cell rises, eventually the internal pressure of the cell is so high that no more water can enter the cell. This liquid or hydrostatic pressure works against osmosis.

Concept note-2: -Endosmosis is when the cell is kept in hypotonic solution, water flows inside causing it to swell up.

Concept note-3: -Turgidity is a cellular phenomenon of cell swelling because of the absorption of high fluid content. The cells take up water and it gets stored in vacuoles resulting in swelling of cells. Turgidity occurs because of the presence of the cell wall and the osmoregulatory effect of the vacuole.

Concept note-4: -A plant cell that is placed in a hypotonic solution would cause the water to move into the cell by osmosis. The resulting influx of water leads to a large turgor pressure being exerted against the plant cell wall. This makes the cell turgid.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: The tonicity of the turgid plant cells is hypertonic, because the plant cells have a higher concentration of solutes than distilled water. Turgid cells have more solutes in their cytoplasm and on their membrane, which means they are hypertonic compared to distilled water.
