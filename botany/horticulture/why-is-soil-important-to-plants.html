Why is soil important to plants?

(A) Plants extract water from the soil

(B) Plants extract nutrients from the soil

(C) Plants anchor themselves in the soil

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Soil provides plants a foothold for their roots and holds the necessary nutrients for plants to grow. Soil filters the rainwater and regulates the discharge of excess rainwater, preventing flooding.

Concept note-2: -Soil provides nutrients, water and minerals to plants and trees, stores carbon and is home to billions of insects, small animals, bacteria and many other micro-organisms.

Concept note-3: -It supports plant growth by holding the roots firmly and supplying water and nutrients to the plants. It acts as a natural habitat for many organisms like an earthworm, fungi, bacteria, ants, etc. It is also essential for agriculture which provides us with food, clothing and shelter for all.

Concept note-4: -Soil is an important element essential for the survival of living organisms. The importance of soil is mentioned below: The fertile soil helps in the growth and development of the plants. The plants thus produced are healthy and provide food, clothing, furniture, and medicines.

Concept note-5: -The Importance of Soil Soil provides a place for plants to grow. It holds water in place for their roots. It contains nutrients, or food substances, needed for their growth. Soil also provides a home for many animals and other living things.
