Jenny needs to construct some beds to use for rooting azalea cuttings. How deep should the rooting media be?

(A) 1 inch

(B) ** 4 inches

(C) 12 inches

(D) 24 inches

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The major types of asexual propagation are cuttings, layering, division, separation, grafting, budding, and micropropagation. Advantages of asexual propagation include: It may be easier and faster than sexual propagation for some species.

Concept note-2: -Asexual propagation can be done in a variety of ways. These ways include: Cuttings – Cutting a portion of a leaf, stem, or root off the parent plant then replanting and therefore the plant begins to grow. This is the most common and widely used form of vegetative propagation.

Concept note-3: -Using tissue culture, sometimes called micropropagation, tiny fragments of plants are treated with plant hormones in a sterile growing medium. The hormones stimulate the growth of a callus, from which a new seedling can grow. This method is used to produce large numbers of identical seedlings.

Concept note-4: -Asexual propagation involves taking a part of one parent plant and causing it to regenerate itself into a new plant. The resulting new plant is genetically identical its parent. Asexual propagation involves the vegetative parts of a plant: stems, roots, or leaves.
