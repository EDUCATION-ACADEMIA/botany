looses leaves during dormant season

(A) ** deciduous

(B) turgid

(C) evergreen

(D) wilted

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Perennial plants which shed their leaves during stressful conditions such as low temperature, drought, etc., are called deciduous plants. Generally, during autumn season, in order to overcome the upcoming winter season, plants shed their leaves and enter into an inactive state like hibernation.

Concept note-2: -In temperate forests in the Northern Hemisphere, trees shed their leaves during autumn as a cold season. In tropical and subtropical forests, trees shed their leaves at the beginning of the dry season. Many types of trees shed their leaves as a strategy to avoid harsh weather conditions.

Concept note-3: -The deciduous forest is characterized by trees that lose their leaves at the end of each growing season. Concept note-4: -Tropical deciduous trees shed their leaves in the dry season.

Concept note-5: -The leaves of the deciduous trees have a tendency to lose water. But during the dry season, water is scarce. Hence, deciduous trees shed their leaves to prevent water loss.
