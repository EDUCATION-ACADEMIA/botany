Which warm-season grass grows from stolons and has medium-textured light green leaves?

(A) ** Centipede

(B) Kentucky bluegrass

(C) Perennial ryegrass

(D) Zoysia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eremochloa ophiuroides, or centipedegrass, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae. Used as a warm season lawn grass, it forms thick sods and spreads by stolons. It is medium to light green in color and has a coarse texture with short upright seedhead stems that grow to about 3-5 inches.

Concept note-2: -The best grass to mix with centipede, to avoid disease issues, is Bermuda grass (Cynodon dactylon). Purchase quality grass seed for the best results. Check to make sure the germination rate is greater than 85 percent.

Concept note-3: -Bermudagrass is extremely heat tolerant-daytime temperatures of 95 to 100 degrees Fahrenheit are optimal. Pennington Bermudagrass yields dense, resilient, heat-tolerant lawns. Zoysia grass, Centipede grass and Bahiagrass also tolerate high heat very well.

Concept note-4: -The nutrient requirements of centipedegrass are different from most other turfgrasses. An acid soil pH of 5.0 to 6.0 is preferred by this turfgrass species. As the pH goes above 6.0, Iron (Fe) availability decreases. Iron deficiency causes the grass to become chlorotic or yellow.

Concept note-5: -As soils and nighttime temperatures warm, chlorotic conditions usually improve. Centipedegrass color may also improve with supplemental applications of iron. When undergoing environmental stress or when leaves are bruised from traffic, centipedegrass leaf blades often turn a dark red to purple color.
