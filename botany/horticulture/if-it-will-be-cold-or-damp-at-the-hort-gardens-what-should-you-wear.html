If it will be cold or damp at the hort gardens what should you wear?

(A) Sister please, it doesn’t matter! Your teacher can’t take you outside when it is cold.

(B) ** Dress in layers so you can be cool indoors and warm outdoors.

(C) Just wear whatever you want. If you get too cold you will be excused from working that day.

(D) Bring your blanket. Everyone is more comfortable with a blanket right?

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Wool is one of the best fabrics fabric for retaining heat even in humid conditions. Synthetic materials used in fleece jackets are pretty good at repelling moisture as well. Clothing like scarves, neck gaiters, gloves, and beanies make a huge difference! Keep your extremities warm.

Concept note-2: -Middle Layer-A middle insulating layer, like a sweater, fleece, or thermal shirt can add an extra layer of warmth. This layer should be snug, but not tight fitting. Base Layer-Polyester or any wicking material is the best choice for your base layer. Avoid cotton for this layer, as any moisture will sit on your skin.

Concept note-3: -Natural fibers, like wool, goose down, or fleece work best.
