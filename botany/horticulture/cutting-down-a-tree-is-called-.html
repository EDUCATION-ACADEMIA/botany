Cutting down a tree is called ____

(A) ** Felling

(B) Knelling

(C) Dropping

(D) Chopping

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Felling involves cutting the standing tree and dropping it in the place you want it. Limbing is the removal of the branches from either standing or downed trees. Bucking is the process of cutting the downed tree into appropriate lengths.

Concept note-2: -Essentially, tree felling is the action of cutting down a tree to prevent the spread of disease and improve safety in the area.

Concept note-3: -’Tree felling’ is another term for removing a tree. Specifically, it is used to describe the purposeful removal of a tree by cutting it in a way that forces it to fall down – hence ‘felling.

Concept note-4: -Which is grammatically correct: “Falling of trees “ or “ Felling of trees"? A “falling of trees” is when they are knocked down by a rainstorm, “a felling of trees” is when someone goes after then with an ax or other instrument to “fell” them. Fell is an active verb meaning to cut don.
