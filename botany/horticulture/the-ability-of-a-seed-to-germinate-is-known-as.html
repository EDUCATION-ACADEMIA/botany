The ability of a seed to germinate is known as:

(A) Inert

(B) ** Viable

(C) Grading

(D) Debris

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A viable seed is one which is capable of germination under suitable conditions. The definition includes dormant but viable seeds, in which case the dormancy must be broken before viability can be measured by germination.

Concept note-2: -Germination is the ability of a seed to produce an embryonic root and stem within a specified period of time.

Concept note-3: -Seed or plant viability is the measure of how many seeds or how much plant material in a lot are alive and could develop into plants that will reproduce under appropriate field conditions. It is very important that seeds or plant material stored in the genebank are capable of producing plants when sown in the field.

Concept note-4: -(1) The embryo must be alive, called seed viability. (2) Any dormancy requirements that prevent germination must be overcome.

Concept note-5: -One method to check for seed viability is the water test. Take the seeds and place them in a container of water. Let the seeds sit for 15 minutes. If the seeds sink, they are still viable; if they float, discard, because they probably will not sprout.
