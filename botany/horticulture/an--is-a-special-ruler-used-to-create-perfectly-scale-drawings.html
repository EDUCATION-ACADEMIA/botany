A(n) ____ is a special ruler used to create perfectly scale drawings.

(A) anthropologist’s scale

(B) architect’s ruler

(C) ** architect’s scale

(D) special ruler

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Architect scales, such as 1/4˝ = 1´0˝ (1/48 size) or 1/8˝ = 1´0˝ (1/96 size), are used for structures and buildings. They are used to measure interior and exterior dimensions such as rooms, walls, doors, windows and fire protection system details. Other scale tools include flat scales and rolling scales.

Concept note-2: -Architectural drawings are done in scales that are smaller than the real size. Floor plans are commonly produced at 1/ 50th (1 to 50) or 1/100th (1 to 100) of their actual size. Site plans are often drawn at 1/200th (1 to 200) or even 1/500th (1 to 500) of actual size.

Concept note-3: -Reduction scale It is the most used scale of representation in architecture, and some of the most used are 1:5, 1:10, 1:20, 1:50, 1:100, 1:200, 1:500, 1:1000, 1:2000 and 1:50000. In this way, when we measure our plan or model, we must apply the scale to discover its real extension.
