The green color of leaves is caused by the:

(A) ** Chloroplasts

(B) Epidermis

(C) Guard cells

(D) Margin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chloroplasts are green in colour due to presence of a green colouring pigment called chlorophyll.

Concept note-2: -As shown in detail in the absorption spectra, chlorophyll absorbs light in the red (long wavelength) and the blue (short wavelength) regions of the visible light spectrum. Green light is not absorbed but reflected, making the plant appear green. Chlorophyll is found in the chloroplasts of plants.

Concept note-3: -So, plants and their leaves look green because the “special pair” of chlorophyll molecules uses the red end of the visible light spectrum to power reactions inside each cell. The unused green light is reflected from the leaf and we see that light.

Concept note-4: -Leaves of plant contain green pigment called chlorophyll. Green coloured plastids are called chloroplasts.
