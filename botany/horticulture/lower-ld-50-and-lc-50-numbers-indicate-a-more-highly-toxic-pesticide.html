Lower LD 50 and LC 50 numbers indicate a more highly toxic pesticide.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A pesticide with a lower LD50 is more toxic than a pesticide with a higher number because it takes less of the pesticide to kill half of the test animals. The LC50 value is a measure of the toxicity of a pesticide when test animals breathe air mixed with pesticide dust, vapours or spray mist.

Concept note-2: -A low LD50 means the species is highly susceptible to the poison. The LD50 for a particular poison can vary greatly between different species of animals. Since LD50 is quoted in mg/kg of body weight, the amount of poison to kill an animal is a direct function of the speciesâ€™ body weight.

Concept note-3: -LD50 is the abbreviation used for the dose which kills 50% of the test population. LC50 is the abbreviation used for the exposure concentration of a toxic substance lethal to half of the test animals. LD50 is expressed in milligrams per kilogram of body weight of the test animal (which must be mentioned).

Concept note-4: -LC50 is defined as the concentration in air at which 50 percent of the test animals (generally rats or mice) died, usually within a specified time after being exposed by inhalation. A substance is considered extremely toxic if it has an LD50 of less than 5 mgs/kg of animal body weight.

Concept note-5: -In general, the smaller the LD50 value, the more toxic the chemical is.
