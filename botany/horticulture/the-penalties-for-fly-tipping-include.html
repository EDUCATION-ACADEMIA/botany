The penalties for fly tipping include

(A) ** A prison sentence of up to 5 years

(B) ** An on-the-spot fine

(C) ** A fine of up to £50, 000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fly-tipping is a serious criminal offence which carries a fine of up to £50, 000 (unlimited if the case goes to the Crown Court) or an offender can even be sent to prison.

Concept note-2: -Fly-tipping is a crime, which carries serious penalties. In the Crown Court the maximum penalty is 5 years jail and/or an unlimited fine. In the magistrates’ court, persons convicted of fly-tipping can be fined an unlimited amount, jailed for 12 months, or sent to The Crown Court for more severe penalties.

Concept note-3: -Print this page. Fly-tipping is illegal dumping of liquid or solid waste on land or in water. The waste is usually dumped to avoid disposal costs. You need to follow different rules for litter, which is usually less than a black bag’s worth, for example food or tobacco-related litter.

Concept note-4: -Penalties for litter and flytipping If the case goes to court, a person who is caught littering will have to pay a fine of up to £2, 500. Someone convicted of flytipping could be fined up to £40, 000 and/or imprisoned for up to 12 months.
