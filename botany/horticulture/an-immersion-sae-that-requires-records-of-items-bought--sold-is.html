An immersion SAE that requires records of items bought & sold is:

(A) research

(B) ** ownership/entrepreneurship

(C) service based learning

(D) placement/internship

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Immersion SAEs are an extension of the Agricultural Literacy component of the Foundational SAE and will contribute to a studentâ€™s growth in all of the Foundational components in an authentic, contextualized manner. The five Immersion SAE categories build upon the Foundational SAE components in a real-world application.

Concept note-2: -Students with an ownership/entrepreneurship type SAE own the enterprise, equipment and supplies, make the management decisions and assume the financial risks to produce a product or provide a service. All products or services must be agriculturally related.

Concept note-3: -In entrepreneur SAEs, the student owns animals, crops, equipment and other required inputs and keeps financial records to determine return to investments. Examples of entrepreneur SAEs include livestock projects, a lawn care business and growing bedding plants in the school greenhouse.

Concept note-4: -There are three major kinds of research SAE programs: experimental, analytical and invention.
