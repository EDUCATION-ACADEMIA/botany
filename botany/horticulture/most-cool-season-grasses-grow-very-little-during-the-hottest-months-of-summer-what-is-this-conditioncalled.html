MOST cool-season grasses grow very little during the hottest months of summer. What is this conditioncalled?

(A) dead

(B) ** dormant

(C) turgid

(D) wilted

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deciduous. MOST cool-season grasses grow very little during the hottest months of summer.

Concept note-2: -The term dormancy means the plants have a period of rest. It is a natural part of most plants’ life cycle. When a flower is in a dormant state, it consumes less water and nutrients. Therefore, you shouldn’t fertilize dormants.

Concept note-3: -Annual plants germinate, bloom, set seed and die all in one year. Biennial plants have a life cycle of two years, so they germinate and grow one year, bloom and die the following. Everything which lasts longer than two years is perennial, which in practical terms usually means it grows and flowers for many years.

Concept note-4: -As part of regular biological processes, a deciduous tree: drops its functional leaves in the dormant season. Plants that complete their life cycles in two years are: Biennials.

Concept note-5: -Biennial plants complete their life cycle in two years.
