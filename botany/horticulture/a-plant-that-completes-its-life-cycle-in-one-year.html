a plant that completes its life cycle in one year

(A) ** annual

(B) deciduous

(C) biennial

(D) horticulture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Annuals take a single year to complete a full life cycle. Most are planted in the spring, bloom in the summer, and die in the fall. A few, known as winter annuals, germinate in the later summer and autumn, are dormant during the winter, and grow during the next spring and summer.

Concept note-2: -Annuals are plants that complete their life cycle in one year.

Concept note-3: -Examples of true annuals include corn, wheat, rice, lettuce, peas, watermelon, beans, zinnia and marigold.

Concept note-4: -biennial, any herbaceous flowering plant that completes its life cycle in two growing seasons. During the first growing season, biennials produce roots, stems, and leaves. During the second growing season, they produce flowers, fruits, and seeds, and then they die.

Concept note-5: -Perennials are plants that survive for a number of years. Examples of perennials are mango, coconut, apple and bougainvillea.
