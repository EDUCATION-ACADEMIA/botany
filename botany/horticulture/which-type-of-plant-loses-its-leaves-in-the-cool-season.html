Which type of plant loses its leaves in the cool season?

(A) ** deciduous

(B) evergreen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deciduous plants are the plants which shed their leaves in winter season. The maple tree is an example of a deciduous plant.

Concept note-2: -There are several reasons why deciduous trees lose their leaves: It means the tree spends less energy through the harsh winter. It conserves moisture within the trunk and keeps it from drying out. It allows wind to blow through the branches, putting less strain on the tree – a serious concern in winter storms and gales.

Concept note-3: -Common examples of deciduous trees include oak, maple, and hickory trees. Oak trees are characteristic deciduous trees that lose their leaves in the fall and re-grow them in the spring.

Concept note-4: -The leaves of the deciduous trees have a tendency to lose water. But during the dry season, water is scarce. Hence, deciduous trees shed their leaves to prevent water loss.

Concept note-5: -Deciduous trees lose their leaves toward the finish of their developing season. This happens in the fall in mild deciduous backwoods, and in the dry season in tropical and subtropical deciduous woods. Deciduous plants totally lose their leaves for a while every year, supplanting them when ideal conditions return.
