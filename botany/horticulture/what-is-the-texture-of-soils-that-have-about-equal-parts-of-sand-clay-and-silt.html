What is the texture of soils that have about equal parts of sand, clay, and silt?

(A) clayey

(B) ** loamy

(C) sandy

(D) silty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A clay loam texture soil, for example, has nearly equal parts of sand, slit, and clay. These textural separates result from the weathering process.

Concept note-2: -"Loam means you have roughly equal portions of sand, silt and clay, ” he said.

Concept note-3: -Clays stick to your boots, loams are easily moulded but non-sticky, sands are not cohesive at all and cannot be moulded when moist. The relative size of particles is important. For example, the finest sand particles are 10 times the diameter of the largest clay particles.

Concept note-4: -LOAM: Will form a weak (<1”) ribbon; intermediate (some) grittiness. SILT LOAM: Will form a weak (<1”) ribbon; very smooth (little grit). SANDY CLAY LOAM: Will form a moderate (1-2”) ribbon; very gritty. CLAY LOAM: Will form a moderate (1-2”) ribbon; intermediate (some) grittiness.

Concept note-5: -Loam soil contains almost equal amounts of sand, silt, clay humus, air and water.
