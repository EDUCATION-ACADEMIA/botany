The study and cultivation of grape vines.

(A) Olericulture

(B) ** Viticulture

(C) Floriculture

(D) Horticulture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cultivation of grapes is called Viticulture. The cultivation and harvesting of grapes are known as viticulture (from the Latin word for vine) or winegrowing (wine-growing). It is a branch of horticulture science.

Concept note-2: -Viticulture is the study and practice of cultivating grapevines, usually with the overall goal of producing fruit that is suitable for some specific end purpose.

Concept note-3: -The scientific term “viticulture” refers to the science, study and production of grapes. The term “viniculture” also refers to the science, study and production of grapes.

Concept note-4: -Grape is usually propagated by hard wood cuttings, though propagation by seed, soft wood cuttings, layering, grafting and budding is also used in some cases. The grapevines are usually planted in pits. The size of the pit depends upon the spacing of the vines and also on the specific requirements of the variety.

Concept note-5: -Viticulture is cultivation of grapes. v. Horticulture is growing vegetables, flowers and fruits for commercial use. Question 6.
