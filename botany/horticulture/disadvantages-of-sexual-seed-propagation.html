Disadvantages of sexual (Seed) propagation

(A) they are all long and large

(B) ** Most seeds are very slow and germinate

(C) True

(D) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A disadvantage is that sexual reproduction takes longer than asexual reproduction. A mate must be found, the egg must be fertilised by sperm, and then the offspring develop. The benefit of introducing genetic variation into the species, however, outweighs this disadvantage.

Concept note-2: -The major disadvantages of asexual reproduction are: Lack of diversity-Since the offsprings are genetically identical to the parent they are more susceptible to the same diseases and nutrient deficiencies as the parent. All the negative mutations persist for generations.
