Who funds the Cooperative Extension Service in each state?

(A) The federal government

(B) State government

(C) Local community organizations/charities

(D) ** The land grant university in each state

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Beyond the original land grants, each land-grant college receives annual federal appropriations for research and extension work on the condition that those funds are matched by state funds.

Concept note-2: -Many people felt that these colleges would not receive adequate support without the financial support of the federal government. Because of this public sentiment, Vermont Representative Justin Morrill introduced the first land-grant bill to congress in 1857.

Concept note-3: -Twenty-three historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs) are members, of which 21 are land-grant institutions (19 under the 1890 Morrill Act, two under the 1862 Morrill Act).

Concept note-4: -The 1862 Institutions are the first land-grant institutions; 1890 Institutions are historically Black colleges and universities (HBCUs); and 1994 Institutions are tribal colleges and universities (TCUs).

Concept note-5: -First proposed when Morrill was serving in the House of Representatives, the Morrill Land Grant College Act of 1862 set aside federal lands to create colleges to “benefit the agricultural and mechanical arts.” The president signed the bill into law on July 2, 1862.
