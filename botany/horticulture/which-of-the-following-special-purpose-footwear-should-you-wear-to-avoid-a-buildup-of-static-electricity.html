Which of the following special purpose footwear should you wear to avoid a buildup of static electricity?

(A) Metatarsal guards

(B) ** Safety toe shoes

(C) Electrically conductive shoes

(D) Foundry shoes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Electrically conductive boots: Electrically conductive boots protect against the buildup of static electricity.

Concept note-2: -Static Dissipating Safety Shoes and Soft-Toe shoes are designed to dissipate (reduce) the amount of static electricity build-up on your body. Unlike Electrical Hazard Safety Shoes, Static Dissipating Shoes can be either safety toe or non-safety (or soft-toe).

Concept note-3: -Wear steel toe boots with rubber soles. These boots will protect your feet and toes from shocks that can occur during a power outage. They also prevent chemicals from getting into your shoes, which can cause irritation and discomfort while working.

Concept note-4: -Electrical Hazard (EH) – Non-Conductive EH rated shoes are electrical insulators and prevent or reduce the flow of electrical current from the feet to the ground. EH rated shoes can also prevent electric shock from contact with a live conductor.
