Mass flowers are used in floral designs to add mass and to:

(A) create the accent or focal point

(B) ** draw attention to the focal point

(C) fill empty spaces and hide frame

(D) outline shape and size.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mass flowers consist of a single stem with one solid, rounded head at the top of the stem. They function to add mass or visual weight to an arrangement.

Concept note-2: -Mass Flowers will give the bouquet weight – or mass – and are generally round and full faced. Sometimes they are referred to as “face” flowers. They are usually the focal point of color and interest in a bouquet. Most mass flowers come with only one flower on the end of the stem.

Concept note-3: -The focal point of a design is the area that attracts and holds the interest of the viewer. This spot or area dominates. the design. The focal point, also called the” center of interest, ” is located near the place where the stems or main lines appear to meet.

Concept note-4: -FRAMING: A design technique in which branches of flowers are used around the perimeter of a floral arrangement to direct attention to the materials in the center of the arrangement.
