Jamie has a tray of marigold seedlings she started from seed. She transplants the seedlings from the seedling flat to cell packs when the:

(A) first mature roots appear

(B) ** first true leaves are on the plant

(C) plants are two weeks old

(D) seedlings are 6 inches tall

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Jamie has a tray of marigold seedings she started from seed. She transplants the seedings from the seedling flat to cell packs when the: First true leaves are on the plant. Chris is interested in producing the most new plants in the shortest time period.

Concept note-2: -Leaf Section Cut off one of the leaves at its base, then cut it into 2-4 inch segments. Dip the basal end (the end of the segment that was closest to the base of the plant) of each segment in rooting hormone and then insert 1-2 inches into the rooting mix.

Concept note-3: -Asexual propagation, sometimes referred to as vegetative propagation, involves taking vegetative parts of a plant (stems, roots, and/or leaves) and causing them to regenerate into a new plant or, in some cases, several plants. With few exceptions, the resulting plant is genetically identical to the parent plant.

Concept note-4: -80 out of 100 seeds to germinate. When a plant first begins growing from a seed, the stage of growth is: juvenile.

Concept note-5: -Asexual propagation involves taking a part of one parent plant and causing it to regenerate itself into a new plant. Genetically it is identical to its one parent. Asexual propagation involves the vegetative parts of a plant: stems, roots, or leaves.
