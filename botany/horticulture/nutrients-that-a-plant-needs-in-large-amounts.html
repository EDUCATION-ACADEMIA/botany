Nutrients that a plant needs in large amounts

(A) Micro Nutrients

(B) Fertilizer

(C) Soil

(D) ** Macro Nutrients

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The primary macronutrients are Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K). Nitrogen is essential for plant development, since it plays a fundamental role in energy metabolism and protein synthesis.

Concept note-2: -Nutrients that plants require in larger amounts are called macronutrients. About half of the essential elements are considered macronutrients: carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium and sulfur.

Concept note-3: -Plants require a steady supply of macronutrients and micronutrients. The difference between the two is quite simple: Macronutrients are required in larger quantities than micronutrients.

Concept note-4: -The first three, nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, are primary macronutrients. These nutrients are needed in the largest amounts and are critical for your plantâ€™s health. Without these three nutrients, plants will die quickly.
