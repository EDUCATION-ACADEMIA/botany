On a financial statement, items that can be quickly converted to cash or that will be sold within 12 months are:

(A) ** current assets

(B) current liabilities

(C) equity

(D) net worth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A current asset-sometimes called a liquid asset-is a short-term asset that a company expects to use up, convert into cash, or sell within one fiscal year or operating cycle. Non-current assets, on the other hand, are long-term assets that cannot be readily converted into cash within one year.

Concept note-2: -Assets that can be swiftly turned into cash are called cash equivalents. The risk associated with cash equivalents is minimal and can be easily converted. Savings accounts, T-bills, and money market products are some examples. Current liabilities are debts with a one-year maturity.

Concept note-3: -Liquid assets means cash or cash equivalents which can be easily converted to cash in a very short time. Inventories and prepaid expenses are not considered as liquid asset else every current asset is liquid asset.

Concept note-4: -What Are Current Assets? Current Assets are those business assets that will be converted into cash within one year, and assets that will be used up in the operation of a business within one year. Thatâ€™s the quick definition, for those of you who want the basics.

Concept note-5: -Current assets are short-term assets that can be used up or converted to cash within one year or one operating cycle. Non-current assets are long-term assets that a company expects to use for more than one year or operating cycle.
