What should be used to determine if amendments are needed in native soil?

(A) Aeration

(B) Fertilizing

(C) Treatment

(D) ** Soil test

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To figure out how much of an amendment you need by weight, multiply the square footage by the application rate on the package. For example, if the application rate is 2 pounds per 100 square feet, and the bed is 350 square feet: (2 pounds / 100 square feet) x 350 square feet = 7.0 pounds.

Concept note-2: -Soil Acidity Test (pH) Proper pH in the field is essential for plant productivity, and either too high or too low pH will adversely affect crop growth. Testing pH of soil, one calculates its hydrogen ions. pH values may range from 0 to 14.

Concept note-3: -The most common is the Proctor Test, or Modified Proctor Test. A particular soil needs to have an ideal (or optimum) amount of moisture to achieve maximum density. This is important not only for durability, but will save money because less compaction effort is needed to achieve the desired results.
