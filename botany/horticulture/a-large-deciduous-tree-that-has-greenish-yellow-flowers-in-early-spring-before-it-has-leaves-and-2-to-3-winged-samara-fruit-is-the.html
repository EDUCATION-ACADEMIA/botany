A large deciduous tree that has greenish-yellow flowers in early spring before it has leaves and 2” to 3” winged samara fruit is the:

(A) Japanese yew.

(B) ** Norway maple.

(C) Norway spruce.

(D) Savannah holly.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Acer (/ˈeɪsər/) is a genus of trees and shrubs commonly known as maples.

Concept note-2: -In the fall, leaves usually turn a pale yellow. One of the easiest ways to differentiate Norway maple from sugar maple is to cut the petiole (or leaf stalk) or vein and if a milky substances oozes out, it is a Norway maple. Also, bud tips of Norway maples are more blunt, whereas sugar maples are pointy and sharp.

Concept note-3: -Leaves: Norway maple has palmately compound opposite leaves with five lobes. The leaves are 4-7 inches (10-18 cm.) in length, and they possess shallow sinuses with a few long teeth. The leaves are dark green above, and lighter beneath.

Concept note-4: -More commonly referred to as “helicopters, ” “whirlers, ” “twisters” or “whirligigs, ” samaras are the winged seeds produced by maple trees.
