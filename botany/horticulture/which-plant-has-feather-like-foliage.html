Which plant has feather-like foliage

(A) Fortune tea olive

(B) ** Plume asparagus fern

(C) Southern red oak

(D) Sycamore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Asparagus ferns are not ferns at all, they belong to the asparagus family. The common names are derived from their appearance, the plumes of feathery foliage resemble delicate, lacy ferns. They need bright indirect light, without full sun and high humidity.

Concept note-2: -Both foxtail ferns and asparagus ferns have extremely similar hardiness zones. While this is likely because they are the same species of plant, some asparagus ferns can survive in more zones compared to the foxtail fern.

Concept note-3: -Asparagus densiflorus (foxtail fern, emerald fern or plume asparagus) has foliage that look more like a brush or a fox’s tail.

Concept note-4: -’Sprengeri’ Asparagus Fern is a rounded herbaceous perennial that is used in the landscape for its attractive, fine-textured foliage. This 1 to 4 foot-tall plant has true leaves that are scale-like and inconspicuous. The structures that most refer to as leaves are actually leaf-like branchlets called cladophylls.

Concept note-5: -Versatile asparagus fern is an attractive herbaceous perennial that is easy to grow, though not actually a fern. The soft texture of this plant’s small needle-like leaves resembles the foliage of asparagus plants.
