Which foliage plant has yellow and orange in its leaves?

(A) Amaryllis

(B) ** croton

(C) peony

(D) snapdragn

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -croton, (Codiaeum variegatum), also called variegated laurel, colourful-leaved plant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) native to Malaysia and the Pacific and extensively cultivated.

Concept note-2: -The croton is an easy-to-grow houseplant known for its variegated foliage covered in green, scarlet, orange, and yellow splotches. Hereâ€™s how to care for a croton in your home or garden.

Concept note-3: -Gold Dust Croton Also known as Sun-Spot Croton, it features bright green oval-shaped leaves sprinkled with golden yellow spots. You can also grow it as a houseplant, but it needs full exposure to bright indirect sunlight. Growing Tip: Plant this beautiful variety in bright light for vibrant colored leaves.

Concept note-4: -Oakleaf croton features interesting lobed leaves, giving the plant extra texture (and lending it its name). Each dark green leave is accented with veins and strips of yellow, orange, and red. Perhaps the most common croton available, Petra has shield shaped leaves veined in red, orange, and yellow.
