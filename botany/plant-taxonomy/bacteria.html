Bacteria

(A) ** Prokaryotes

(B) Eukaryote

(C) both

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bacteria are classified as prokaryotes, along with another group of single-celled organisms, the archaea. Prokaryotes are tiny, but in a very real sense, they dominate the Earth. They live nearly everywhere – on every surface, on land and in water, and even inside of our bodies.

Concept note-2: -Prokaryotic cells are believed to be the oldest cells on Earth. Thus, all bacteria are prokaryotic, but not all prokaryotes are bacteria. The other major group of prokaryotes is the blue green algae (also known as cyanobacteria).

Concept note-3: -Bacteria lack a membrane-bound nucleus and other internal structures and are therefore ranked among the unicellular life-forms called prokaryotes.

Concept note-4: -The bacteria are the oldest and simplest living organisms, and all of the bacteria are “prokaryotes, ” meaning that they do not have a true membrane-bound nucleus as eukaryotes do. [Prokaryote is derived from Greek, meaning “before nucleus"; eukaryote means “true nucleus."]

Concept note-5: -Prokaryotes include the domains, Eubacteria and Archaea. Thus, examples of prokaryotes include bacteria, archaea, and cyanobacteria (blue-green algae).
