Which is an example of an aerial plant?

(A) ** waling-waling orchid

(B) banana

(C) mango

(D) water lily

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vanda sanderiana is a species of orchid. It is commonly called Waling-waling in the Philippines and is also called Sander’s Vanda, after Henry Frederick Conrad Sander, a noted orchidologist. The orchid is considered to be the “Queen of Philippine flowers” and is worshiped as a diwata by the indigenous Bagobo people.

Concept note-2: -Vanda sanderiana (Waling-Waling) is a large sized epiphytic orchid species boasting stout, erect or ascending inflorescences giving rise to 4-10 large, long-lasting, fragrant flowers. Blooming mostly in the fall, the blossoms, 3.5-4.5 in.

Concept note-3: -Waling-ivaling as Second National Flower.-The Waling-waling orchid 2 is hereby declared as another national flower, together with Sampaguita, which was 3 declared the national flower of the Philippines in Proclamation No. 652, dated 01 4 February 1943 by Governor-General Frank Murphy.

Concept note-4: -Dubbed as the “Queen of Philippine Orchids, ” the Waling-waling is flower endemic to the island in Mindanao, where the indigenous Bagobo tribes worship it as deity.

Concept note-5: -When you plant a Waling-waling orchid in a pot, place the top of the root ball above the level of the growing medium and do not cover it with your medium. This allows the roots to breathe and start growing. Orchids require good drainage to prevent root rot.
