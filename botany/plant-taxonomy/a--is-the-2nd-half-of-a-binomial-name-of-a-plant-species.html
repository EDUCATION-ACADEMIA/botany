[SOURCES]
A ____ is the 2nd half of a binomial name of a plant species.

(A) ** SPECIFIC EPITHET

(B) GENUS

(C) VARIETY

(D) CULTIVAR

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The first part is known as the genus. The second part is the specific epithet. Together, they are known as the species, Latin binomial, or scientific name. These help us understand how plants are grouped together or classified.

Concept note-2: -There are two words, hence this system of naming organisms is called binomial nomenclature. The first word is the name of the genus, and the second word is the species name.

Concept note-3: -The second word of a scientific plant name is called the specific epithet. This word is lowercase and in italics and follows the generic name. Together, these two words form a species name. This is the same binomial nomenclature that Linnaeus used in Species Plantarum in 1753.

Concept note-4: -The first part of the name – the generic name – identifies the genus to which the species belongs, whereas the second part – the specific name or specific epithet – distinguishes the species within the genus.

Concept note-5: -Species and Genus are the two taxa used in binomial nomenclature.
