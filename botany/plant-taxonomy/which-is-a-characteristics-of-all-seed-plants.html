Which is a characteristics of all seed plants?

(A) have nonvascular tissue

(B) ** use seeds to reproduce

(C) grow near water

(D) produce flowers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Another characteristic shared by seed plants is the presence of vascular tissue . Because they produce both male and female spores, all seed plants are heterosporous.

Concept note-2: -All seed plants share two characteristics. They have vascular tissue and use seeds to reproduce. In addition, they all have body plans that include leaves, stems, and roots. Most seed plants live on land.

Concept note-3: -Seed plants have special structures on them where male and female cells join together through a process called fertilisation. After fertilisation, a tiny plant called an embryo is formed inside a seed. The seed protects the embryo and stores food for it. The parent plant disperses or releases the seed.

Concept note-4: -A seed is a mature ovule that comprises an embryo or a miniature undeveloped plant and food reserves, all enclosed within a protective seed coat. Seeds are a way of reproduction for all flowering plants.
