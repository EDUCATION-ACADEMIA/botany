[SOURCES]
DICOT PLANTS HAVE ____ VEINS IN THEIR LEAVES.

(A) PARALLEL

(B) ** NETTED

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vascular structures of dicot leaves form net-like veins. Dicot leaves are not as linear in shape as monocot leaves, and their vascular structures form net-like veins, instead of parallel ones. This reticulate venation pattern generally has one of two appearances.

Concept note-2: -The dicot leaf is characterized by its unique net vein orientation, which is unlike monocots who have a parallel venation or a pine needle which have no veins at all. Dicots also have an extra tissue layer known as the palisade parenchyma.

Concept note-3: -Monocots have parallel venation in their leaves, while dicots have netted venation. Q. Assertion :Leaves of monocot plants generally possess reticulate venation. Reason: Leaves of dicot plants generally possess parallel venation.

Concept note-4: -Monocot leaves tend to have parallel veins; in dicots the veins are netted. Monocot floral parts are in multiples of 3; dicots are based on 4’s or 5’s. The vascular bundles in monocots stems are scattered; in dicots they form a ring surrounding the pith.

Concept note-5: -There are two basic patterns of Venation. Dicots have Net (Reticulate) Venation. This is characterized by a network of veins which branch such that each branch becomes smaller and smaller. The smallest veins circumscribe small regions of the blade called Areoles.
