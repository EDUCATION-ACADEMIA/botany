Which is a characteristic of members of the plant kingdom that distinguishes them from members of the animal kingdom?

(A) Storage of energy in chemical bonds

(B) Exchange of water with the environment

(C) Use of mRNA during protein synthesis

(D) ** Use of chlorophyll for solar energy transformation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Expert-Verified Answer. The characteristic of members of the plant kingdom that distinguishes them from members of the animal kingdom is the use of chlorophyll for solar energy transformation.

Concept note-2: -Three features distinguish plants from animals: Plants have chlorophyll, a green pigment necessary for photosynthesis; Their cell walls are made sturdy by a material called cellulose; and. They are fixed in one place (they donâ€™t move).

Concept note-3: -The plant kingdom has the following characteristic features: They are non-motile. They make their own food and hence are called autotrophs. They reproduce asexually by vegetative propagation or sexually.

Concept note-4: -The main difference between Plantae and Animalia is that Plantae are autotrophic and Animalia are heterotrophic. Plantae are able to create their own food through photosynthesis, while Animalia must consume other organisms in order to get their nutrition.

Concept note-5: -Plant Kingdom organisms are distinguished from other kingdoms by the presence of chlorophyll, which is required for photosynthesis, the fact that they are stationary and cannot move from one location to another, and the presence of cellulose in their cell walls.
