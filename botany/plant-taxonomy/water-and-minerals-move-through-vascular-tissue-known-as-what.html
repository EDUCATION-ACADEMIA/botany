Water and minerals move through vascular tissue known as what?

(A) phloem

(B) cambium

(C) root hairs

(D) ** xylem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem is the plant vascular tissue that conveys water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the rest of the plant.

Concept note-2: -Transpiration is ultimately the main driver of water movement in xylem. The cohesion-tension model works like this: Transpiration (evaporation) occurs because stomata are open to allow gas exchange for photosynthesis.

Concept note-3: -Xylem transports water, minerals, and nutrients from the soil to all the plant parts. There are two types of “transport” tissues in plants-xylem and phloem. Water and solutes are transported by the xylem from the roots to the leaves, and food is transported from the leaves to the rest of the plant by the phloem.

Concept note-4: -The two primary vascular tissues are xylem, which transports water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the leaves, and phloem, which conducts food from the leaves to all parts of the plant.

Concept note-5: -Xylem is responsible for the transport of water and dissolved ions from the roots upwards through the plant.
