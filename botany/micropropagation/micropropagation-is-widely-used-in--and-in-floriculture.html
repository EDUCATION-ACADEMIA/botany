Micropropagation is widely used in ____ and in floriculture.

(A) Wilderness

(B) Deforestation

(C) Sustainability

(D) ** Agriculture

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Micropropagation is used to increase the vigor and yield of floriculture species by multifold. Micropropagation is popular for the production of synthetic seeds that analog to true seeds. These seeds match with the morphology, physiology, and biochemistry of the zygotic embryos.

Concept note-2: -Micropropagation is the process of multiplying plant stock plant material by growing plantlets in tissue culture to produce a large number of progeny plants and then planting them out.

Concept note-3: -Micropropagation is a method of plant propagation using extremely small pieces of plant tissue taken from a carefully chosen and prepared mother plant, and growing these under laboratory conditions to produce new plants. It is widely used in commercial horticulture.

Concept note-4: -Micropropagation is the rapid vegetative propagation of plants under in vitro conditions of high light intensity, controlled temperature and a defined nutrient medium. The technique has been applied to a substantial number of commercial vegetatively propagated plant species.

Concept note-5: -Micropropagation is known as clonal propagation, in controlled condition. It has great advantage on the commercial propagation and multiplication can be achieved from single plant cell in shorter period for cut flower disease free plantlets to meet the market requirements.
